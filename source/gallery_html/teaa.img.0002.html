Description

This photo is part of the pictorial tribute to "the black soldier" in the Great War. This image depicts a Spahi, one of many light cavalrymen in the French army, from the French colonies in North Africa: Morocco, Algeria, and Tunisia. The caption describes these Algerian Spahis as "Negroid" to distinguish them from the Spahis of Arab and Berber descent. The Spahis constituted an important part of the Armeé d'Afrique, which formed during the French invasion and conquest of Algeria (1830-1587). During World War I, the French colonial army's Black troops included the Tirailleurs Sénégalais (Senegalese troops), Spahis, and African men from sub-Saharan French colonies.

– Jeannette Eileen Jones
