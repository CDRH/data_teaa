I received an invitation from one of the Chief Justices of the City to assist, as witness, the sealing up of the Brazilian Consular Archives in few hours after the death of the Acting Brazilian Consul of this City. I found the Judge with two Brazilian Gentlemen perusing and commenting upon the Brazilian Consular Regulations. My attention was called to an article of the Regulations wherein it was written that whenever a Brazilian Consul should Hon William H. Seward Secretary of State Washington D.C. should die, it was requested of the competent Authority to cull together two Brazilian subjects and a Consul of some friendly nation, to witness the sealing up of the Archives and to invite said Consular Officer to take charge of the duties of the Brazilian Consulate until His Imperial Majesty should be pleased to fill the vacancy. I considered it my duty under the circumstances to cheerfully accept the duty thus assigned to me and in my Despatch to His Excellency the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Brazil copy of which I herewith enclose; I say to His Excellency that I am moved with pride as the Representative of the United States in this country to have it in my power to be of service to this friendly Empire of Brazil. I also addressed His Excellency the Governor General of the Province on the subject copy of which I have also the honor to enclose. I am confident the Department will approve of my proceedings in this case founded as I suppose they are, upon the correct principle of International Courtesy. Considering myself, under the circumstances, fully empowered by the "Juiz de Direito", to act on behalf of Brazilian interest, in this Province; I have this day sent a note to His Excellency the Govenor General of the Province, informing him of the fact for his Government: I have the honor to enclose a copy of this note and His Excellency's reply, and also a "Public Instrument" of the "Aide de Deligencia" drawn up by the "Juiz de Direito" soon after Mr Ramizo Luis de Santos expired, signed by several Brazilian witnesses and others, including myself, wherein is also stated the Authority which confers upon me the honor to address Your Excellency. Not having in my possession the Archives of the Brazilian Consulate for reference and information. I may not carry out exactly the wishes of the Brazilian Government, but nevertheless will endeavour, guided by the Consular Regulations of the United States to protect the interests of your nation and arm of its subjects requiring assistance. A copy of this Despatch I have considered proper to send to His Excellency the Brazilian Minister at Lisbon, that he may inform Snr Manoel Sobral Pinto of the circumstances who I understand still holds His Imperial Majesty's Commission as Consul for the Empire of Brazil in this City. With the assurance of my highest respect. I have the Honor to be Your most obedt Servant, Augustus Archer Silva Commercial Agent United States Commercial Agency St Paul de Loanda Sept 14th 1865.
