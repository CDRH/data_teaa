Dept of State Recd Jan 10 '67 No 26 United States Commercial Agency St Paul de Loanda Oct. 15th, 1866. Sir I have the honor to inform the Department that since my Despatch No 25 the board ordered by the Navy department composed of three officers of the U.S. Store Ship "Purveyor" has established at this city a Naval Store house for the use of the U.S. Vessels of War. On their arrival here they applied officially to me for assist- ance Hon William H Seward Secretary of State Washington -ance which was cheerfully offered them, with, I am pleased to say favorable results. Being familiar with the inconvenience which the former United States Store Keeper had to contend with, I lost no time to request His Excellency to allow me the privilege to suggest a plan more simple than the former and equally as secure to the Portuguese Government. His Excellency requested an interview with me for explanation, which resulted in his acquiescence to all my views. I suggested the privilege to allow the United States Naval Store Keeper on the arrival of every Store Ship to deposit at the Custom house a duplicate copy of her cargo, and to permit without Custom house vigilance the Ship to discharge said cargo with the Store house. The duplicate Invoice would answer as a correct voucher for all stores that entered the Store house and as voucher for delivery, each vessel of war should furnish the Store Keeper a duplicate receipt for what they receive, one of which to be deposited at the Custom house. The Custom house authorities to have the privilege, whenever they should deem it necessary to verify the balance of Stores on hand. The immediate willingness of His Excellency to concede to our request request and practically carrying it out, speaks of itself the high esteem that distinguished official entertains toward the American character. Leuit. Commander Devens of the U.S. Store Ship "Purveyor" left this port highly impressed with the kind attention he received from His Excellency, and before his departure, in a letter expressed his thanks to him, in very appropriate words, which was enclosed by me to him in another note joining Capt Devens in his sentiments The advantages gained by the present concession over that concedede to the former store house established here by Flag officer Inman in 1859 can can only be appreciated by those familiar with the vexation the former Store Keeper had to undergo, being as he always was dependant in the slow motion of a native Custom house guard, who held a second key to all the Store houses, and even so the Store house could only be opened in the Custom house hours. His Excellency has always manipulated his friendliness towards the United States by special cordiality to United States Naval Officers when they visit him, and during the visit of the U.S. Ship "Funeata" at this port, he visited that ship which was quite unexpected as I never knew a Governor General to visit any any grade below a Commodore. His Excellency has been promoted to a rear admiral since he assumed charge of this government. I have the honor to be Your Most Obt Servt Augustus Archer Silva Commercial Agent
