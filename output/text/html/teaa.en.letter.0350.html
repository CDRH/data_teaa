Department of State Received Jan 28 1890 No. 123. Consulate of the United States Zanzibar December 17 1889 Mr. Pratt. To the Dept of State Subject: Arrival of Stanley and The Emin Expedition at Zanzibar. Abstract of Contents. No. 123. Consulate of the United States Zanzibar December 14 1889 Honorable William F. Wharton Assistant Secretary of State Washington, D.C. Sir I have the honor to advise the Department of the arrival at Zanzibar on the 6th inst of Mr. Stanley and the Emin Relief Expedition. Emin Pasha unfortunately met with quite a serious accident immediately after the banquet given by the Germans at Bagamayo and it has not been deemed advisable to have him removed to Zanzibar. Mr Stanley is the guest of the British Consul General and the Emin Relief Committee and has been shown every attention a reception rendered him last evening at the British Consulate General was largely attended and was a success. It is expected that Mr. Stanley with his will take his departure from Zanzibar about the 25th inst. by one of the Steamers that is coming here to convey Emin's people to Egypt. It is hoped that Emin Pasha will be able to go at the same time but it is considered doubtful if he can be moved with safety. I have the honor to be Sir Your obedient servant Seth A. Pratt U.S. Consul
