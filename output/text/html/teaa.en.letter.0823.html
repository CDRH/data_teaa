No. 20 Department of State Received Feb 27 1888 United States Consulate, St. Paul de Loanda. December 31st, 1887. Mr Downing To the Department of State. Subject: Vice Consular Officer. Abstract of contents: Requesting consideration of the Vice Consulate &c., &c. United States Consulate St. Paul de Loanda, December 31. 1887. Hon. George L. Rives, Assistant Secretary of State, Washington, D.C. Sir: In referring to Par's. 32, 33, /33 and Par's 529 and 564, I have the honor to request the Department to act upon what may be suggested by comparisons between enclosures Nos. 1 and 2 in my despatch No. 17 of this date and between enclosures in my despatch No 19 of this date. Commercial relations between the province and the United States are worthy of a cultivation not to be had through silence nor the new publication of information. The incumbency of the office of Consul or Vice Consul carries with it certain duties that, to be properly performed, should be in the hands of some one interested interested in American Commerce. I enclose herewith Instrument executed in accordance with "Consular Regulation." for the action of the Department. The gentlemen mentioned therein is an English subject and a man of capacity. He refers to Messrs Mosbacher & Co. 105 Water St, New York, for which house he is Agent here. I have the honor to be, Sir, Your obedient servant, Henry F. Downing. U.S. Consul List of Inclosures. No. 1 Vice Consular Bond of Arthur Edmund Baines with of Signature & Seal.
