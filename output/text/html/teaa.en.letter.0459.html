No. 3 Department of State, Washington, Dec. 5, 1888. Emory H. Taunt, Esqre Commercial Agent of the United States at Boma, Congo Free State, (Now at 1532 J St, N.W, Washington, D.C.) Sir: In addition to the instructions heretofore given you in regard to proceeding to your post, it is proper for me to state that under the act making appropriations for the diplomatic and consular service of the United States for the fiscal year ending June 30, 1889, approved July 18, 1888, the provision for a a commercial agent at Boma is coupled with "authority to visit and report upon the commercial resources of the upper and lower Congo basin, their products, their minerals, their vegetable wealth, the openings for American trade, and to collect such information on the subject of the country as shall be of interest to the United States." The Department is not in a position, even if it were thought advisable, to give you any definite instructions as to the extent of your travels in that region, or as to the special subjects upon which you should report. The general direction in which Congress desires that that inquiries should be made is sufficiently indicated in the quotation from the act given above. You will bear this authority in mind, and use your best judgment and discretion in visiting the various parts of the Congo basin, and in reporting upon the subjects which may be of interest to the people of this country. I am, Sir, Your obedient servant, G. L. Rives Assistant Secretary
