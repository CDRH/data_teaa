No 68. Consulate of the United States Zanzibar, March 21 1889 Honorable G. L. Rives Assistant Secretary of State Washington, D.C. Sir: I have the honor to inform the Department that the last mail steamer from Aden brought one hundred Somalis who have been engaged by the German East African Company to help fight their battles on the coast They were not allowed to land at Zanzibar but were transferred in one of the Sultan's steamers to the main land. It is very doubtful if the Somalis can be made to fight against the Coast Arabs being of the same religion. On the 18th inst I received a Cablegram from Commander McCalla of the U.S.S. Enterprise stating stating that he was at Port Said on his way to Zanzibar and Madagascar, and asking if there is any reason why he should not go first to Tamatave. As everything is quiet here in Zanzibar at the present time I see no need of his immediate presence in harbor and I shall probably cable him to this effect, upon his arrival at Aden on the 26th inst. recommending that he time his arrival at Zanzibar not later than the first of May. I have the honor to be Sir Your obedient servant Seth A. Pratt U.S. Consul
