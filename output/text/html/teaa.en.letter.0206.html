Department of State Received Mar 3, 1883 Ack'd Mch 9 '83 No 36 United States Consulate at St Paul de Loanda S.W.C. Africa L. de R. du Verge to State Department Washington D.C. Subject Destitute Am: Seamen and Petitions of the Am: subjects condemned for Life. Encloses Duplicate copies &a &a forwarded to Don John M. Francis at Lisbon. United States Consulate St. Paul de Loanda Jany. 11th 1883 To Hon: John Davis Asst. Secty of State Washington D.C. Sir I have the honor to state, that I forwarded to Hon John M. Francis Minister Resident at Lisbon; copy of clothing &a issued to destitute Am: seamen forwarded by Portuguese Brig "Trorador" with receipt for their passage &a &a duplicates of which I herewith enclose. I am in hopes that the state Dept: will approve of my actions in this case, to have sent them to Lisbon for $10 a piece instead of Cape de Verde Island for $23 a piece and that only as a great favor from the Capts of the vessel: January, February, March and April; there on hardly any Portuguese vessels which come here, and it would be a great difficulty to send away from this place during these months if I had any on my hands. I have also forwarded to Hon John M. Frances the petitions of the Am: Subject, condemned for life deportation to this place by the Portuguese Authorities; Their petitions are enclosed by the best merchants of Loanda, and I truly hope that their liberty may be to them, being exhausted, with one foot in the grave, having been here since 1869. I have the honor to be Sir Your obedient servant L. de R. du Verge US Consul
