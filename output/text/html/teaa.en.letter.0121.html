United States Consulate at St Paul de Loanda 23rd April 1879 Sir, I have the honor to inform the Department that I leave this day for England via Lisbon per steamer "Benguella" in conformity with licence granted me for six months in your Despatch No. 71 of 22nd November 1878. As Mr. Thomas the newly appointed Consul has not yet arrived, I have delivered over the Archives to the care of Mr Andrew Galbraith Colville who will act during my absence. I have made up the accounts of this Consulate to todays date according to consular instructions, and have to advise my Draft on the Secretary Secretary of the Treasury for $56.19 in favor of Mr Frederick A. Silva of Boston. Enclosed is form No. 120. I have the honor to be Sir, Your most obedient Servant Robt S Newton Vice Consul To the Hon. F. W. Seward Assistant Secretary of State Washington, D.C.
