No 26. United States Consulate at St Paul de Loanda 18th September 1878. Sir, I have the honor to acknowledge receipt of despatch No. 11 addressed to Consul Jackson also despatch dated 5th July informing me that Mr William H. Thomas has been appointed Consul for this Port. On arrival of Mr Thomas I shall deliver over to him all the Archives, records and other property in my possession in accordance with instructions. As it is necessary that I should proceed to England about the end of this year, I have to request the Department to grant me leave of absence for six months, and in the event of Consul Thomas not having arrived at this Port I I beg to suggest to the Department to allow me to leave the Consulate in charge of Mr. Andrew Galbraith Colville during my absence. This gentleman is of the highest respectability and is a Merchant of several years standing in this place. I have the honor to be Sir, Your obedient servant Robt S Newton Acting Consul To the Assistant Secretary of State Washington D.C.
