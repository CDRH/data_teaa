No 15. Department of State Received Mar 6 1893 Commercial Agency of the United States, Kongo Free State, Stanley Pool, Dec. 23, 1892 Mr. R. Dorsey Mohun To the Department of State. Subject: Return to Pool from Kassai River. Abstract of Contents. Visit to Lake Leopold II &e the Loukenye River. Commercial Agency of the United States, Stanley Pool, Dec. 23rd 1892 Honorable William F. Wharton Assistant Secretary of State Washington, D.C. Sir: I have the honor to inform you that I have just arrived here from the waters of the Loukenye River, and shall leave next week for the Sankourou & Kassai Rivers. The country any great distance back from the river banks it is hardly possible to see on account of the hostility of the natives, and now that the Mauyema people (commonly called "Arabs") have declared a general war against all whites traveling without a large escort is most dangerous. To illustrate: November 23d I was some 375 miles up the Loukenye slipping was a village called Maiyeaye. I desired to see the natives making cloth & asked permission of the Chief to visit the town, which was readily granted. I only took six men with me armed with revolvers under their shirts. When I had gotten about six minutes distance from the town, I was most foully attacked from the bush with spears & poisoned arrows. Fortunately none of my men were struck, and before they could throw their arrows again we had opened fire, & drove the natives ahead. We captured the village and I burned it to the ground to teach them a lesson. The next day the Chief sent me some goats & desired to make blood brotherhood, & declare peace, which I was more than gland to grant This is only one of the five or or six times I have been compelled to fight during the past few months. If my report, when sent to the Department does not seem to comprehend the whole of the Kongo State, it will be because I have not enough of a force to penetrate to those parts. The whole Eastern boundary of the state is now shut by the Arabs, and all the white men who were in the different ports have been killed. Their bodies were afterwords eaten by the large number of cannibal slaves attached to the caravans. So far my health has been good. I am sir, Very respectfully Your obedient Servant R. Dorsey Mohun Commercial Agent
