Department of State Received Sep 17, 1883 No. 50 Consulate of the United States at St. Paul de Loanda S.W.C. Africa Ls de R. du Verge to State Department Washington D.C. Subject Visiting my jurisdiction & consular Agencies Enclose Report of Benguella, Catembella, Equimina Bay, Abriz and Banana Congo. United States Consulate St Paul de Loanda July 9th 1883. To Hon John Davis Asst. Secty. of State Washington D.C. Sir I have the honor to state that upon the receipt of Despatch No. 12 from the State Dept, I went South by order of my medical adviser, and visited Benguella, Catumbella & Equimina Bay. "Catumbella is the center of commerce of the interior of Benguella, and the great rendezvous of all the caravans from Balindu and Bihé, I appointed Mr Passens the chief agent of the Dutch African Trading Co. there, to be the agent for the Am: Mission of Balindu & Bihé Equimina Bay is the great rendezvous of Am: Whalers and Mr Bastas Pina a Brazilian gentleman is the owner of the whole bay and has very fine plantation of coffee, cotton, sugar &a he furnishes also vegetables meat water wood &a to the whalers having no customs duties established there, the whalers have made the bay their Head quarters. On my return south my health was not improved at all, and the Doctors ordered me to leave at once for the U.S. and gave me a certificate to that effect which I forward in another despatch. I left Loanda for my Northern Jurisdiction about a month ago, Ambriz is worst than Loanda, but from Kinsembo to Banana Congo, the coast which is still under the rule, is covered with foreign trading houses. At Banana Am: Whalers are seen almost daily during the Whaling season and Anchor there for water & provisions. I have forwarded in another despatch my report on the Congo. While in Banana and waiting on a sick bed for the mail steamer to Loanda, the U.S.S. Quinnebang Comdr Nicholl Ludlow, landed and took me on board and brought me here I have the honor to be Sir Your obedient servant Ls de R du Verge US Consul
