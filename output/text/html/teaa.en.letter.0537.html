No. 4 Department of State Received Nov 20 1889 Consulate of the United States, St. Paul de Loanda, October 12, 1889. Mr Ed Bannister. To the Department of State Subject: Sorghum. Abstract of Contents. Informs department that no Sorghum is grown in this district. No. 4. Consulate of the United States, St. Paul de Loanda, October 12, 1889 Honorable Wm F. Wharton Assistant Acting Secretary of State, Washington, D.C. Sir: In reply to your circular dated July 13, 1889. Conveying a request from the Secretary of Agriculture to collect and forward to the Department a small quantity of seeds of variety of small quantity of seeds of varieties of Sorghum, I have to inform you that such plant is unknown here. Bishop Wm Taylor of the American Mission here, some time ago informed me that he intended to try and introduce it from America, and would make his experiments on some of this mission stations in the interior. Whether he has done done so or not, I am not at present in a position to say, as I have heard nothing further on the subject, and the missionary in charge of the station here is at present in the interior thus preventing my getting any information from him on the subject. I am, Sir, Your obedient servant Ed Bannister. Acting Vice Consul.
