Department of State Received Aug 8, 1889 No 12 Commercial Agency of the United States, Boma, Congo Free State, Aug 5th, 1889 (Written at Culpeper, Virginia) Mr. Emory H. Taunt To the Department of State. Subject: Conditions of "Faith Cure Mission" on the Congo River Abstract of Contents Asking instruction in case they apply for assistance No. 12 Commercial Agency of the United States, Boma, Congo State, August 5th 1889 (Written at Culpeper Virginia) Honorable Wm F. Wharton Assistant Secretary of State, Washington, D.C. Sir: There are certain citizens of the United States now on the Congo River. Missionaries, belonging to a "Faith Cure Sect," Who, I fear, may become destitutes, Without means to return home. As will be seen by my despatch No 11, these people are now in anything but a prosperous condition. And as the members of this same mission in /85 or /86, were thrown upon the charity of the English Baptists, and by that society provided with passages to Europe, I, anticipating their coming to me for relief, respectfully ask instructions from the Department as to what action I am authorized to take in the matter. I am Sir: Your Obedient Servant Emory H. Taunt U.S. Com. Agent Boma
