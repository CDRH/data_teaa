Recd Mar 31
Ack April 5
No 5.

U.S. Comcl Agency
Gaboon Jany 3d 1866.

To the Hon. Wm H. Seward,
Secretary of State,
Washington D.C.

Sir: I have the honor to acknowledge, since my last, the receipt, from the Dep. of State, of despatch No 14, requesting me to continue to act as Vice Comcl Agt &c., proclamation of president Johnson in relation to N. Carolina, Circular in relation to the Am. Geo. and State Soc. N.Y. and a copy of "List of Diplomatic & Consular offices &c."

I reply to note on page 2 of said "List," that the "Manual" here, was printed at "Washington, 1863."

To note on page 54, I reply that "Gaboon" is erroneously put, on page 10, among "Portuguese dominions." It should be among French on page 9.

I have no arrivals of vessels to report during the past six months, nor any fees.

During the year 1865 only two American vessels (barks) have entered this port.

There is no material change in the trade. The articles of export (Ivory and Barwood) are becoming yearly scarcer & dearer; and year by year subject to more & more [?] competition.

I have the honor to be, Sir, your obt servt
J. M. Preston
Vice Comcl Agent.
