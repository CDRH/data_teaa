No. 25.

Consulate General of the United States, Monrovia Liberia
30 June 1877.

Sir,

I have the honor to transmit herewith my consular report for the quarter ending this day.

The difficulty which, on account of the scarcity of money at Monrovia, attends the exchange of Bills or Drafts of any considerable amount, has made Honorable Frederick W. Seward
Assistant Secty of State
Washington   made it necessary for me to draw, for my salary for the month of June, two drafts instead of one, as instructed heretofore. I regress this circumstance and will endeavor, hereafter to obey the instruction referred to ask near as it may be possible.

I have to be, Sir, with expressions of high esteem
Your obedient Servant,
J. Milton Turner
