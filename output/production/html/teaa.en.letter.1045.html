Legation of the United States
Monrovia May 31st 1887

Hon. Jas. D. Porter,

Sir I have the honor to inform you that I arrived at my post of duty on the 30th day of May A.D. 1887. The time actually taken to make the transit was from the 11th day of April A.D. 1887 to the 30th day of May A.D. 1887. Forty nine days. I have drawn Five (5) drafts, on the Secretary of State for Forty three days compensation allowed on account of "transit allowance" I was three days coming from Wyandotte Kansas to Washington D.C. Praying that you may approve my action. I am your obedient servant Chas. H. J. Taylor
U.S. Minister & Consular General
