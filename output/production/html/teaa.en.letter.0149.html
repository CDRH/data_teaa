United States Consulate
St. Paul de Loanda
Augt 11. 1882.

To the Hon: J. C. Bancroft Davis
Asst. Secty. of State
Washington D.C.

Sir

I have the honor to state that I have forwarded by same mail a monthly report of Diseases, Cases, and Deaths to the National Board of Health for the month of July; only of Europeans and mullatoes admitted to the hospital, but the diseases, cases, and deaths, occurring in the others different Natives villages around the city of Loanda, such as the Cabindas, Caboundos, and Crews, are almost impossible to be obtained by the Portuguese govt. consequently more so by one.

The Bilious or Green fever and Dysentry are raging at present among Europeans and Mullatoes, the report I have sent was obtained from one   of the best doctors of this place: Another peculiar disease of a dysenteric character and which is at present causing great havoc among the black children is called by the natives "Macculot or Macaullo", and is very quickly fatal if not attended to at once, when it can be very easily cured It commences with diarrhoea; the character of this disease is the production in the anal orifice both internally and externally of little ulcers containing small worms or maggots. The Native mode of treatment although very cruel is quickly efficacious and consist in plugging the orifice with a wad of crushed "filia and "Herva Santa Maria", dipped in strong solutions of Rum, Lime Juice powdered gun powder and Cayenne pepper.

This disease I was told is due to overcrowding quarters, want of cleanliness and improper foods; But change of places   will also produce it, as it can be seen by the boys and girls captured or stolen in the interior and sold daily for a pretended 3 to 5 years, servitudes which last forever.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your most obedient Servant
L de R. du Verge
US Consul
