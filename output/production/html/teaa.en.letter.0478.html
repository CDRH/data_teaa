REPORT

Consular Bureau
May 15th, 1875.

The oath must, in my opinion, be taken. The Act of July 2, 1862 (12 Stats. 502) prescribes the "iron clad" oath to be taken by every person elected or appointed to any office of honor or profit in the civil, military or naval service. That oath has been and is now required from Consuls who are not in the class of persons named in the Act of July 11, 1868 (15 Stats 85) whose legal disabilities have been removed. These oaths are prescribed by the Revised Statutes sections 1756 and 1757.

The oath must be taken before the appointee can enter upon the duties of his office or be entitled to any part of the salary or other envolvements.

A. B. Wood.
