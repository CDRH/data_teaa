Department of State Received Jun 30 1891

Legation of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, June 2nd 1891.

Mr. Beverly Y. Payne,
To the Department of State

Subject:

Death of Minister Clark.

 

15

Legation of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, June 2nd 1891.

Hon. James G. Blaine,
Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

It is my painful duty to inform you that, Alexander Clark, Esquire, United States Minister Resident and Consul General near the Government of Liberia, departed this life at this Legation, on the 31st ult., at 820 A.M. and his remains were interred on the 1st instant.

The President of Liberia, members of his cabinet, all Diplomatic Representatives in the City, were in attendence at the funeral, and Military and Masonic honors were shown him.

I have instructed Consul Bowser, at Sierra Leone to cablegram this sad   sad intelligence to the Department of State at Washington, D.C.

I have the honor to be,
Sir:
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne,
U.S. Vice Consul General.
