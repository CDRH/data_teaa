Department of State Received Feb 8 1890

No. 49

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, January 3rd, 1890.

Mr. Ezekiel E. Smith,
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Last Will and Testament of Mrs. Alice Fuller

 

No. 49.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, January 3rd, 1890.

Honorable Alvey A. Adee,
Second Assistant Secretary,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to acknowledge the receipt of your No. 9. Despatch of November 16th 1889, instructing me to procure a copy of the "Last Will and Testament" of Mrs. Alice Fuller, who died in this city in the month of June 1888.

In compliance with said instruction I have procured and inclose herein a copy, or said "Will."

I am,
sir,
Your obedient servant
Ezekiel E. Smith,
U.S. Consul General.

Enclosures one.

Copy of the Last Will and Testament of Mrs. Fuller.

 

In the name of God Amen I Alice Fuller of the City of Monrovia County of Montserrado and the Republic of Liberia being of Sound disposing mind but feeble in body do make public and declare this to be my Last Will and Testament in Manner following

1st After all my just debts and funeral expenses are paid, I give and devise my half Lot on Broad Street in the City of Monrovia to Clara Fuller Weaver wife of Lincoln Napoleon Weaver to her and her heirs forever, free from all control or disposal of her husband to have and to hold the same to her and her heirs forever.

2nd I give and devise to Mrs Sarah J. Woods wife of Tyson Woods of Cape Palmas, one of my Town Lots located if I recollect rightly in the Town of Greenville Sinoe County to have and to hold to her and her heirs forever and I give and devise the other two Lots located in said Greenville, one to my dear friend Emma A. Cooper and the other to my dear friend Sarah C. Yates to have and to hold the said property to each in severalty to them and their heirs

3rd I give and bequeath to Benjamin V. R. Melville, all my right and Title to the house in Front Street in Monrovia that I am now having built and I also give and bequeath to him Two Hundred dollars in order to help him to finish said House

 

4th I have several orders coming out from America and England and when they come and are disposed of I give and bequeath an equal share of the proceeds to the said B. V. R. Melville Anna W. Fuller his mother, my friend Sarah C. Yates, and my friend Maria Q. Powell and a Potion of said proceeds I wish to go to my friend Mrs Braxton But fifty six dollars of the money in these orders belong to Anna W. Fuller and the above division of proceeds cannot be made until after her said money are deducted out of the proceeds.

5th I give and bequeath all my new goods now in the house where I am living to my friends Anna W. Fuller B. R. Melville Maria Q. Powell Sarah C. Yates and Mrs Braxton to be divided in equal shares to all except Mrs Braxton who is to have a lesser share than the others

6th I give and bequeath to my dear friend Emma A. Cooper my large show case now in her possession, and anything else of mine that she may want; and also my Scales.

7th I give and bequeath to my friend Y. C. Dickens my small scales now in his possession

8th I have (Eighteen hundred dollars) $1800 now in the East Brooklyn Saving Bank which I wish to remain there on interest for two years after my death, at which time I want it divided. and I give and bequeath   out of said money (Five hundred dollars) to my afflicted Sister Mary Douglas residing in Charlstone South Carolina, and I request Messrs Yates & Porterfield of 59 South St New York who now have my Deposit Book in their possession to find out my said sister and pay over said money to her in person or to her legal representative, and to my nephews Robert Cand John B. Douglas of the City of Washington D.C. U.S.A. I give and bequeath Two hundred dollars each out of said money to be paid to them by Messrs Yates & Porterfield, and I give and bequeath to Mary M. Douglass wife of Robert I. Douglass my brother one hundred dollars $100 out of said money, and out of the remainder of said money I give and bequeath to my friend Maria Q. Powell one hundred dollars and to my friend Sarah C. Yates one hundred dollars and to my friend Emma A. Cooper one hundred dollars and to my friend Anna W. Fuller one hundred dollars and also one hundred dollars to my friend B. V. R. Melville also Fifty dollars to my dear friend Mrs Nimms also I give to Joseph M. Gibson Fifty dollars also to Mary Euphemia daughter of W. M. & E. S. Davis Fifty dollars to Mary McIntosh daughter of Anna V. Houston I give Fifty dollars and to Louisa Leone daughter of the late Lewis R. Leone I give Fifty dollars also I give Fifty dollars to my friend Thomas W. Howard   I give and bequeath the above several sums of money to the several persons above named in the order named The above erasure and alteration were made before this was signed Ye name Wm M. Davis and Benj. V. R. Melville to be Executors of this my last will and Testament

Monrovia June 14th 1888 (Signed) Alice Fuller

Signed Sealed and delivered in presence of us and declared by the said Alice Fuller to be her last will and testament in presence of us who at our names as witnesses

Emma A. Cooper
Sarah C. Yates

A true copy from the original the 2nd day of Jany 1890

N L Perkins
Clk Mo. & Pro. Ct Mo. Co.
