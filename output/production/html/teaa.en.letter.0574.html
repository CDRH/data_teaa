Subject

Arrival of United States Barque Azor with emigrants for Liberia, short of water and in want of medical assistance towed to Monrovia by S.S. Senegal

 
Department of State Received Jul 3 1878

No 30

United States Consulate
Sierra Leone, 4th June 1878

Sir

I beg to report for Your Excellency's information the arrival in this port on the 28th of May of the American barque "Azor", Captain Holmes, from Charleston, South Carolina, bound for Monrovia with emigrants. This vessel having drifted to within a short distance of this port and being without water and in need of medical assistance put into this port and received from the consulate and likewise from the Colonial Government every assistance that could be rendered to her. Out of her numerous passengers she had nineteen deaths before her arrival here, and two died while the vessel lay at anchor in this port. On the 1st inst, His Excellency
the Honble F. W. Seward   the vessel left here in tow of the British Mail Steamer "Senegal", and I hope that she has 'ere now safely arrived at her destination and landed her over crowded passengers.

Without expressing any opinion as to whether the "Azor" was properly filled out for the large number of passengers which she carried, and who I understand were sent out under the auspices of the Liberian Exodus Irish Stock Steam Ship Company, I deem it my duty to bring the fact and the circumstances of her arrival in this port to the Knowledge of the Department of State

I have the honor to remain
Sir
You must obedt servant
J. Broadhurst
Acting for U.S. Consul
