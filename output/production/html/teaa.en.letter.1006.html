Department of State Received Nov 30 1886

No. 35.

U.S. Legation
Monrovia
Oct. 28/86

Mr. S. S. Sevier
To the Assistant Secy. of State.

Subject.

Reception of Diplomatic series Nos. 11 and 12. Application for temporary recognition. Grave of the late United States Minister Moses A. Hopkins.

Synopsis

Temporary application for the office of Vice Consul Gen. Stone vault over the grave of the late U.S.M. Moses A. Hopkins.

 

U.S. Legation
Monrovia
Oct. 28/86.

Hon.
Jos. D. Porter
Assistant Secy.
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge Diplomatic series Nos. 11 and 12 under date Sept. 24th '86.

Herewith you will find enclosed my temporary application for the office of Vice Consul General of the United States at Monrovia. I only want the   the position until the next United States Minister is appointed and then I will send a satisfactory bond. The late Minster Moses A. Hopkins was buried during the rainy season and I could not then have his grave properly fixed at that time, but since the beginning of the dries[?] I have had it so done in the usual common way we fix our graves to prevent it from sinking under the heavy rains. I had a stone & vault   vault built over it which cost twenty-five dollars ($25.00) at the expense of the Legation. I could not bare see the grave of an American citizen in a dilapidated condition under such grand government as the United States, therefore I took the work upon myself to have it fixed. Hoping my undertaking will meet your approval and the Department States

I am sir
Your Obedient Servant
Samuel S. Sevier
Vice Consul.

Enclosure

1. Temporary application.

 
Department of State Received Nov 30 1886
Enclosure to Dispatch No. 35.

United States Legation
Monrovia
Oct 28th '86

Hon.
Jas. D. Porter
Assistant Secy. of State:-

Sir–

In my behalf please inform the Department of State that I this day apply for the position of Vice Consul General of the United States at Monrovia, until the next Minister is appointed to fill the vacancy caused by death   death of the late Minister Resident and Consul General Moses A. Hopkins. I shall endeavor to perform the duties of said office to the best of my ability.

I am Sir
Your Obedient Servant
Samuel S. Sevier
