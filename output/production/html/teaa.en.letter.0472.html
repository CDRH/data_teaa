No. 6

United States Consulate
Sierra Leone
June 2nd 1874

Sir

I have the honor to request that you will furnish this Consulate with a small parcel of assorted Stationary and a Consular Seal for immediate use.

The Bark "Albertina" has sailed from this port, I believe for Boston, but being under English colors, the Consignees did not apply to me for any papers. She is commanded by Captain Joseph Upton, and was consigned to Mess Randall and Fisher of this place, from Boston, by (I believe) Messrs Roberts and Ridout of the latter named place.

I have the honor to remain
Sir
Your obedient Servant
Nathl Lyons
U.S. Consul for Sierra Leone &c

The Honble J.C.B. Davis
Assistant Secretary
Department of State
Washington, U.S.A
