Subject.

The indigent Todd children; late of Minneappolis, Minnesota

 

No. 5, Consular Series.
Consulate Gen'l of the U. States,
Monrovia, Liberia,
Sept. 22, 1898.

Hon. J. B. Moore,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor, to say, that in my dispatch No. 4, Consular Series, Sept. 19, 1898, I endeavored to setforth the deplorable condition and suffering of the late J. C. Todd's children, late of Minneappolis; now in Liberia.

I appealed to the Department, if possible, for permission to send the said 4 children, back to America. In this, I desire to say that while writing my dispatch   dispatch,. No. 28, Diplomatic Series, Sept. 22, 1898, it suggested itself to me, that should the Department see fit to send a Cruiser out here in December, next, with the expectation of returning, direct to America, that by order of the Department, that would be the cheapest and most direct way of having said children returned to America.

I have the honor to be your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith
