Department of State Received Sep 21 1871
No 6

Commercial Agency of the United States of America at Sierra Leone July 9th 1871

Sir

I have the honor to acknowledge receipt of the Department Circular #5 dated at Washington March 6th 1871 and now transmit herewith a statement of all the information obtainable, as required by the Schedule accompanying the above circular intended for the U.S. Hydrographic Office. viz.

1st No charts published by local authorities

2d The entrance to the Sierra Leone River is obstructed by an extensive sand bank called the Middle Ground, which in many parts is dry at low water - there is a passage on either side of it.   of it.

The northern passage is only fit for small vessels & by them seldom attempted. The principal channel lies between Cape Sierra Leone and this bank, having a clear passage of more than a mile in breadth, carrying a depth of from 5 to 12 fathoms.

Carpenter Rock the only danger on the Southern Shore lies W. 1/4 N. mile distant from N.W. extremity of the Cape, it shows at half-tide and may be easily distinguished by its breakers, and it should be well borne in mind that both Flood & ebb tides set directly upon it.

On the extremity of Cape Sierra Leone a light house 69 ft. high was errected in 1849 in which is a fixed light, visible 9 miles in clear weather, it stands in 8° 30 N. and 13° 17' 45" W., and bears from Carpenter Rock W. 1/2 N. from western edge of Middle Ground   Middle Ground N.E. 1/2 N.

Ships coming from sea will probably endeavor to strike sounding[?] &[?] in 8° 30 N. with 9 or 10 fathoms of water, they will be about 4 leagues from shore, or if the weather be clear they will have seen the high land - which should be brought to bear E.S.E.; and when about 4 or 5 leagues from the land, the Cape will be seen making like a low point, with a ridge of Cocoa nuts trees close to the Water's edge; and shortly after the Light house will is seen

The cape may be passed within a quarter of a mile with a commanding breeze in 10 fathoms.
