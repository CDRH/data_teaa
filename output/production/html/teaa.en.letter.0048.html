Recd 5 Nov.
(No. 4)

July 31/63
Commercial Agency of the United States
at St Paul De Loanda

Sir

I have the honour of advising you of my arrival at my Post of Duty on the 15th day of July 1863. After a passage (by sea) of Sixty Days, and ten days occupied in transit between my Place of Residence (Havre De Grace Md.) and detention in Boston Harbor by contrary minds, the latter occupying nine days, makeing in all Seventy Days transit;

I have drawn upon the Hon Secretary of the Treasury for the amount due me for transit viz $217.56. being obliged to allow 15 pr cent upon the face of the Draft, netting me $186.93 the net amount due me after deducting $2.28 for internal revenue tax, and $3.00 for Passport forgotten while in Washington;

I was unable to wait upon his Exelency "Sr. Jos√© Baptista D' Andrade" Governor General of the Province of Angola, until the 30th of July, in consequence of formalities necessary to be observed;

Having complied with the customary Etiquette of the Country, on the said 30th of July, I called upon his excellency, accompanied by John G. Willis, Esq. and Snr. Francisco Antonio Flores as interpreter and presented my commission for his inspection, he was entirely satisfied that it was Genuine.

But it having been customary for Agts here to be furnished with an Exequatur, in accordance with the 10th article of the Treaty of 1840. He could not recognize me in the official "Boletin [illegible] furniture viz Chairs, settee, &c. and a book case or "Escritoir" obtained by me by Receipting for it to Mr Willis (a copy enclosed) for the purpose of keeping the Archives and Books belonging to this agency safe;

The said property was in possession of Mr Birney, formerly U. States Naval Store keeper here, left with Mr Cunningham by Mr Birney as property of the United States, and by him left with Mr Willis, each of the last named Gentlemen giving their receipts to their predisessors for the property;

When I presented the Order of your Honour upon Mr Cunningham for articles in his possession, to Mr Willis, I called his attention to the latter clause of said Order namely "and all other property in your possession belonging to the United States" Mr. Willis informed me that the said property was not intended to be included by said order, because it is property under the exclusive control of the Navy Department;

Not being prepared in the absense of instructions in the premises to controvert this position, I leave it; simply calling the attention of the Department to the facts, to the end that in case it was the intention to place said property in my possession, that neither my Bondsmen or myself be held responsible for it, inasmuch as the said property is not now, or ever has been in my possession;

I beg leave to call your attention to a custom (or Practice) that has obtained here, regarding the place of Deposit of Ships Papers, namely by Sec 479 manuel of 1863 it is made the Duty of every Master of an American Vessel to deposit the papers (ships Papers) with the Consular Officer, and in default heavy Penalties; but here the ships papers are retained by the Custom House authorities, consequently no Consular Certificates (as per instructions) can be given;

Mr Willis assures me that he has attempted to induce an abandonment of said practice. but without effect, and from present information upon the subject, I am induced to believe that the services of one having higher powers than myself, (probably our Minister to Portugal) is necessary to accomplish the desired reformation and enable ship masters and Commercial agents to comply with the United States Law;

By schedule no 1 (herewith enclosed) it will be perceived that this Post has no Books of Record as required by sections 391-392 of manuel of 1863. Consequently the Records are upon unbound detached peices of paper, very liable to loss by accident;

Other articles also are much needed as per enclosed schedule No 3. Please send them as soon as practicable, the agency being absolutely destitute of them;

Mr Willis informs me that he has received notice that a box containing a flag and other articles has been sent to this Post via New York, but it has not been received here, and it is probably now lying in that City or entirely lost

There is no certain way by which articles intended for this Post can be sent, except via Salem Mass, Care of R Brookhouse & co, and viz England, Care of U.S. Consul of St Helena and via Lisbon, care of U.S. Consul at Lisbon, no vessels sail from the U.S. Direct to this Port, except from Salem Mass and occasionally one from Boston;

I have the Honour of informing your that it has been and is now the Custom at this Port for Oaths to be administered by the Commercial Agents to Citizens of the United States;

By Circular No 8 of Feb 21st 1863, directed to me; and by Art 1st[?] of said Circular, I understand that I am instructed to render an account for office Rent to the amount attained by General instructions, namely, 10 per cent on the amount of salary which I am thankful for; inasmuch as the salary allowed is barely adequate to a support;

Not being engaged in trade of any kind the room occupied by me is used exclusively for the business of the agency;

It gives me much pleasure to acknowledge the kindness of John G. Willis Esq in affording me every desireable facility for transacting the business of the agency;

With much Respect
I have the Honour to be
Yours Most Obed
John T. Bradberry
Com Agt

Hon
Secretary of State
of the United States Washington

Loanda July 30th 1863

Received this day from John G Willis Esqr one Escritoir or book case as a Depository for archives, books and papers belonging to the United States Commercial Agency at St Paul De Loanda, and to be returned when called for

John T. Bradberry,
Com Agt
