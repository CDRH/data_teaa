Department of State Received Feb 21 1888

No 3.

U.S. Legation.
Monrovia, Liberia,
January 17th 1888.

Sir.

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your communication No 9 Diplomatic Series of December 9th 1887, with seven enclosures.

I take pleasure in reporting that I have in accordance with your instruction delivered in person Mr Eccleston and Bishop Penick's letters to Mr Barclay Secretary of State of Liberia, and expressed orally my personal satisfaction that the incident was at an end, which was reciprocated by Sec'y Barclay. The copy enclosures I have on file in this Legation.

Referring to my No 2. of the 23rd ult., I have the honor to state, that Mr Taylor saw fit to have me in charge of this Legation in the capacity of Acting Vice Vice   Vice Consul General, to be appointed Vice Consul General immediately after his arrival to Liberia: since his departure, I have heard (unofficially) of his resignation of the position of Minister Resident and Consul General to Liberia. hence I take pleasure in transmitting to you my Bond, which I trust will meet your approval.

Referring to Mr. Taylor's No 10 of 30th September last, in which he stated, "A Bond availeth nothing in this Country (Liberia) so far as the United States is censored", I have for my Bondmen the chieves of the most wealthy mercantile business houses in the County, General R. A. Sherman, and the H. J. Moore, (the latter of the firm of Messrs G. Moore & Son.) both of whom are known to have capital in the United States and of good good   good reputation.

I am at present unaware of my real position, and what course to persue in the management of this Legation.

Awaiting your instructions
I am,
Sir,
Your Obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne.

Hon.
T. F. Bayard,
Secretary of State.
