Department of State Received Aug 20 1891

Consulate General of the United States.
Monrovia, July 1st 1891.

Mr Beverly Y. Payne,
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Explanation of Quarterly Account rendered, and the effects of the late Consul General A. Clarke.

 

17

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, July 1, 1891.

Hon., William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to state, that I have rendered full Account for the quarter ended June 30th 1891. Sixty days of which the late Consul General Clarke was in charge: there are no outstandings in paid quarter but his salary.

I have not assumed to charge any of his personal nor burial expenses to the Government, but will ship his effects to his relatives in Muscatine, Iowa and render full account to them, by an American Bark, expected at this port daily, and I have so informed them.

I shall keep a copy   copy of said account on file in this Consulate, and if required, can furnish the Department with same.

I have the honor to be,
Sir:
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne
U.S. Vice Consul.
