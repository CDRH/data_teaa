Department of State Received Nov 2 1889

No. 33.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, September 19th 1889.

Mr. Ezekiel E. Smith
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Receipt of Circular.

 

No. 33.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, September 19th 1889.

Honorable William F. Wharton,
Acting Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to acknowledge the receipt of the Department Circular of August 2nd 1889, with reference to the financial condition of Liberia. It reached me on the 17th inst., and was transmitted to the Secretary of State of Liberia on the morning of the 18th.

I beg to transmit, herein, a report on the Pilotage, Light Dues, Tonnage &c, as required by the laws of Liberia. The   The information contained in the report was asked, of the Secretary of State of Liberia, on the 24th of August, but not received until to day.

I have the honor to be,
sir,
your obedient servant,
Ezekiel E. Smith
U.S. Consul General.

1. Report on the Pilotage, Light Dues, Tonnage &c in Liberia.
