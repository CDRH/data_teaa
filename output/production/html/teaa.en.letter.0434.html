Consular Bureau,
February 11th, 1874.

Subject: The office of Vice Commercial agent at Sierra Leone

Upon the nomination of H. Rider, formerly Commercial Agent at Sierra Leone Mr. J. B. Upton was appointed Vice Commercial Agent on February 20 1871 and the usual certificate was issued. Mr Upton writes from Sierra Leone March 30, 1871, ackg. the receipt of notice of appt., and says that the condition of his health compels him to have for America where he will fill his bond. He reports his arrival at New York on August 29, 1871, and that he had left Mr. Wm Hogan in charge of the office. He has not informed the Department of his return to his post nor of his intention to return.

Upon Mr. Hogan's appointment his commission was made out as Consul; He made no nomination for Vice Consul, but on leaving his past he states (August 7, 1873) that Mr. Nathaniel Lyons will be left in change. This dispatch was ordered "filed", and no action was taken. When Mr. Hogan was appointed Consul, the name of Mr. Upton as V.C.A. should, I think, have been[?] Â  stricken out of the Register.

Resy submitted
A. B. Wood.
