Department of State.

Monrovia June 8th 1888.
Liberia W.C.A.

Sir,

In response to a request emanating from your Legation I have the honor to communicate for the information of your Government, the following particulars relative to the present outbreak or rebellious attitude assumed by certain of the aboriginal tribes of the south eastern portion of the Republic, towards my Government and which in its opinion constitutes an emergency or a sufficient reason for a solicitation of the immediate assistance of the Government of the United States by means of its naval forces and otherwise.

About the middle of last year the major portion of the inhabitants of Half Cavalla, a district about 12 miles South of Cape Palmas, began to exhibit systems of restlessness and a disposition to obstruct the execution of, and to disobey the Revenue Hon Chas H. J. Taylor

Efforts [?] immediately set on foot by the authorities of the County of Maryland to allay their manifestations in a peaceful manner, by convening[?] a council of the adjacent tribes in compact with the Government and before whom the conduct of the Half Cavalla Tribe was brought for consideration. The council unanimously condemned the course taken by the Half Cavalla Tribe as being improper and disloyal, [?] them in a small fine, which after considerable demurring on the part of the offenders, was agreed to be paid and matters seemed quiet for the present. Towards the close of the year however, this tribe [?] thereto by the intrigues of one Julio, a foreigner and illicit trader, residing at Saboo[?], and another British trader setting at defiance our Revenue Laws at Half Cavalla itself, assumed an attitude of open defiance, and insubordination toward the Government, and in spite of repeated efforts on the part of the Authorities to induce them to peacefully recede from their rebellious attitudes, and return to their allegiance, have steadfastly refused to permit the execution of the laws within their district, have organized themselves into an armed force fortified Â  fortified their villages; and have openly declared their intentions to resist by force of arms any attempt of the Government to destroy peace and order in their districts. They have also appealed to the British Government through the Governors of its Colonies adjacent to the Republic of Liberia to be taken under its protectorate, and are now actively endeavoring to stir up and enlist more powerful tribes as their auxillaries in this outbreak, so as to assume the offensive against the Government as soon as possible, and have lately expelled from their midst christianzed and civilized natives to the number of 80 more or less, whose only offense was, a decided refusal to fight against the Government to whom they had sworn allegiance.

It is but fair however to state that the British Naval authorities on the West Coast have so far declined affording any aid to these insurgents, and their sympathies have hitherto been exerted on our behalf, but the fact that they do not count us prime factors in this matter, will I trust be made sufficiently clear in the succeeding paragraph of this Despatch.

The effect of these occurrences has has been to paralyze lawful commerce in that portion of the Republic, and to gravely embarrass our government. It is obvious that if this state of affairs at Half Cavalla, should continue unchecked, that a powerful combination will soon be formed against the Government, incited and openly aided and abetted by the foreign traders aforesaid and secretly encouraged by certain Foreign Governments, signatories of the Berlin Conference, who for some time past have been intriguing to obtain possession of this position of our Territory, if not indeed of the entire territory of the Republic. With reference to the points raised in Enclosure C. the undersigned would remark as follows;

1. That Liberia does not contemplate or project an extension of Frontier, the territories between the Grand Cesters and San Pedro Rivers having been an integral part of our territory since the year 1857, (before that period it was comprised in the State of Maryland in Liberia 1834-6, 1846-1850) and recognised as such by the European Powers until the era of the "scramble for Africa".

2. Liberia is neither a European Power nor a signatory of the decisions of of the Berlin Conference, she was not invited to assist in those deliberations, and is therefore not bound by its decisions, and further those decisions refer to future acquisitions of African Territory by European Powers, and not to the present possessions or future acquisitions of an African State.

My Government declines on these grounds to commit suicide, as it would do by adopting the course suggested by the British of Foreign Office in Enclosure C. or even for the present to enter into such correspondence with Great Britain as may occasion the opening a South eastern Boundary question which seems to be desired by the gentlemen of the Foreign Office hinder the pressing circumstances my Government feels itself compelled to solicit the interference of that of the United States in this matter between itself and its aboriginal citizens, in the following measures, in which it is to be hoped Mr. Minister Your Government will see its way clear the concur viz:

Naval Naval co-operation with the Liberian forces for the subsiding of this outbreak, against the authority of the Republic.

Assisting in establishing and protecting one or more settlements in the Southeastern portion of the Republic one of them being planted on the San Pedro River as the Port of Entry, which would confront foreign intrigant with accomplished facts.

The keeping up of regular communication between the proposed settlements and the centre of Government, and the enforcement of our Revenue Laws along our Coast. For these purposes the gunboat recommended by His Excellency President Cleveland in his last message to the U.S. Congress to be presented to the Republic of Liberia, and regular visits of a U.S. Ship would be invaluable.

Soliciting Mr Minister the exertion of your good offices with your Government in regard to its favorable action on the particulars herein brought to your notice. I have the honor to be with sentiments of the highest consideration and esteem

Your obedient Servant
