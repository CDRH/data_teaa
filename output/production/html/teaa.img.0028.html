Among the attendees of the First Universal Races Congress of 1911 were representatives from Egypt and Haiti—Mohammed Sourour Bey and General Legitime, respectively. In addition, J. Tengo Jabavu (a South African newspaper editor) and Mojola Agbebi (a pastor from Nigeria), both important political activists, participated in the congress. W.E.B. Du Bois attended as the representative of "The American Negro."
