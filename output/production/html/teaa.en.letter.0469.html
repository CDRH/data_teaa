United States Consulate
Sierra Leone
May 25th 1874

Sir

I have the honor to acknowledge receipt of your dispatches Nos 2 and 3. to which I beg to reply.

The Consular property has been siezed by Mssrs Harris & Co for the expenses of the American Consulate only. The amount claimed is exorbitant, and they have no agreement or contract of any kind to show that they are entitled to that amount. Up to the time of the departure of the late Consul W. Hogan Esq: Messrs Harris & Co received all the fees of the Consulate, by private arrangement with the said W. Hogan. They are not therefore entitled to any thing up to August 1st last. Be pleased to instruct me in this matter.

I should suggest a settlement by arbitration fully believing such a course practicable.

I have the honor to remain
Sir
Your obedient Servant
Nathl Lyons
U.S. Consul for Sierra Leone &c.

Honble J. C. B. Davis
Assistant Secy
Department of State
Washington U.S.
