Feby 4/59
 

By virtue of Authority vested in me as Commander in Chief of the United States Naval Forces on the West Coast of Africa, I hereby appoint John E Taylor, Acting Consul of the United States of America, for the Colony of Sierra Leone on the West Coast of Africa, and do authorize and empower him to have and to hold the said Office and to exercise and enjoy all the rights, pre-eminences, privileges, and authorities, to the same of right appertaining: during the pleasure of the President of the United States, or of the Commander in Chief on the West Coast of Africa, for the time being, he demanding and receiving no fees or perquisites of office whatsoever, which shall not be expressly established by some law of the United States.

And I do hereby enjoin all Captains, Masters, and Commanders of Ships and other vessels, armed or unarmed, sailing under the Flag of the said States, as well as all other of their citizens to acknowledgement and   and consider him, the said John E Taylor accordingly

And I do hereby pray and request the Governor and Officers of the Colony of Sierra Leone to permit the said John E Taylor, fully and peacefully to enjoy and exercise the said Office, without giving or suffering to be given unto him, any molestation or trouble; but on the contrary, to afford him all proper countenance and assistance in accordance with the usage happily existing between the Government of Great Britain, and of the United States of America.

Given under my hand on board the U.S. Flag Ship "Constitution" at Sierra Leone the 14th day of August, in the year of our Lord, One thousand eighth hundred and fifty three.

(sd) I. Mayo
Commander in Chief
U.S. Naval Forces

Secretary's Office
Sierra Leone 8th June 1854

Sir,

I have received the Governor's Commands to inform you that a man by the name of William Taylor declaring himself to be a subject of the United States of America has been admitted to the Colonial Hospital and become chargeable to the Government as a pauper.

I am to request that you will be good enough to state for the information of His Excellency whether you are prepared to take charge of the man in question on his discharge from the Hospital and to pay for his treatment therein.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your most obedient
humble servant
(sd) J. F. Smyth

West Coast of Africa, Sierra Leone
September 1854

The Humble Memorial of the undersigned the commander of vessels belonging to citizens of the United States of America; Merchants; super cargoes traders and others citizens of America trading or residing on the West Coast of Africa.

Humbly Sheweth,

1 That we your memorialists have after much consideration on the subject matter of this petition come to the unanimous conclusion that it is our duty to the Government of which we are citizens to report the very unsatisfactory position in which the representation of the American Government stands in the Colony of Sierra Leone.

2 That notwithstanding the very important and extensive commerce between the United States and West Coast of Africa, and particularly with this Colony it is a singular fact that there has never been a Consul nor event Consular Agent appointed by the United States Government as its representative here.

That   3 That on the 11th of August 1853 Commander I Mayo of the United States Flag Ship "Constitution" having due regard to the interests of his Government was pleased to appoint Captain John Etting Taylor an American citizen and Merchant residing in this Colony as Acting Consul on the West Coast of Africa.

4 That such acting appointment has never been confirmed by the Government of the United States nor legally recognized by the English Government.

5 That although the American Government has at all times sanctioned those acts pertaining to the consulship, which have been performed by the acting Consul and bearing in mind the fact that the local authorities in Sierra Leone have hitherto recognized Captain Taylor as acting Consul, yet we regard it as unsafe to permit matters to remain as they now are, inasmuch as it is in the power of the English Government whenever it may think proper to decline any further recognition of the authority of the preset acting Consul.

6 That your memorialists desire to bear testimony to the strict attention which the acting Consul has at all times paid to the requirements of the commanders of American vessels and American citizens generally on this   part of the Coast and to his sensitive regard for the dignity and honor of the Union.

Your memorialists therefore pray that the United States Government will be pleased to confer this appointment of Consul of the United States Government on this Coast on the present acting Consul. And your memorialists will ever pray &c.
