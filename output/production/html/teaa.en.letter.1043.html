Department of State Received Jun 6 1887

No 70.

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia May 3rd 1887.

Mr. S. S. Sevier
To the Secretary of State.

Subject

Receipt of Dep. Series No 18 with certificate inclosed.

Synopsis

Dep. series No 18–Exequatur from Liberian Government– inclosure of Hon. E. J. Barclay Secretary of State.

 

No. 70.

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia May 3rd 1887.

Hon. T. F. Bayard
Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform you that your Dep. Series No. 18, under date March 17th 1887 with the enclosed certificate received. Exequatur from the Liberian Government, also received.

I herewith enclose a despatch No. 1. under date April 29th/87 from   from Hon. E. J. Barclay Secretary of State of the Republic of Liberia.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient servant
Saml S. Sevier
U.S. Vice Consul Genl.

Enclosure

Hon. E. J. Barclay to S. S. Sevier

 
Copy.

No 1.

Department of State
Monrovia April 29, 1887.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your No. 14 of the 21st inst, informing me of your appointment to the port of United States Vice Consul General near[?] this Government, enclosing your certificate of appointment from the Hon. Thomas F. Bayard secretary of state of the United States of America Washington D.C. and requesting that the necessary exequatur from my Government   will be granted you in the usual manner.

2d In reply I am to say that I have exhibited your communication and Certificate to the President and that His Excellency has been pleased to act favorably with regard to the same. I have therefore the pleasure to enclose herewith your Certificate and the Exequatur recognizing you in the capacity to which you are appointed, with my congratulations and the hope that the good relations of friendship now   existing between our respective Governments may be increased and strengthened thereby.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your Obedient Servant
E. J. Barclay
Secretary of State.

Sam'l S. Sevier Esq.
U.S. Vice Consul Genl
U.S. Legation
Monrovia.
