Acknowledgment of the same and trade on Petroleum.

 

United States Consulate
St Paul de Loanda
December 7th 1882

Hon: W: Hunter
Acting Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your 2 circulars dated Sept 5th and 13th 1882. In answer to your circular of Sept. 13th I would respectfully state that I have already brought the subject before the civil and military goods of Angola with the Islands of St. Thomé and Princess included, offering them the best of refined Petroleum for city and military use, as mentioned to the state Dept. in my despatch No 6. "That Kerosene oil was in great demand here and all over this coast. But the shippers and masters of Am: vessels should be very careful how they deal with the Whites and Blacks of this part of the country, Am: commerce has almost run down by Am: shippers and [?] own fault, as reported in my despatch No 17. Another important trade will soon be of farming implements of all kinds.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient servant
L. de R. du Verge
US Consul
