United States Commercial Agency
Loanda May 23 1860.

Sir.

Herewith I enclose Returns from this Commercial Agency for the Quarter ending March 31, 1860. The several accounts & certificates I have directed to the Treasury Department & have drawn on the Hon Secretary of Treasury for $227.11Â¢ being balance of my Salary for that Quarter.

I last had the honor of addressing you under date of January 24 enclosing Returns for the Quarter ending December 31, 1859 at which time I requested leave of absence to take effect as soon as practicable after the receipt of your answer, which I trust has been granted.

For three months past there has been considerable trouble at Ambriz & Kisembo caused by the receipt of a Letter at Ambriz sent by HBM Commissioner of this place, but said to have been written by the Brazilian Consul of this Port, said Letter being directed to His Royal Highness Don Nicolau, Prince of Congo &c &c. said Nicolau a Black being at the time employed by the Portuguese Govern--ment Hon Lewis Cass
Secretary of State. -ment at Ambriz in some moderate capacity.

The Letter informed "Nicolau" that the Governor-General of this Province intended to imprison him for writing to the Lisbon Journals in respect to his Government, & advising him to leave immediately for Kisembo. On his arrival at Kisembo he entered an English house & soon afterwards the Natives collected & demanded him. After considerable delay Don Nicolau was given up & immediately shot & beheaded by the Natives.

As there is an American House at Kisembo owned by Messrs Brookhouse & Co. of Salem Mass. I at once on hearing the proceedings of the Natives requested Capt Colhoun of the USS "Portsmouth" to go there, to which request he immediately complied. On his arrival at Kisembo & during his stay there of two or three days, everything was quiet. Some days afterwards the Gov. Genl of this Province went with a force of 300 to 400 men to Ambriz & Kisembo. At the latter place they burnt the native town & then returned to Loanda. While the Portuguese Troops were at Kisembo, Capt Brent of the USS "Marion", together with Capt Fitzroy of the HBMS "Falcon" were on Shore with a number of their men. On the arrival of the Gov Genl with his Troops requesting permission to enter the Houses for Refreshment & Rest. he was Refused, as it was the opinion of the Residents there, that if the Portuguese entered, their Lives & Property would not be so secure after the Portuguese Americans & English left, as they would if they were not allowed to enter. Since then the Natives have made two unsuccessful attacks on Ambriz. Trade continues to be brought in by the Natives to Kisembo & it is now quiet there.

About the 1st of April some Portuguese Soldiers were coming from the Copper Mines to Ambriz & when within 15 miles of the latter place they were attacked by a large number of Natives & obliged to retreat. in doing so they jumped into the River "Loge" in order to cross to the other side. Their loss was near 100, including one Lady, the Wife of the officer in command of the Party.

The Roads between Ambriz & the Mines are filled with Natives & the Portuguese have collected a force of about 1000 men to march into the Interior. They left Ambriz on the 21st to punish the Natives & also open the roads to the Mines. This force is commanded by the best of the Portuguese officers attached to this Province, & it is thought will be sufficient to drive the Natives from the vicinity.

This interference of HBM Commissioner & the Brazilian Consul General, has been the source of great anxiety to the Government of this Province, & causing great expense. It will probably result in the Portuguese Government requesting the withdrawal of the above mentioned parties from their official functions.

Very Respectfully
Your Obt Servt
John G. Willis
US Coml Agent.
