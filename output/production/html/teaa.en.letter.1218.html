Index Bureau Dept of State Received Aug 7 94

No 39.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, June 30, 1894.

Mr Beverly Y. Payne,
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Accounts and Returns for the quarter ending June 30, 1894.

 

No 39.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, June 30, 1894.

Honorable Edwin F. Uhl,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor most respectfully to transmit herewith the Accounts and Returns of this Consulate General for the quarter ending this date, as per subjoined list of enclosures: also, Annual return of Exports for the fiscal year. I beg to say, this return is far that of the exports from this Consular district to the United States on account of the major portion of coffee   coffee having been shipped to Hamburg and Liverpool, and transhipped to the United States. Invoices for such shipments are not sent to this Consulate for verification. I have to regret not being able to transmit herewith Aggregate of Fees for this Consular district the Returns of the Consular Agent at Cape Coast has not reached the Consulate for the June quarter 1894. I have the honor to be,
Sir:
Your obedient servant,
Beverly Y. Payne,
U.S. Vice Consul.

Enclosure.

1. Annual Return of Exports.
2. Contingent Expense account
3. Certificate as to absence
4. General quarterly account
5. Vouchers (5) for Contingent Expenses.
