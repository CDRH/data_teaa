Department of State Received Jan 12 1880

No 10 US Consulate Sierra Leone Dec. 13, 1879

J A Lewis To Dept of State

Paying transportation of destitute Seaman, Draft for $255.36

 
No 10

U.S. Consulate at
Sierra Leone Dec. 13th 1879

Hon F. W. Seward
Asst Secty State
Washington D.C.

Sir

I have to inform the Department that there arrived at this Consulate on Dec. 1st 1879 Ten destitute Seaman from the American Brig Julia F. Carney of Boston which was burned at Loango Oct 25th 1879.

Said Seamen arrived here by Steamer "John Dixon" and then being no U.S. Consul which they could reach nearer than Sierra Leone (Steamer not calling on her way up) I gave a Draft in Secty of State to Henry Bricknall and Sons for $255 35/100 for said transportation to this place. Four of the said Sailors I shipped sent   on the following day per Bark "Hazard" to Boston the Balance will go by the Schooner Walter F.[?] Plummer leaving here in a few days for New York I gave the drafts on Seconds and Thirds as I had no blanks Firsts.

I have the honor to be
Sir Your Obt Servt
Judson A. Lewis
U.S. Consul.
