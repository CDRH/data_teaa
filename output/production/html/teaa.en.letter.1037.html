Department of State Received May 7 1887

No. 64.

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia April 4th/87.

Mr S. S. Sevier
To Secretary of State

Subject

Trouble at Cavalla

Synopsis

Disloyalty and rebellion.

 

No. 64.

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia April 4th/87.

Hon. T. F. Bayard
Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to refer you to my despatch No. 48 of which I sometime wrote in regard to the trouble at Cavalla. Since then the trouble has not quailed. Spirit of disloyalty and rebellion is at work. The civilized christian native and many of the Americo-Liberian have been forced   forced from their homes to subject on the kindness of their friends or starve. His excellency President H. R. W. Johnson of this Republic has not as yet taken any steps to arest the spirit of rebellion. Further information will be given to the Department of State whenever steps are taken to arest the present trouble.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
Sam'l S. Sevier
U.S. Vice Consul Genl.

To the Monrovia Public:

There is trouble at Cavalla; spirit of disloyalty and rebellion is at work. Because the civilized and christian natives did not sympathize, with this feeling, they were forced to leave their homes and relations to save their lives, and flee to Harper, Cape Palmas. Leaving their farms and employments they are deprived of the means of support, and are dependent upon christian help until they can help themselves.

The people at Cape Palmas, have done, and are doing all in their power for the comfort of the refugees. This appeal comes to you now. What will you do? Out of your abundance will you give much, and out of your poverty give a little? Every little will be a help. Let not the benevolent hard working poor fail to give because they have but a little: but of that little give something.

Every thing is needed, food, raiment, household articles &c: the sufferers number between forty and fifty men, women and children. Show your liberality towards your brethren and sisters of this native church, the church of the Epiphany at Cavalla. Right Rev. Bishop Ferguson will gladly bear to them your christian gifts.

C. T. O. King,
Mayor.

City of Monrovia,
March 21st 1887.
