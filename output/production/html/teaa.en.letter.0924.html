J Milton Turner

Subject:

Referring to the propriety of removing Commercial Agency from Grand Bassa to Cape Palmas

 

No. 11.

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia Liberia 29th December 1875

Sir,

I have the honor to state the fact that American vessels entering and clearing this port, invariably go to Grand Bassa and Cape Palmas; from which latter port, they frequently clear for the United States and no official information is received of them after leaving Grand Bassa. This suggested the propriety of having the post of the Commercial Agency removed from Bassa to Cape Palmas which is the other extreme port of entry of this Republic. Since the removal to that post would also in chide the reports from the ports between Monrovia and Cape Palmas, the two extreme ports of entry, together with the fact that there are greater American interests at Cape Palmas than any other port on the Liberian coast. The recent troubles at Honorable
John L. Cadwalader
Assistant Secretary of State, Washington   at Cape Palmas also furnished strong means of confirming my opinion in this matter.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
With high consideration
Your obedient servant
J Milton Turner
