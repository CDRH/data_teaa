Department of State Received May 5 1888

No 7.

Consulate General of the United States.
Monrovia, Liberia, March 31, 1888.

Mr. B. Y. Payne to the Assistant Secretary of State

Subject.

Returns for Quarter ended March 31st 1888.

Sir.

I have the honor to respectfully transmit the following as my returns or report[?] since my incumbency October 1887 to date, which consist of Digest of Invoice book (1) of Arrivals and departure of American vessels (2) Summary of Consular business (3) Record of official official   official fees (4), Miscelleneous expenses with vouchers (5) and General account (6)

Permit me to express my deep regret that I have not forwarded these returns in regular order (quarterly): My reason for not doing so, was that Mr. Taylor requested me, not to, until his return, which would have been before the ending of December quarter, last, according to his arrangement, and not until I had entered upon the quarter ending this date did I hear of his resignation, and forwarded my Bond.

I have put no house house   rent in the account, but there is one quarter due Mr Sharp, to date: having no authority to settle same, I have requested him to await until I can get advice in reference to the matter.

I have further to state in explanation of my account rendered, that the bill of Mr R. A. Sherman, is for landing the case Stationary &c consigned to the Legation when Mr Taylor was in charge, which was carried to the Leeward, and landed a few days after Mr Taylor's departure. The Bill of Messers G. Moore & Son, is for sundries which ever necessary to be be   be had for the cleanliness of, and light for safety of the Legation.

The two boys Thomas Roberts, and [unclear, one name] were left at the Legation by Mr Taylor.

I trust my report will be in order and satisfactory, as Mr. Taylor left the Legation to return in two and a half months, and matters very scattered and awkward.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your very obedient Servant
Beverly Y Payne.

Hon.,
George L. Rives,
Ass't Sec'y State
