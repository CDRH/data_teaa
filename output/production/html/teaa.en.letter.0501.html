New York, June 11, 1888

Hon. G. L. Rives
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform the department that on my departure from my Consular district to visit the United States on leave, because of the absence of Vice Consul Newton at Madeira. I was forced to entrust Mr Julius L. Judson with the property of the United States in any charge and he trusting that my recommendation for his appointment as Acting U.S. Vice Consul would be favorably acted upon is now performing the duties of the office and when so instructed will forward to the department his bond.

Mr Judson is a citizen of the United States, a graduate of one of our colleges and a man of intelligence and proberty and is worthy if the favorable consideration of the department.

I have the honor to be
Sir,
Your obedient servant
Henry F. Downing
U.S. Consul
