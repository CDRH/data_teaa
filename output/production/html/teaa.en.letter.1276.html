Subject.

The United States' Cruiser, Montgomery, in Liberian Waters.

Synopsis.

Reached Monrovia, Oct 14, 1899, and sailed Oct 18, 1899.

 

No. 37, Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia,
Oct. 18, 1899.

Hon. David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to transmit, herewith, the intelligence, that the United States' Cruiser Montgomery, John P. Merrell, Commander, from Pernambuco Brazil, reached this Port, Saturday afternoon– Oct., 14, 1899. We arranged for and obtained an audience with His Excellency, the President of Liberia, and his Cabinet, at the Executive Mansion, at 3, oclock, p.m., on Monday the 16th instant; with   with an adjourned conference, to Tuesday the 17th at 2:15 p.m.

The presence of an American Man of war, in Liberian waters, were highly gratifying; warmly received by the Liberian populus; most hospitably entertained; and in company with the Minister Resident and Consul-General; and the Secretary of the Legation, dined with His Excellency, the President and his Cabinet, at the Executive Mansion, in the afternoon of Tuesday Oct. 17, 1899; at which time Commander Merrell, proposed the health of His Excellency, the President of the Republic of Liberia. Following which, His Excellency, in acknowledging the very cordial and friendly relations existing   existing between the United States and the Republic of Liberia, during the administration of President McKinley, proposed the health of His Excellency Wm. McKinley, the President of the United States; which was responded to by the Minister Resident.

The Montgomery sailed on Wednesday morning, Oct. 18, by way of Cape Mount, for Freetown, Sierra Leone, to coal; thence back, to Cape Palmas, by way of Baffon Bay Liberia.

I have the honor to be
your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith,
M. R. and C. General.

Inclosure.
