Gaboon November 29th 1866

Gentlemen,

Your circular dated Nov 26th 1866, having been handed to me to-day I hasten to inform you that I take into deep consideration the general opinion which have dictated it, but persuaded as I am that the decisions of the Admiral Commanding in Chief have been inspired as much by the great interest which he takes in the countries of which the principal administration has been intrusted to him, as by a profound knowledge of the means of assuring their industrial and commercial development, it cannot enter into my mind to hesitate a moment in executing those decisions at the time appointed and to their full extent.

Never-the-less that period is far distant. Many serious reasons lead me to think that the Admiral may arrive before January 1st of next year, in which case you cannot doubt that I will lay your Circular beforehand and he himself will consider the observations which it contains, but are those observations sufficiently clearly expressed?

I do not think so. I believe that a serious discussion would put them in a better light, at the same time it would dissipate certain errors of political economy, certain impressions without any real foundation, which appear to me to be shown in the spirit of your letter.

Consequently I have the honor to request that you will call at the Government House on monday most at 2 P.M. Assisted by the administrative experience of the Ordennateur, inspired myself by the most earnest desire to reconcile your [?], which are in point of fact there of the Colony, with the strict execution of the orders of the Admiral Commanding in Chief perhaps I may be able to make you understand better both the ideas and the elevated motives which have dictated his decision.

Finally it is not to be doubted that from this discussion I shall obtain a deeper insight into your opinions, and that will aid me much in acting as your interpretors[?] with the Admiral Commanding in Chief.

Be pleased to accept Gentlemen, the assurance of my distinguished consideration,

Post Captain, officer of the legion of honor, Commandant of Gaboon.
Commandant Superior ad interior,
(Signed) Aube

To
The Gentleman signing the Circular forwarded to the Commandant Superior of Gaboon
