No. 9

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, La., Mar. 8, 1879.

Mr. J. H. Smyth to the Third Ass't Sec'y of State.

Subject:

Receipt of despatches.

Synopsis:

An acknowledgement of the receipt of two dispatches, one as to the appointment of a Consular Agent at Cape Coast Castle, and the other manner of heading Consular correspondence.

 

No. 9.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, La., March 8, 1879.

Sir:

I have the honor to advise the Department of the receipt of your Nos 2 & 3, dated, each, January 2nd, 1879; on the 7th of March, the mail having been sent to Palmas, the steamer not stopping at Monrovia.

At to your No. 2, I have to inform the Department that Mr. Hamel has gone to the Netherlands in consequence of severe illness.

As to No. 3, the instruction will be heeded.

I Hon. Chas. Payson,
Third Ass't Secretary of State
Washington, D.C.   I am, Sir,
With high consideration of esteem,
Your obd't serv't,
Jno. H. Smyth
