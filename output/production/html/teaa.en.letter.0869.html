Department of State Received Jan 29 1881

No 42. U.S. Consulate at Sierra Leone Dec 27th 1880

Judson A. Lewis To Dept State

Sending Two Destitute Am. Seamen Daniel Freethey & Walter Duval to Liverpool per S.S. Kinsembo.

 

No 42. U.S. Consulate at
Sierra Leone
Dec. 27th 1880.

Hon John Hay
Asst Secty of State
Washington D.C.

Sir

I have to inform you that I have this day sent to the Consu. Genl at Liverpool per S.S. Kinsembo two Destitute American Seamen viz Daniel Freethey and Walter Duval. Daniel Freethey came upon this Consulate from Grand Bassa Oct 25th as per my Dispatch No 35 and Walter Duval came upon this Consulate Dec. 11th both Deserters from Am. Bark Jennie R. Deverty. I intended to have sent them Both to Boston   per Bark Hazard D. A. Roberts Master - but said Master refused to take them as you will see by my Dispatch No. 41 Dec 24th The reason I now forward them by Steam to Liverpool is this - There is no American Vessel in Port - And I am not informed when one will arrive and I fear the men may get sick in this climate if detained for a long time & believe their chances for shipping at Liverpool are good I have given a Fifteen[?] days draft on Secty of State for 96 dollars being £20. for their passage payable to the order of Randall and Fisher Agents - which draft I trust will be duly honored.
