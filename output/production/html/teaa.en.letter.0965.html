C. No. 6.

Legation Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, La., Jan'y 16, 1879.

Mr. J. H. Smyth to the Secretary of State.

Subject:

Coffee pkg's sent.

Synopsis:

Explanation as to the two pkg's of coffee sent by me through B. F. Stearns, Esq., London to Dep't at Washington, D.C.

 

C. No. 6.

Legation of the United States,
Monrovia, La., Jan'y 16, 1879.

Sir:

I have the honor, in accordance with the request made in your No. 16, dated Nov. 27, 1878, to inform you that I sent two pkg's of coffee addressed to the Dep't through our London Ag't Mr. B. F. Stearns.

This coffee was specimen or a sample of the best growth and preparation for transportation of Liberian coffee; and as such I supposed there were to be no charges upon it.

One Hon. F. W. Seward,
Act'g Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.   One pk'g. was intended for the Hon. The Secretary of State, and I enclosed a prive note in an official envelope to him, and the other was for Col. Morgan, Mr. Morrison, a similar note being enclosed for them.

I am, Sir,
Your very obd't serv't,
Jno. H. Smyth
