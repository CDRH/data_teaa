Department of State Received Mar 14 1887

No. 55.

U.S. Consulate General,
Monrovia Feby 10./87

Mr. S. S. Sevier
To the Secretary of State.

Subject

Death of Miss Enice Knapp an American citizen.

Synopsis

Death of Miss Knapp a missionary of the Free Methodist Church–inventory of her personal property–her stated residence.

 

No. 55.

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia Feb'y 10/87

Hon. T. F. Bayard
Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

It becomes my painful duty to inform you that a missionary of the Free Methodist Church by the name of Miss Enice Knapp who had been only two months within the bounds of this Consulate working earnestly to spread the gospel of   of Jesus Christ in this land died on the 2nd of Feb'y from the contagion fever of this country. Inventory of her personal property has been taken and will be disposed off just as soon as I can obtain the right address of her relatives. The place of her birth is unknown to me. I am told by a distant friend that Mrs. Alford Wilkins #26 W. 38th Street New York City is her nearest known relative. The expenses of her funeral   funeral were settled without auctioning any of her effects according to the Consular Regulations. In inclose an inventory of her effects.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient servant
Sam'l S. Sevier
Acting U.S. Consul Gen.

Hon. T. F. Bayard
Secretary of State
Washington D.C.
