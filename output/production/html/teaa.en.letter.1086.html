Department of State Received Nov 13 1888
No. 7 Consulate General, U. S. A.
Monrovia, Liberia, Sept. 28th 1888 Mr. Ezekiel E. Smith
To the Department of State. SUBJECT:

Consular Agency of Elmina, Gold Coast Colony.

ABSTRACT OF CONTENTS.

Returns and accounts for the quarter ending, June 30th 1888.

Â  No. 7 Consulate General, U. S. A.
Monrovia, Liberia, Sept. 28th, 1888. Honorable George L. Rives, Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D. C. Sir:

I have the honor respectfully, after an unavoidable delay, to enclose you the quarterly returns of the Consular Agency at Elmina, Gold Coast Colony, for the quarter ended June 30th 1888.

These returns consist of the following enclosures: arrivals and departures of American vessels (1); detailed report of official services to American vessels and seamen necessarily rendered (2); and summary of Consular business (3).

I am, sir,
Your obedient servant.
Ezek. E. Smith.
U.S. Consul General.
