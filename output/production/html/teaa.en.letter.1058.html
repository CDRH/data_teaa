Department of State Received Jan 3 1888

U.S. Legation,
Monrovia, Liberia,
November 27th 1887.

Sir,

It becomes my painful duty to acknowledge the receipt of your communication of the 24th ultimo announcing the death of Honorable Elihu B. Washburne, who was at one time Secretary of State of the United States, with instruction to adopt the usual symbol of public mourning for a period of ten days as a testimony of respect to the memory of the deceased; which was immediately obeyed. I conveyed to the Government of Liberia through the State Department the bad[?] intelligence; and I am advised, by the Secretary of State, to convey to you, the sympathy of the Government of Â  of Liberia in the taking off of such an eminent Statesman; and with the Government of the United States in the loss it has sustained in the death of so prominent and honored citizen.

I have the honor to be
Sir,
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne
U.S. Acting Vice Consul
