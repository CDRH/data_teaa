Department of State Received Feb 18 1887

No. 45

Consulate General of the U.S.
Monrovia Dec. 31/86

Mr. S. S. Sevier
To the Ass't Sect'y of State

Subject

Funeral Expenses of the late Minster Moses A. Hopkins. Re-shipment of goods sent by Despatch Agent of New York.

Abstract of Contents.

Funeral Expenses–reshipment of goods–payment of G. T. Hoove & Son.

 

Consulate General of the U.S.
Monrovia Dec. 31/86

Hon. Jas D. Porter–
Ass't Sect'y of State,
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform you that your despatch No. 12 dated Sept. 24th 1886 authorizing me to dispose of the goods of the late Minister Moses A. Hopkins in order to pay his funeral expenses could not well be performed. G. T. Hoove & Son to whom the government is indebted for the funeral expenses would   would not take them to their account; nor could I dispose of them to her profit to any merchant here; therefore by her request and by my own judgement, I have shipped said goods back to her with the rest of afects the left of her husband to her home in Franklinton Franklin County N.C. I have also paid G. T Hoove & Son for funeral expenses.

Hoping to receive your approval for steps taken

I am Sir
Your obedient servant
Saml S. Sevier
Acting U.S. Consul Gen.
