Subject.
The Cape Coast Castle Agency.

 

No. 18. Consular Series
Legation of the United States
Monrovia Liberia,
Jan. 30 1899.

Hon. David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to transmit to you the intelligence, that in obedience to your No. 5 of July 15, 1898, demanding of me to give reasons why they Cape Coast Castle Agency, should be continued; and your No. 8, of Oct., 6, 1898, informing me that all correspondence, concerning said Agency, would be held in abeyance until the desired information, relative, to said Agency, shall have been furnished. In my replies, I have endeavored in No.   No. 2, of Aug., 29, 1898, to give, what then, appeared to be general reasons why said Agency should not be discontinued.

In my No. 28, of Sept. 22, 1898, I suggested, that should a United States Cruiser visit this coast, with instruction to take me to Cape Coast Castle, during the mouth of Dec., 1898, I would visit said Agency, during the stay of said Cruiser. In my No. 12, of Nov. 16, 1898, I assured you, that in case said Cruiser did not come, in December 1898, that I would visit said Agency early in 1899. Failing to receive a more direct order, to draw upon the Special or Emergency Fund and to visit said Agency, I have endeavored to obtain the desired information, by correspondence but   but having totally failed in my efforts; as per my letter of advice, relative to drafts of January 6, 1899, I did draw my draft, No. 21 for One Hundred, Twenty-five Dollars, upon the Secretary of State; on account of Special or Emergency Fund, preparatory to embarking upon the next English Steamer for Cape Coast Castle.

The following, may; therefore, inform you that I have this 30th day of January, 1899, left the Legation in the hands and care of the Secretary thereof, Hon. Jas. Robt. Spurgeon, and do embark for Cape Coast Castle.

I have the honor to be your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith,
M. R. and C. General.

 

Inclosure 1. Dispatch No. 19 of Jan. 30, 1899. 2. Advice as to Draft.
