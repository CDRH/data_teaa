No 7 Dispatch Consular Series,
Legation of the United States.
Monrovia Liberia December 13th 1890
To the Honorable William F. Wharton
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington DC

Sir;

I have the honor to respectfully acknowledge the recpt from the department of state the following viz.

1 copy diplomatic and consular series of the United States for October 20th 1890;

2 copies No 4 Series of 1890 Statement of Debt of the United States for the month of October 1890;

3 copies Hydrographic department of the Navy;

3 Copies United States coast and geodetic Survey No 133 to Marines for the month of October 1890

Inclosures   Inclosures

No 1 Needed Furniture and fixtures for the Legation

" 2. Inclosure Rain, Heat, Sickness &c

" 3 Inclosure

Most respectfully
Remain yr
obedient servant
Alexander Clark
Minister Resident & Consul General of United States

 

No 1 Inclosure needed furniture and fixtures for the Legation as follows,viz:

Sofa; Looking Glass; Washbole and pitcher; Carpet and rug; Lamp and buckets one small stove for a stove aught to be kept in the Legation room at times to keep the books and furniture dry and in good condition especially during the wet or rainey season, for all or nearly all of the houses are wet and damp during said season on my arrival at my post. I found the book and furniture in a bad condition as I mentioned my dispatch of the   ult I am doing the best I can to save and preserve the book by butting them out in the sun and changing the place in the room for the case & on taking change of the post I found none of the articles mentioned and was compeled to furnish[?] therefore[?] [unclear, three words]. please instruct me on the subject.

 

No 2 Inclosure

Rain-fall &c

on my arrival at the Legation November 17th 1890 - 6 AM - it was raining and to date of this dispatch I can say I think there has not been one 24 hours during these 27 days that there has not been a ra a rain shower or pour down but as to quantity I can say as there is no rain-gauge &c here in Liberia, nor Newspaper published to keep one posted. The weather has not been very hot but Sickley and a goodly number of funerals taken place during these 27 days Up the St Paul River some 30 miles from Monrovia there is a case of small pox at one of the missions viz

I remain your obedient
servant Alexander Clark USM

 

No 3 Inclosure

Please instruct me concerning my duty with reference to the answering letters from buisness firms or others &c a sample of some reccord[?] find attached to this inclosure viz:

John Wanamaker &c

No 4 Please Instruct me with your desired form that my dispatch should be headed and send form to guide or govern me &c and should all my Inclosures be signed officially as same as this dispatches containing said Inclosures as there is no forms in Legation.

I am your
Obedient servant
Alexander Clark M. Resident & consul general
