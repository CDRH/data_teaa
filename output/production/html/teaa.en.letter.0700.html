No 12

U.S. Consulate at Sierra Leone
June 17th 1879

Hon F. W. Seward
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir,

I beg to inform the Department that my attention has been called to an article in the London Times, May 23rd, stateing that the President has seen fit to appoint one of the Clerks of an American house at this port. (a Mr Lewis) U.S. Consul &c. I am not surprise at the appointment of Mr Lewis; as I am quite aware of the repeated Efforts made by him & his friends to procure his Appointment by writing gross falsehoods to the Department detremental to me, as was shown in a dispatch from the war department to Commodore Shufeldt requesting him to investigate the same on his arrival here, at which investigation said charges were proven to be false; but I am surprise to know that a Government as great as the United States would remove an Officer from his post by appointing another, without a charge or sufficient known cause Common courtesy at lease, I think, is due a servant of any Government let him be black or white.

I am Sir
Your obedient Servant
Jno. A. Parm M.D.
