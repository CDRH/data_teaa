Subject.

Accident in the midst of National Celebration.

Synopsis.

The firing of canon, shakes down the gable of Trinity Chapel Church

 

No. 13, Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia,
Dec. 3, 1898.

Hon. David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to transmit to you, the intelligence, that during the annual celebration of the first day of December, by the Liberians; in commemoration of the decisive battle, and victory of the Liberians, over the aboriginal inhabitants and native tribes of Cape Montsurrado; the present site of Monrovia, on December 1, 1822; while firing the National salute, December 1, 1898, from guns situated on the   the summit of Montsurrado, the concussion of the guns caused the east gable of Trinity Chapel, Protestant Episcopal Mission, situated, at the base of the mountains, to fall in; greatly demolishing the Altar, reading stand, organ, chandelier and other furniture! Fortunately, no person was in the building, at the time, as the National ceremonies; the President, cabinet officers, and Foreign Representatives, were, at the time, being conducted in the Methodist Episcopal Church.

I have the honor to be your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith,
Consul General.

Inclosure. The assemblying of the Liberian Legislature.
