Gaboon Nov. 16th 1866

We, Rear Admiral, Commander-in-Chief of the Naval Division of the West Coast of Africa; Chief Commandant of the Settlements of the Gold Coast and the Gaboon.

By reason of the new commercial regime which is about to be established in the waters of the Gaboon.

Have decreed and do decree.

Art 1st The provisions of the Commercial Code relative to the keeping of accounts and to declarations of Bankruptcy will be put in force from January 1st 1867. Commencing on the same date with all individuals entering into commercial transactions and who make such their habitual profession will be held to be traders.

Art 2d Every person, French, foreigner, or native following the profession of traders within the radius of the French professions at Gaboon or who may intend to follow it, must make   his declaration to the Commandant "Particuliar" and to the "Ordonnateur" officiating as Director of the Interior, between this day and the 20th of December 1866; and later, ten days before commencing business. The "Ordonnateur" is charged with keeping a register of the said declarations.

Art. 3d Each merchant established at Gaboon, or who wishes to establish himself there, must provide himself within the delays and periods aforesaid.

1stly With a day-book in which must be entered day by day all his business transactions, his account current, and generally all that he may have received or paid on any account whatever. This day-book must moreover show month by month the amount disbursed for house expenses.

2dly With a Letter book into which month be copied all the letters sent by the merchant concerning his business affairs.

3dly With a stock-book, in which must be entered each year the Inventory of his property, both goods and effects not of his   account current. These books must be posted according to date without blanks, hiatus or transferences to margin. They must be preserved by the Merchant for ten years.

The Day-book and Stock-book must be paged, paragraphed and signed between the 10th and 25th of December of each year by the Commandant Particular who officiates at Gaboon as Justice of the Peace and as Mayor. This Magistrate will keep a register of the accomplishment of this formality.

Finally every Trader must preserve the letters which he may receive concerning his bills received and bills payable and other business operations.

Art. 4th Commencing with January 1st 1867. Bankruptcy will be declared by the Commandant Particulier upon the advice of the Commercial Committee established by Decree of Baron Didelot, Commandant Superior, dated March 28th 1862, approved by ministerial despatch of Febr'y 9th 1863 (Colonies 3d[?] Bureau[?])

 

Art. 5th of the Commercial Committee in verifying the Books of a Merchant, should conceive doubts as to the nature of the trust given to natives, it may appoint one of its members to clear up the matter. The Commandant Particulier will preside at the debates which may arise from these enquires and shall decide in the first instance upon the reality of the debt, according to the latitude allowed him by the local ordenances or decrees; the right of petition before the tribunals of St. Louis or Goree remaining always open.

Art. 6th At the end of the year the Commercial Committee meeting in the absence of a Chamber of Commerce shall draw up a report in duplicate, to be laid before us, upon the importance of the transactions which have been carried on in the waters of the Gaboon.

Art 7th The Commandant Particulier and the Ordonnateur officiating as Director of the Interior are intrusted, each in that which concerns him   with the execution of the present decree which shall be registered whenever it may be necessary and brought to the knowledge of the merchant and trader and of all the Gaboon chiefs in relation with us.

Art. 8th The tariff of duties on the entry of merchandise and the shipment of produce, already sent for the approbation of His Excellency the Minister of Marine and the Colonies will be notified by as later to the public.

Art 9th All previous enactments contrary to the present decree are and shall remain repealed.

The Rear Admiral Commander in Chief Commandant Superior of the French settlements on the Gold Coast and at Gaboon.

(signed) Vte A. de Langle
