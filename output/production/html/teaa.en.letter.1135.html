Honorable William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir;

I have the honor respectfully to acknowledge the receipt of your despatch, No. 11. of January 8th 1890, replying to my No. 40. of November 5th in relation to the Agencies at Cape Palmas and Grand Bassa.

I shall, at the earliest moment possible, comply with the instructions therein contained. The steamers call here once in two weeks. Occasionally one calls during this interval; but those which call here rarely stop at Grand Bassa; and, only occasionally, at Cape Palmas.

I am, sir,
your obedient servant,
Ezekiel E Smith,
U.S. Consul General
