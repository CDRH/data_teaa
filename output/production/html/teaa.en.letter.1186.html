No. 8. Consulate of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, July 6, 1892.

To the Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to submit the enclosed letter from the Thurber, Whyland Company, together with my reply.

Should the reply meet your approval please forward the same to the firm, and oblige.

Your obedient servant,

William D. McCoy,
Minister-Resident and Consul General.

New York, May 27, 1892.

Hon. Wm. D. McCoy,
Consul-General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, Africa.

Dear Sir:-

If not asking too much of you, we will be pleased to have you send us a list of the provision merchants in Monrovia, as we are desirous of working up more trade in this section.

We are now shipping goods to Messrs Henry Cooper & Son, and J. C. Dickinson, and taking their coffee in exchange. We believe a larger business can be done with Africa, and therefore ask your help as above. The writer wrote to your predecessor a long time ago in regard to this but has never received any reply.

If we can be of any service to you here in any way at any time kindly command me. Trusting   Trusting you will grant our request and that you are enjoying good health. I have the honor to remain, sir, with great respect,

Yours faithfully,
Albert W. Minick
