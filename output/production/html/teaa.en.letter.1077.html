Department of State Received Jun 27 1888

Franklinton N.C.
June 26/88

Hon. G. L. Rives
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington D.C.

Sir:

Your letter of May 26th 1888, came to hand when I was from home; therefore I could not reply. I now take this opportunity to say that it is impossible for me to send in to the State Department proper voucher for messenger service rendered the U.S. Legation at Monrovia Liberia for the amount of $75.50/100 because I cannot get said proper voucher from the messenger, for I dont know where he is. And Â  again, Sir, if I could get a voucher it would not be a proper one; because the messenger cannot write his Name. He was a native man of the usual[?] land, one who could hardly speak the English language.

Sir, please deal with me kindly and let me have my money ($75 50/100) for I am in need of it. I am willing to take oath on the fate of my life that I paid the messenger of the U.S. Legation at Monrovia Liberia that money. Please give me justice. I know you will do what is right and just. Hoping to hear from you soon.

I remain, Sir,
Yours with highest esteem
Sam'l S. Sevier
