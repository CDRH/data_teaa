Mr. Ezekiel E. Smith
To the Department of State

Subject:

Archives of the Consular Agencies at Grand Bassa and Cape Palmas.

 

No. 56.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, March 10th 1890.

Honorable William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to inform you that, in accordance with the instructions contained in your dispatch No 11. of January 8th 1890, I proceeded to Cape Palmas per S.S. "Ella Woermann", leaving here for that point on the evening of the 20th ult. and arriving there on the afternoon of the 22nd.

Upon looking around and making inquiry, I ascertained there was no American commercial interest operated at Cape Palmas. Only one American vessel had touched at that point during the past three or four years, and it called in December 1886 to land emigrants. There   There are, however, a number of missionaries, at Cape Palmas, from the United States, who, under the direction of Bishop William Taylor, are laboring with commendable zeal to christianize and ameliorate the native African in that section of Liberia.

I collected the archives of the former United States' Agency at Cape Palmas and shipped them to this office. Of which I enclose an inventory.

On the evening of the 24th per S.S. "Gertrude Woermann", I proceeded to Grand Bassa, arriving there on the morning of the 27th.

Upon observation and inquiry I ascertained that there was no American shipping or commercial interest conducted at Grand Bassa, consequently, I had shipped to this office the archives and furniture of the former Consulate at that place.

Please find enclosed inventory of said   said office.

Returning, I arrived at my post of duty here on the evening of the 28th ultimo. I was absent eight days, leaving the Consulate General in the care of Vice Consul General.

I beg, also, to enclose a statement, with vouchers, of the actual and necessary expenses incurred on the trip. I have this day drawn draft for said amount.

Hoping the same may meet the approval of the Department.

I am, sir,
your obedient servant,
Ezekiel E. Smith,
U.S. Consul General.
