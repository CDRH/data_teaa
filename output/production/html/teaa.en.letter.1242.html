To the Department of State.

Subject

Relief and Shipment of American Seamen

 

Legation of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia. April 18, 1898

To the Hon. Auditor For State and Other Departments,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform you that I have relieved the following seamen of the schooner "Alice" of Provincetown, Mass. which was destroyed by fire, in this port, on the 4th instant; Geo. E. Rich, Edward L. Doyle, St. Augustine Bravo, Peter Scott and August Britt, all citizens of Massachusetts, U.S.A. and Joseph Santo Estrelinha, Peter Manuel, Theophilo Pereira, Manuel de Reis, Manuel Consalue, Joaquin Lopes, Jordan Lopes, and Morral Gonsalina: all these of Bravo, Cape Verd Island. They were rlieved according to Consular Regulations 1896. Paragraph 271. and shipped to Freetown Sierra Leone under Paragraph 287   same book. I had to negotiate a draft drawn on the Secretary of the Treasury for ($258.54) two hundred and fifty eight dollars and fifty four cents. There are no hospitals in Monrovia, therefore I were oblige to have private physicians. Most of the seamen had African fever and Captain J. H. Chase died from the effects of the same. The seamen were sent to Sierra Leone that they might secure passage on an American Vessel from there; and the mode of living is better, and the price cheaper there than here, besides there are hospitals there for treatment of the sick.

I have the honor to be very obediently, William H. Heard
Minister Res. and Consul General
