June 30th 1887

Chas. H. J. Taylor to Hon. J. D. Porter

Subject:

Quarterly report 2nd qr June 30th 87

Enclosures

1. Record of treasury fees
2. Arrival & Departures of A'a Vessels
3. Digest of the invoice book
4. Gen'l quarterly account
5. Contingent acc't for qe end'g June 3d -87

 

No 5. U.S. Consulate
Monrovia Liberia
June 30th 1887

Hon Jas. D. Porter,
Ass't Sec. of State, Washington D.C.

Sir:

Please find enclosed my report, for the 2nd quarter ending June 30th 1887. I am unable to furnish the Department with vouchers, for expenses incurred, on acc't of contingent allowance, as shown in enclosure No 5, The only item for which I can be held accountable is the Postage for the quarter the remainder of the account belongs to Mr S. S. Sevier. My Salary account will be found in enclosure 4, which I hope is correct. I   would most respectfully call your attention to enclosures, 3, 2 and 1, containing digest of the invoice book, arrival and departure of American vessels and the acc't of treasury fees rec'd at this consulate. Mr S. S. Sevier, was unable to account to me for the $22.00/100 rec'd by him, as Treasury fees, during this quarter. Trusting that this account may be easily understood, I am your obediant servant &c., &c.,
Chas. H. J. Taylor
U.S. Consul Gen'l
