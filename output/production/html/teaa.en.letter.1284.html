No. 45, Consular Series,
Legation of the United States
Monrovia, Liberia, Apr. 20, 1900.

Mr. James Robert Spurgeon,
To the Department of State.

Subject.

Marking of the wrecked Gunboat in the Monrovian Harbor.

Synopsis.

British Gunboat Widgun visits the ports of Monrovia and Cape Palmas and marks the wreck and sunken rock in the harbors respectively.

 

No. 45, Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia Apr. 20, 1900.

Honorable David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform you that on the 3rd of April, 1900. Her Majesty's Ship "Widgun" of the British Navy anchored in the harbor of this port, for the purpose of marking the wreck of the Liberian Gunboat Rocktown (See dispatch No. 77, Diplomatic of March 12, 1900) that evidence of said wreck may appear in the British Admiralty Charts. Her Majesty's Ship remained here two or three days, then sailed for Cape Palmas, Liberia, where she is to mark a sunken rock in the harbor of that port.

I have the honor to be, Sir,
Your obed't servant,
James Robert Spurgeon
U.S. Chargé.
