Subject:

Receipt of Despatches and enclosure.

 

20

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, August 22, 1891.

Hon., William F. Wharton,
Acting Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to acknowledge receipt of your Nos 10, & 11, of 18th & 21st ultimo: the latter enclosing copy of a letter from Rebecca J. Appleton, and other of the family of Mr Clark, late Minister of the United States at Monrovia.

Your instruction to take the necessary steps to cause the transportation of the remains of the deceased Minister, to Muscatine, Iowa, U.S.A, will be carried out as soon as it is safe to do so. There being no facility of embalmment in Liberia, the remains were interred without, and cannot be exhumed for transportation immediately, but   but may be in three or four months hence.

I thank you for your advice in reference to the shipment of Mr Clark's personal affects, and expense incurred thereby. I had acted in accordance with same prior to its receipt as per enclosed copy of my letter to Mr. George W. Appleton, of the 20th ultimo.

By the last mail I received a certificate of his appointment and commission, Administrator of the Estate of Minister Clark deceased.

I have the honor to be,
Sir:
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne
U.S. Vice Consul General.

Enclosure
1. Copy of Mr Payne's to Mr Appleton, July 20th 1891.

 

Consulate General of the United States.
Monrovia, Liberia, July 20, 1891.

Mr George W. Appleton.
Muscatine, Iowa.

Dear Sir:

I have written you several letters since the death of your Father, and I am without any in reply. This serves to advise that I shipped all his personal affects to you on the 16th inst., per Bark "Liberia" in care of Messrs Yates & Porterfield, 19, William Street, New York City, to whom you will make application for same.

I enclose a copy of Inventory and two keys to trunks, which I trust you will find in order. I also enclose six family letters, three of which I did not recognize to be from his relatives, hence I opened them. I shall forward the Account at earliest opportunity.

I remain, Your ob't serv't
Beverly Y. Payne
U.S. Vice Consul Genl.
