No. 24.

Consulate Gen'l of the U.S.,
Monrovia, La, July 15, 1879.

Mr. J. H. Smyth to the Sc'd Ass't Sec'y, State.

Subject:

Arrival of the Monrovia.

Synopsis:

The Monrovia - a bark - out 31 days, from N.Y. with 47 passengers, immigrants, officers & crew expected.

 

No. 24.

Consulate Gen'l of the U.S.,
Monrovia, La., July 15, 1879.

Sir:

I have the honor to repost the arrival off Monrovia, on the night of July 14, of the bark Monrovia from N.Y., and on the morning of the 15th, in harbor.

Forty seven immigrants were reported to have arrived–all of whom are well.

These immigrants having arrived sooner by one week than they were expected, they may suffer Hon. W. Hunter,
Second Asst Secy of State,
Washington, D.C.   suffer for want of comfortable quarters, especially as this is winter–or the rainy season.

I am, Sir,
Your obd't serv't,
Jno. H. Smyth,
U.S. Consul Gen'l.
