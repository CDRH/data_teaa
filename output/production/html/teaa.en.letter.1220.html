Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, August 23, 1894.

Mr Beverly Y. Payne,
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Receipt of Despatch.

 

No 41.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, August 23, 1894.

Honorable Edwin F. Uhl.
Assistant Sec'y of State.
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge receipt of your No 9. of 9th ult., enclosing a copy of Circular of September 19, 1893, calling my attention to the transmission of Consular Agent's return at Elmina, and inquiring the exact location of said Consular Agency. I beg to say in reply that it is at Elmina, being the residence of the Consular Agent, but Cape Coast Castle is the principle sea port town   town in that vacinity.

I have transmitted to the Consular Agent there a copy of the Circular referred to and I am of the opinion it will be observed in future.

I have the honor to be
Sir:
Your obedient servant
Beverly Y. Payne,
U.S. V. Consul.
