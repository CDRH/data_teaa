No. 43.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, November 22nd 1889.

Mr. Ezekiel E. Smith
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Public Debt, Currency, Receipts and Expenditures of Liberia.

 

No. 43.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, November 22nd 1889.

Honorable William F Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to transmit herewith a meager statement concerning the public debt, currency, receipts, and expenditures of Liberia, as furnished this Consulate General by the Secretary of State of Liberia.

The Circular was handed said officer on the 17th of September; and, at the time, a request was made that the information desired be furnished as soon as practicable. The gentleman's attention having been repeatedly called to the same, it was returned to this office on the 17th inst with the within enclosure.

I am, sir, your obedient servant,
Ezekiel E. Smith.
U.S. Consul General.

Enclosures, two.

1. Statement concerning the Public Debt &c
2. Mr Barclay to Mr. Smith (copy)

 

Department of State,
Monrovia, November 16th 1889

Sir.

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your No. 9 of the 12th inst and to enclose herewith the Circular referred to in said communication.

2. I am informed by the Department of the Treasury that by reason of unavoidable circumstances, it is presently impossible to render the statement of receipts, expenditures, interest, charges &c encouched in the said Circular.

I have the honor to be,
sir,
Your obedient servant,
(Signed) E. J. Barclay

Hon E. E. Smith
U.S. Minister Resident and Consul General,
Monrovia.
