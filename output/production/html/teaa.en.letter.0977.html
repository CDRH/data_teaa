No. 18.

Consulate Gen'l of the United States,
Monrovia, La., May 10, 1879.

Mr. J. H. Smyth to the Ass't Sec'y of State.

Subject:

Receipt of dispatch.

Synopsis:

As to Act approved June 4, 1878, making appropriations for consular and diplomatic service of the U.S., &c., &c., &c.

 

No. 18.

Consulate Gen'l of the United States,
Monrovia, La., May 10, 1879.

Sir:

Your circular dispatch dated Mar. 8, 1879, calling attention to the consular and diplomatic Act of June 4, 1878, has been received, and in reply, I have to state as follows:

That I shall furnish the Sec'y of the Treasury, as soon as I shall be able to obtain them, the prices current of "all articles of merchandise, usually[?] exported to the U.S. from my Hon. F. W. Seward,
Ass't Sec'y of State
Washington, D.C.   my district.

To the appraising officers, I will also send as directed.

There are not any publications of prices current, made by the merchants of this district, but by inquiry, I shall be able to learn them as they relate to the general articles of merchandise exported.

I am, Sir,
Your obedient servant
Jno. H. Smyth,
U.S. Consul Gen'l.
