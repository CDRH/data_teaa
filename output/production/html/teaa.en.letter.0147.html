No 12 6.

United States Consulate
St Paul de Loanda
June 30th 1882.

The Hon. J. C. Bancroft Davis
Asst. Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir

Since the death of General Dantas Govr Genl of Angola and the provinces, "Joseph," Lord Bishop of Angola, has been the acting governor and President of the Council composed of four; Two british gun boats and a corvette left here for the Cape of Good Hope, St Helena, and Sth America. The Irish Earl of Mayo with two others, left for Mossamedes and "Huïlla" the new "Boër" settlement in the Portuguese possession of the South West Coast of Africa. on a supposed shooting and british geographical expedition; We have now in Port, one frigate two corvettes and a Portuguese gun boat the latter will leave for the Congo on the 5th of July. The city of Loanda is for the present in a very sickly state, and the billious fever now existing is very dangerous for Europeans who have resided here for a few years, The change of air further North or South is highly recommanded by the principal physicians of this place. The living in St Paul de Loanda is very high, and a man; particularly a consul here with only $1000. a year, can only prevent himself from starving, as the agency attached to this consulate and mentioned by Mr Swellon A. Brown does not exist any more.

Kerosene oil, American lumber of all sorts, Naval stores, Lard, Butter; cans stores, Hams Bacon, salted beef and Pork in half Barrels cheap Furniture, good flour in half barrels are now in great demand; I believe that a vessel of 200 to 250 tons with the above mentioned articles would realize a very good profit and find a return cargo.

The Portuguese Govt. have in custody a man named J. B. Silveira citizen born in the U.S. and who was once U.S. Consul at Lisbon, as soon as I will be able to find out more about him and his case will notify your Departt - In Loango there is two Americans traders Messrs Parks and Marsins, the latter a Sea Captn who lost his vessel at that place under very suspicious circumstances, I am told that in "Cabinda" Benguela, Mossamedes and four other small places on the coast a few americans are now trading, and that great many americans Whalers call at thoses places for provisions &a and in many instances, the crew are put on shore if they do not suit, or in most cases run away from their vessel for ill treatment; the consequences are; that they generally come upon this consulate for support as destitute steamer.

I would respectfully suggest that I may be authorized to appoint Consular agents all over the coast from Gaboon, as far as Great Fish bay, beside the Islands of Fernando Po, Princess, St Thomé & Annaboa[?]. I have also applied to the Medical and other authorities to give me if possible a weekly return of sickness and diseases existing, beside the list of import and export to this port, also to gentlemen in Benguela Mossamedes, Bengo, Ambriz and other ports for a plain statement of their their Coffee, Cocoa, Sugar, Rum and Cotton factories.

If the State Department would allow me to call at those places once and while, I believe that I would be able to give you and your Departt a full and correct statement of everything of interest and valuable to the United States Government and its different Departments beside promoting its commerce

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient servant
L. de R. du Verge
U.S. Consul
