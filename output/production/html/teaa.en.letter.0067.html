United States Commercial Agency
St Paul de Loanda October 22 1865

Sir;

On the 29th ult José Baptista de Andrade who had been Governor General of the Province for the past three years delivered over to the Government Council, the temporary charge of this Government, until his successor should arrive out; and sailed for Lisbon, on board of the Steamer "Lincolnshire" on the 3d inst.

Three years ago this Province was in a most deplorable state, the consequence of two years war with the Native tribes on the frontier. The distance from Loanda to that locality was about three hundred miles, making it extremely difficult to transport munitions of war, and the sickly climate, made it absolutely impossible Hon William H. Seward
Secretary of State
Washington. D.C.   impossible to preserve good, disciplined, [?] troops to force these tribes into subjection. Overwhelming numbers of Natives seriously threatened the safety of what few troops were sent there and on two occasions were defeated by them, in a general engagement. During this state of affairs, the Commerce of the Provence decreased very much indeed, and at one time almost stopped. The then acting Governor General (Calheiros) was seriously cautioned by the Public and charged with imprudence and misjudgement in allowing the war to reach such a climax when it was not in his power to meet the emergencies of the moment. His Majesty the King of Portugal wisely appointed Captain José Baptista de Andrade of the Portuguese Navy to relieve him; He arrived here in September 1862. Governor Andrade instituted the Olive Branch policy at the commencement of his administration, and with a   a firmness of action, worthy of the highest praise he was enabled, during his term of service to accomplish more real good than a force of 20,000 men could have done. He brought the Native chiefs to his feet imploring pardon. Confidence was restored, Commerce blossomed afresh & happiness substituted misery. He established for himself a name that many would envy. He left here deeply regretted by all. I had the honor of officially congratulating him, on his successful administration. He was highly esteemed by all loyal Americans, on this Coast, for his devotion to the Federal cause & during the darkest days of the Rebellion was always ready to [?] us all and to predict the final inglorious of defeat of the Confederates and when this took place, no foreigner rejoiced more than he did.
