No. 55.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, February 17th 1890.

Honorable William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir;

I have the honor respectfully to acknowledge the receipt of your No. 12. despatch of January the 8th, informing me that the despatch paper requested in my No. 42. of November 11th 1889, had been ordered to be sent.

The supplies, consisting of flag, record book etc, previously forwarded, came per S.S. "Benin" which reached here on yesterday. I received Bill of lading for the same, and sent aboard the steamer for the case, but the officers on the steamer failed to find it though they turned about and moved hundreds of other cases in search for it. I hope to receive it, however, on the homeward voyage of the steamer.

I am, sir, your obedient servant,
Ezekiel E. Smith.
U.S. Consul General.
