Received Dec 28 1866
No 1.

Commercial Agency of the United States of America at Sierra Leone
19th November 1866.

Sir,

Having by a dispatch, No 1, and dated May 24, from the Department of State, received a commission as Commercial Agent of the United States for Sierra Leone and the dependencies thereof, I beg to thank the Department and will to the best of my ability endeavor to carry out its instructions as received from time to time.

I called upon, and acquainted His Excellency Colonel Samuel W. Blackall, the Governor in Chief of my appointment F. W. Seward Esq
Assistant Secretary
&c & &c   appointment and at the same time presented him with my commission to examine, he appeared not to perfectly understand the office and desired that he might correspond with the home Government before officially recognizing of course I could not refuse.

Therefore as it is of great importance that the Consular officer of the United States in this Colony should be fully recognized on account of out commercial interests and especially as there is a large British Dependency, I beg to request that the Department will apply for an exequatur for me from the British Government.

The oath is enclosed, I have sworn to it before a Police Magistrate in this place   place, the bond I have signed before two witnesses here and enclosed to T. A. Sweetser Esq of South Danvers Mass, to obtain sureties for me after which he will forward it to the Department of State.

The Consuls Manual, list of Diplomatic and Consular officers and several of the Circulars issued by the Department are also duly received.

I was born in Salem Massachusetts and was appointed from the same place.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient Servant
Henry Rider
