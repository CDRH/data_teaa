No. 31
United States Consulate
Sierra Leone, 26th July 1878

Sir,

I have the honor to report for the information of the Department of State that I have received the enclosed letter from a Doctor Parm, who I am informed is a Liberian medical practitioner recently established here. It being difficult to do justice to Dr Parm's letter by either a copy or a description I forward the original and request to be favored with your instructions pending which I shall continue to perform the functions intrusted to me and to retain possession of the Consular archives

I have the honor to remain
Sir
Your obedient Servant
J Broadhurst
Acting for U.S. Consul

His Excellency
The Honble F. W. Seward
 
Copy

United States Consulate
Sierra Leone 26th July 1878

Sir,

I am in receipt of your communication dated 25th inst in which you inform me that the President of the United States has appointed you Consul for this place and request me to administer to you the Oath of Allegiance and Office and to deliver up to you within 30 days the Archives of Consulate

In reply I have the honor to inform you that I have not received my official intimation of your appointment and that by this mail I am sending your letter to the Department of State asking for instructions - meanwhile I must decline to administer the oath of office and allegiance or to transfer the property of the Consulate to your keeping

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient servant
(Sd) J. Broadhurst
Acting for U.S. Consul

[?] A. Parm Esq M.D.
&c. &c. &c.
 

Sierra Leone
July 25th 1878

Hon J. Broadhurst
Act U.S. Consul &c

Sir-

I have the honour to inform you that His Excellency the President of the U.S.A. has appointed one U.S. Consul for this port.

I will be pleased (if it Suits your convenience) to call at the consulate tomorrow at 10 O'clock A.M. to have the Oath of Office and Allegiance Administered, as I am instructed to do so at once & report the same to the State   department.

I have also to request that you will within thirty days from date deliver to me all the books papers &c belonging to the Office of U.S.A. Consulate, that are in your possession with an inventory of same.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient Servant
Jno A. Parm M.D.
&c &c
