Legation of the United States
Monrovia Liberia March 16, 1896.

Hon Auditor for State and other Departments
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to enclose the agreement between Vice Consul Beverly Y. Payne and myself.

You will see by this agreement his is permitted to draw the salary allowed him in my absence.

I am obediently
William H. Heard
Minister Res. & Con. Gen.

1 Enclosure
Agreement of Vice Consul Col B. Y. Payne

 

The following Agreement is this day entered into between William H. Heard, United States Minister Resident and Consul General at Monrovia, Republic of Liberia, of the First Part, and Beverly Y. Payne, United States Vice Consul General at Monrovia, Liberia, of the Second Part.

The Party of the First Part, being in bad health, and having obtained a Sixty (60) days Leave of absence, subject to the usual Statutory conditions to visit the United States, does on the day and date hereinafter mentioned, turn over the keys of the United States Legation and Consulate General at Monrovia, Liberia, with flags, arms, archives and furniture belonging thereto, to the Party of the Second part, who, for and in consideration of the sum of Fifty Dollars ($50.00) per month to be drawn quarterly from the Secretary of State at Washington, D.C. United States of America by the Party of the Second part, does promise and agree to take charge of the said United States Legation and Consulate General at Monrovia Liberia, and keep safe and secure all flags, arms, archives and furniture thereunto belonging, during the period embraced in the above mentioned Leave of absence, and transit of the Party of the First part in   accordance with the Consular Regulations governing Leaves of absence.

In witness whereof, the said Parties of the First and Second parts do hereunto subscribe their names and affix the seal of the Consulate General at Monrovia, Liberia this 16 day of March, A.D. 1896.

William H. Heard
Minister Res and Con General

Beverly Y. Payne.
Vice Consul General.
