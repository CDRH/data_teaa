No 3.

U.S. Consulate Sierra Leone
Sept. 22nd 1879

J. A. Lewis To Dept of State

Subject: Circulars No 56. 1865 and No 6. 1876

Abstract of Contents
Is Circular No 56 dated March 15th 1865 and Circular No 6 dated August 22nd 1876 in full force at the present time?

Can half a Dozen different articles be placed upon one Debenture Certificate?

No 3.

U.S. Consulate at
Sierra Leone Sept 22nd 1879

Hon F. W. Seward
Asst. Secty of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have to inform you that upon looking over the Archives of this Consulate I find two Circulars from the Department which have not been complied with to my personal Knowledge during the past year. and I would respectfully enquire if both of said Circulars are now in full force.

The first is Circular No 56 dated March 15th 1865 regarding Passports for all passengers going from a foreign port to the United States

The second is Circular No 64[?] dated August 22nd 1876 with Executive Order attached dated August 8th 1876 and related to to Debenture Certificates revising the Consular Regulations of 1874 and changing the Consular Fee on Debenture Certificates.

I would also respectfully enquire if half a dozen different articles can be placed upon one Debenture Certificate? Thereby reducing the Consular Fees to the minimum.

I am Sir Your Obt Servt
J. A. Lewis
U.S. Consul
