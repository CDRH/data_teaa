Subject.

Relative to estimates of cost of transportation of the 4 Todd children from Monrovia to New York.

Synopsis.

Have transmitted the desired information to Hon. James Boyle, L.pool, with request to answer.

 

No. 15 Consular Series.
Consulate General of the
United States, Monrovia,
Dec., 21, 1898.

Hon. Alvey A. Adee,
2nd Assistant Secretary,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your No. 9 of Nov. 1, 1898, in reply to my No. 4 Consul Series of Oct., 19, 1898, relative to the 4 indigent Todd children. In your No. 9 of Nov. 1, 1898, you suggest that "it would have facilitated matters, had I submitted an estimate of the lowest probable cast of sending the four children from Monrovia to New York."

I have to inform you that,   that I have dispatched the matter of the desired information to the Hon., Jas. Boyle, United States Consul at Liverpool; asking him to ascertain the facts of the lowest probable cost, from the Home offices of the several Steamship Companies, plying between Monrovia, Liverpool and New York, and to so inform the Department at Washington. Reminding him, however, that the Department of State, assumed no responsibility in the matter.

I have the honor to be your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith,
Consul General.

Enclosure. Acknowlegement of receipts Commission V. C. G. B. Y. Payne.
