Department of State Received Nov 28 1885

United States Consulate
St Paul de Loanda
12th October 1885

Sir

I have the honor to transmit to the Department a translation of a Despatch received from the Governor of the Island of Saint Thomas &c in which he advises having claimed a Protectorate over the Coast of Dahomey and other dominions on the West Coast of Africa, and have the honor to further transmit my reply to the same.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your most obedient servant,
Wm. S. Bent
Acting Vice Consul

To the
Secretary of State
Washington D.C.
 
Translation

St Thomé

Sir

I have the honor to inform you that in conformance with the express declaration in Art: 34 of Cap: VI of the act of the Berlin Conference made and signed on 26th February of the current year that in virtue of the treaty made between His Most Faithful Majesty the King of Portugal and His Majesty the King of Dahomey which was made and signed at the Palace of Aguauzum[?] in the district of Gebê on the 5th of August last by the respective representatives   representatives Dr Bernado Meirelles heite[?], magistrate and district judge and Prince Carhandu, heir presumptive to the throne of Dahomey who were furnished with the proper authority to complete the treaty, I have in the name of His Most Faithful Majesty with all formalities and by military occupation assumed the protectorate of all the coast line of the kingdom of Dahomey, comprehending the towns of Cátánum to the east, Godomey, N'véréquétá and Ajudá, intermediate districts of the Pescaria (Boca do Rio) to the west to remain under the protection of the Portuguese nation as well as taking possession of the territory and port of the district of Lomei to   to the west of the town of Ajudá and of the port of Adra, flying the Portuguese flag as exterior signal of the said protectorate (unless the said august sovereign shall resolve otherwise) in the towns of Cátánum and Ajudá, district of the Pescaria and the shore of Lomei

I have further to inform you that in virtue of the said treaty that the Government of Dahomey cannot at anytime make any treaty with respect to the country under the Protectorate without the intervention and consent of the Portuguese Government. And finally I have to inform you that in accordance with Art 35 of Cap VI of the said act to the Berlin Conference   Conference, they have been provisionally named delegates of His Most Faithful Majesty's Government in all the territory of the Portuguese Protectorate on the coast of the Kingdom of Dahomey the honorary Lieutenant Colonel Julias Felix de Souza, and in the Portuguese territory of Lomai, Major Domingues Cortez da Silva Curado.

God Guard
&c &c &c

Head Quarters of the Government of the province of St Thomé & Principe and their dependencies and the the office of the Secretary of the Portuguese Protectorate on the coast of the kingdom of Dahomey at Adjudá 14th September 1885

(Signed) Custodio Miguel de Borja Capitão Tenente of the Portuguese Navy, and Governor of St Thomé and Principe and their dependencies

To the United States Consul at Loanda
