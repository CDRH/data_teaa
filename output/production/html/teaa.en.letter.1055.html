Abstract of Contents:

Transmitting acc't and returns for quar. ended September 30th 1887

 

No 10.

Consulate Gen'l of the United States
Monrovia Liberia Sept 30th 1887

Honorable James. D. Porter
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to enclose the accounts and returns of this office for the quarter just ended as per the subjoined link of enclosures. The arrival and departure of American vessels, for the quarter is not reported owing to the fact, that only one American vessel–the Monrovia has arrived here, during the quarter, and as said vessel is still on the Liberian Coast, the report of arrivals and departures of American vessels will include two quarters, the 3rd and 4th and be stated Dec 31st 1887. Enclosures (1) one and (4) four, will be easily understood by what is said in reference to arrival of American vessels. In explanation of enclosure No (3) Mr Sharp the proprietor of the house, used for the legation informs me that heretofore instead of being paid in   in absence his rent (as I supposed) he has been paid at the end of the quarter. Therefore instead of rent voucher (1) one being approved for rent during the 3rd quarter it must be, taken for rent during the second quarter. The "draft" drawn in Mr Sharp's favor charging $87.50 to the 3rd quarter ending Sept. 30th and dated July 1st 1887, must read "Chargeable to the Second quarter ending June 30th Mr Sevier, kept no intelligent record of his business transactions in the office. I have drawn for Mr Jesse Sharp, a "draft" for $87.50 chargeable to this quarter for rent from July 1st to and including Sept 30th 1887. Said "draft," is dated Sept 30th 1887. and carries number, (16) sixteen. Hence you will have two "drafts" chargeable, to the same quarter, for the same thing with like vouchers, the difference only being in their dates and number. The only work necessary is to charge to the 2nd quarter, one of said "drafts" instead of both to their quarter. I have the honor to state, that I shall leave Monrovia for America, by the next vessel or steamer leaving this port. Capt. Beverly Y. Payne, a young Liberian, 28 y'rs of age, a citizen of the   of the Republic of Liberia, will be left in charge of the Consulate General, during my absence. In my opinion he is the most safe man I could appoint. He will be in the quality of Acting Vice Consul Gen'l. His compensation will be at the rate of $300.00 for annum, and this will be in full payment for all services rendered by him, in obedience to instruction from the Department at Washington or myself. Mr Payne's contract is on file in the State Department, accompanying my Dip. Series No. 23 (quod vide) I did not require Bond of Mr Payne, for (2) two reasons. First, because I am sure that nothing be recovered in a suit on a Bond in Liberia. Second, because I have the utmost confidence in this young man's integrity and believe a Bond to be entirely unnecessary. Mr Payne will draw no drafts on the against the United States treasury, or presume to express sentiments, or opinions of the Government, before first, having received instructions from the Department of State so to do. Believing that in every particular I have tired to subserve the interest   interest of the Government which I have the honor to serve, I beg you, to allow me the great privilege of subscribing myself to be,
Your Obedient Servant
Chas. H. J. Taylor
U.S.M.R. & Consul Gen'l
