No.5

United States Consulate
Sierra Leone, June 2nd 1874.

Sir

I have the honor to acknowledge receipt of your dispatch No 4, saying that representations have been made, informing you that delay has been caused in several instances, by my absence or otherwise, in granting debenture certificates.

I have the honor to inform you, that to the best of my knowledge, nothing of the kind has ever happened; and that such representations could only have been made by parties wishing for the Consular appointment, or by parties with whom I am unfortunately at variance.

I beg to suggest that you will kindly forward to me copies of any letters or documents, which you may have received on this subject, to enable me to reply more fully, to any statements which may have been made therein, to my detriment.

The difficulties which may have arisen through my not having properly affixed the Consular Seal, have been caused by my not having the required seal; the only one in my possession being the one I use for making impressions in ink.

I have also to acknowledge receipt of Copy of Consular Regulations, and Circular No 58, both of which shall receive any strict attention.

I have the honor to remain Sir
Your obedient servant
Nathl Lyons
U.S. Consul for Sierra Leone &c

Honble: J.C.B. Davis
Assistant Secretary
Department of State
Washington U.S.A.
