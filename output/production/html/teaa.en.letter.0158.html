United States Consulate
St Paul de Loanda
Dec 14th 1882.

To Hon John Davis
Asst. Secty. of State
Washington D.C.

Sir

I have the honor to enclose herewith a translated copy of the letter from J. L. Torres Esqr one of Thomas Foster's master of Bark Alice Knowles given to Henry Williams, sent on shore at Mossamedes for sickness. I also enclose his affidavit, and those of David Miller, Charles Sims, & Augustus Jasan; runaway Am: seamen; Charles Sims and Augustus are also sick at present, and the Capt of the Portuguese steamer does not care to take them on board in their present condition. They are now upon this consulate for over a month, and not expecting any Am: vessel for the present, I shall be obliged to send them by some Portuguese merchant, vessel to Lisbon, if the Capt will take them, these men have not any clothes or shoes, but those given to them by this consulate

I have the honor to be Sir
Respectfully
Your obedient servant
L. de R. du Verge
US Consul

United States Consul Loanda.

Mossamedes 1st of November 1882.

Without any favor from you to answer, I satisfy my last of the 8th of october per S.S. "Benguella." "Samples of produce from the district": Enclosed please find a bill of lading for a box of samples of the produce of Mossamedes, which will be sent on by the first packet, due in a few days, the said samples consist of:

Orchilla
Beans
Indn Corn
Rum
Wheat
Almeidina

With regard to these samples, I must say that Almeidina, is a produce from which 50 P.C. of good Indian Rubber is exhacted[?], what[?] regards to wheat, this country has not produced anything to speak of, as they have not turned their attention to its cultivation, but lately and it has been sown to considerable extent, and they sanguinely expect a great result.

Produce exported from this Dispatch: as follows

Hides
Wax

Orchilla
Almeidina
Cotton
Vegetables
Fruits
Oxen
Cows
Sheep
Rum
Garden produce, Beans peas, &a &a Wheat sown as stated above

 

"American Shipping" The Am: Ship "Shelton" Capt. S.B. Potter, entered this port on the 7th of october sailed on the 19th of the same month; Whaling? On the 12th of the same month entd America Bark "Alice Knowles" Capt Thomas Foster, came in to request and returned Whaling on the 19th of the same. On the 10th of the same month entered Am. Ship "Eliza Adams" Capt Walter F. Howland; also came in to refresh and returned Whaling on the 27th ditto. From the Bark "Alice Knowles" the Sailor Henry Williams was landed sick, and who will go on to your City by the first packet, leaving a Certificate from this agency dated october 26th 1882. The "Eliza Adams" shipped on the 25th ult. 4 sailors, 2 of which ran away immediately after signing their articles and receiving 8 dollars and 50 cents in advance; they were arrested 2 days after the sailing of the ship, and I intend sending them on to your consulate by first favorable opportunity that you may dispose of them as you may think fit; It is probable that I shall have to advance the money for their passage of which I will duly advise you. Their names are "David Miller" and "Charles Sims". Another sailor, by name of "Augusto Fernandes" ran away from the said ship, and came to day to present himself to me; I shall send him also to you with   with the other 2. and advise the amount of passages and expences which I have advanced them here It seems to me proper to send them to Loanda that it may be known hereafter to all others coming here in future, that such acts will not be countenanced without being punished according to Law.

Being all for the present, I remain with all due respect and consideration
"Signed" João L. Torres
U.S. Consul Agent

P.S. The sailor above described as "Augustus Fernandes, is Augustus Jason".

 

I David Miller a native born of New York do solemnly swear under oath what I shipped on board Am: Ships "Eliza Adams" of New Bedford Walter F. Howland, master at St. Helena for 6 months on the 18th of April 1882 ad arrived at Mossamedes or Little Fish Bay the latter part of October 1882. Capt said to me do you want your discharge & I said yes; he answered you shan't have it as I have to pay the Consular Agent; and told him I did not know how that was; I was unwell & not fit to go again on board, I also asked him what was due me, he said very little you son of a ---- & he at once gave orders to his officers to give me plenty work to do, & so tie me up to the rigging if I did not do it; I went on shore on leave of absence a four day after, but now returned. I reshipped and received $8.50 in advance, but when I found out that I had   shipped again in the same vessel after all the bad treatment I had already received, I ran away with the advance received and hid myself in the bushes, living on fruits only for a few days, after the ship's departure I went and reported myself to the Consular Agent, but was arrested by the police who had been searching for me & brought to the Consular Agent who put me at once in a boarding house sent me to Loanda by the Portuguese mail steamer

I regret to have deserted, but it was impossible for me to bear any longer the ill treatment received from the Capt. & officers of the Eliza Adams. I am now here, Sir as you see me, almost nude, with nothing else but the old pants & coat I have on, & I wish & pray you to send me home by the first chance

So help me God.
Signed) David Miller

I, Augustus Jason, "seaman" do solemnly swear & take oath and affirm, that I am 18 years of age a native of New Bedford, & shipped in St Helena April 1882, on board the ship "Eliza Adams" of New Bedford for 12 months Walter F. Howland, master; and arrived in "Mossamedes" on Little Fish Bay October 18th 1882

I swam on shore & deserted, the distance from the ship to the shore was about 1 3/4 miles. The cause of my running away from the vessel was brutal treatment from the 3rd mate Mr Joe Smith he being encouraged by the other officers & Capt. ordering him to have me well beaten. I served six months on said vessel & found it impossible to hear the abuse any longer. I ran away & hid myself in the bushes until the vessel left port, & when I went to report myself to the Consular Agent   was arrested by the police, who had been sent after me said my desertion. The Consular Agent treated me well while in Mossamedes & sent me to this Consulate. My greatest desire is to return home, I am sorry that I ran away, but I beg to state that the ill treatment I received on board for six months was more than man could bear; I am here bare footed having only the jumper & overhauls that you see on me, & I beg help from my Govt. I also state that I can bear witness, that the named, Charles Sims and David Miller, of the same vessel were also ill treated & brutally abused by the officers & master

So help me God
Signed) Augustus Jason

I Henry Williams "boat steerer" on board Am: Bark "Alice Knowles" of New Bedford "Thomas Foster" master, do solemnly swear & affirm under oath, that I have been on board of said Alice Knowles for about 3 1/2 years, during which time I only received from the master the sum of $36 00/100 in all, that I never refused duty while on board and was sent on shore at Little Fish Bay or "Mossamedes" by the Capt. on account of sickness. While on shore here the Consular Agent took good care of me & sent me to a boarding house until the Portuguese Steamer left for Loanda, & I believe that the Consular Agent paid my passage to this Consulate.

The Consular Agent gave me $13 of which I expended $1 while in Mossamedes, I now have in my possession $12. When I was sent on shore by the Capt's order I did not sign clear of the Articles, but he   Capt gave me this letter "Letter Enclosed" to the owner of New Bedford. My agreement with the master was that I should have for my self a barrel of oil for every 90 barrels on board; From the slop chest I received only $15.25. I was laid up 3 1/2 weeks sick on shore at Mossamedes waiting the arrival of the Portuguese Mail Steam ship for this port. I never signed or put any mark to any kind of paper or document; the Capt. told me tat I would receive on my arrival at New Bedford $9.00 for my shore, which I think is specified in the letter he gave me for the owner. I am now at the mercy of his Consulate sick and destitute, a stranger in a foreign country & not able to explain in good English as I am a native of the Sandwich Islands but have sailed on Am: Whaling vessels for the last 25 years; I beg your Excellency to send me to New Bedford & to see that an   old man as I am, sick with paralysis so[?], is not cheated out of his hard earned money

So help me God
Signed, Henry Williams

I Charles Sims "seaman" do solemnly swear & take oath: that I arrived at Little Fish Bay or "Mossamedes" on board ships "Eliza Adams" of New Bedford, "Walter F. Howland" master in October 1882: My time had expired upon my arrival at "Mossamedes," having served one year on board according to contract; I told the Capt. of the fact; and received ill treatment from him and his officers; he telling me d-d the Consul at St Helena or any other one, I'll show you I am master of this craft & I'll drive you son of --- to hell if you do not work & do your duty, using in the meantime his fists on my face & all kinds of language.

I deserted and shipped again a few days after for six months at the Consular Agent's office, but I found out that I had shipped back on the same vessel, I again deserted after receiving from the   Consular Agent $8.50 advance which I thought I might keep for wages due me by the master; as to go back on the same ship and was impossible for me to do so; as the Captain and officers would have killed me during that six months.

I was arrested by the order of the Consular Agent at Mossamedes, but too take to be sent on board. The Consular Agent after keeping me a few days sent me here as you see me now without shoes or hat; and having only the old for [unclear, one word] pants and shirt which cover me and I beg your honor to send me home to New Bedford if possible. I also state that I have been often an eye witness of the beating & ill treatment that Augustus Jason and David Miller received on board.

So help me God

Signed) Charles Sims
