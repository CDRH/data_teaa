No 4.

Vice Commercial Agency of the United States of America.
Sierra Leone, August 1st 1868

Sir,

In the absence of Mr. Henry Rider, the United State Commercial Agent at this settlement, I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of a despatch from the Department of State No. 6 and dated June 22nd 1868, requesting Mr. Rider to report to the Department the name of the State or Country in which that officer was born and of the State from which he was appointed, which information it is alleged he failed to communicate as instructed in the Department's despatch to him numbered 1.

In reply I beg to state that after perusing the copies of Mr. Rider's various despatches I F. W. Seward Esqre
Assistant Secretary   I find one dated November 19th 1866 with the following concluding paragraph:

"I was born in Salem, Massachusetts and was appointed from the same place".

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
Rosenbush
U.S. Vice-Comm: Agent.

 

U.S. Vice Comm: Agency
Sierra Leone Augt 1st 1868.

Theodore A. Rosenbush

No 4.

Received

Reply to Despatch No 6 Mr. Riders' place of birth and appointment.
