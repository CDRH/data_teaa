Recd May 28

Consulate of the United States of America at Sierra Leone
March 20th 1863

Sir

I have the honor to request that this office may be supplied with the usual consular blanks and official despatch paper as I am entirely out of them and they cannot be obtained in this colony.

I beg further to say that as considerable business is done in this office great inconvenience is experienced from the want of the regular consular books, real and press, the last administration having neglected to supply them at the time of my appointment.

I therefore beg to request that they may be supplied to this office.

Opportunities of forwarding packages to this colony occur frequently Hon. William H. Seward
Secretary of State
Washington D.C. frequently by the way of New York through Mess E. R. Ware & Co at No. 26 1/2 Broadway, who do a regular business between that city and this port

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your most obedient servant
Henry Rider
U.S. Consular Agent
