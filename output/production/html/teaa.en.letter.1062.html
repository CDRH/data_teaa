Monrovia, March 16th 1888
Liberia, W. C. A.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your communication of the 24th ult., transmitting to me by order of the Secretary of State of the United States a copy of a despatch from the Consul General of the United States at Panama, in regard to reported disturbances or disorders on the line of the Panama Canal, in which Liberian citizens are said to have been concerned.

In reply Sir, permit me to inform you that the copy of the important despatch above cited, has been with its enclosures enclosure   enclosures carefully perused by my Government, and after a due consideration has been given to the matter, it is the opinion of the Liberian Government that the impression of Consul General Adamson, as to the countries to which the so called Liberians really belong, are correct, for the following reasons.

1st It is not the custom of Kroomen or other natives of Liberia, when shipping to work in a foreign country, or even to work along the Coast to take their war customs with them. The fact being that they take away next to nothing but like to return in in   in opposite condition. Furthermore the Liberian natives shipping for work at Panama, had their outfit of clothing furnished by the Company's Agent, at the time of entering into the service of the Company.

2nd The natives of Liberia are notorious for their peaceful and orderly demeanor, whilst away from their homes and in a foreign country, they are as a general rule very careful to avoid any conflict with the laws of the country in which they are residing, which would tend to curtail their liberty of departure at the period fixed for their return to their homes. I have   I have therefore to request, that you will be kind enough to convey to the Government of the United States, the thanks of that of Liberia for the unabated interest it continues to exhibit for the welfare of Liberian citizens at present residing on the Isthmus of Panama, and, also convey to your Government the conclusion in which this Department is in accord with Consul General Adamson, that the parties who were said to have been engaged in the commission of disorder at Panama, are not natives of Liberia, but belong to the more intractable tribes situated to the North and South of Liberian Liberian B.
