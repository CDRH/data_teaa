Clydesdale, Albert Park
Didsbury, Manchester
14 June 1885

Sir,

I beg most respectfully to address Your Excellency through advice of Mr. Consul L de R du Verge, and to enclose originals of two documents in my possession when acting as Vice Consul for the United States of America, and as British Consul at St. Paul de Loanda and I crave Your Excellency's kind attention to my request and to the facts as follows:

I have been in St Paul de Loanda upwards of five years with Messieurs Newton Carnegie & Co, Merchants of that city and left in March last on account of ill health, and not   not being desirous of returning to that place, but to one a little more salubrious, venture to beg of Your Excellency the appointment as representation of the United States Government at some other place should there be a vacancy.

When passing the Cape de Verde Islands I had the pleasure of meeting the American Consul there (at St. Thiago) who told me he had suffered greatly of late through fevers, and did not feel at all satisfied, and desired to leave as soon as possible. Should this be the case I may say that Your Excellency could rely upon my faithfully fulfilling the duties of that post, I being thoroughly conversant with the Portuguese language, and well acquainted with the inhabitants of that Island, and where am very   very desirous of going if possible

During my stay with Messieurs Newton Carnegie & Co at Loanda I had ample time to study the duties of the Consulate, and often performed same under Vice Consul Robert Scott Newton's supervision.

It has long been my earnest desire to serve the American Government and should Your Excellency deem it advisable to grant me an appointment at S Thiago or any other port. I can only say that I shall always have the interests of the Government at heart, and endeavor in every way to prove myself worthy of the honor, conferred upon us. I am 25 years of age, and should Your Excellency desire a personal interview I shall be only too happy to cross the Atlantic whenever Your Excellency may demand   demand it, and are prepared to leave England at any time

I have the honor to be
Your Excellency's
Most obedient and Humble Servant,
(s) Wm S Bent

To His Excellency
The President of The United States of America
&c &c &c Washington D.C.
