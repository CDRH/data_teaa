Department of State Received Sep 3 1888
No. 3 Consulate General, U. S. A.
Monrovia, August 2nd 1888. Mr Ezekiel E. Smith
To the Department of State. SUBJECT:

Examination of Jas. R. Newby

ABSTRACT OF CONTENTS.

Dr. J. Henry Roberts who was first designated to examine Jas. R. Newby declines to do so, and states as his reason that $5. is insufficient. Dr. A. L. Wykham examines him.

  No. 3. Consulate General, U. S. A.
Monrovia, August 2nd 1888 Honorable George L. Rives,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D. C. Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to inform you that, as stated in my No 2 despatch, Dr. J. Henry Roberts was designated by me to examine Jas. R. Newby, the pension claimant. He consented to do so, but subsequently declined, stating as his reason for such declination, that the stipulated fee $5.00 was not sufficient. In consequence of his failure I designated A. L. Wykham, M.D. who proceeded to perform the designed examination, as per inclosure 3.

I have the honor to be, Sir,
Your obedient servant
Ezekiel E. Smith
U.S. Consul Gen'l

Inclosures 3

1

Consulate of the United States,
Monrovia, July 31st 1888.

Sir:

By instruction of the State Department of the United States Government, I hereby designate you to examine Mr. James R. Newby, an applicant for pension from my Government. You will find all necessary information appertaining to said case from the inclosed papers.

Permit me to state that Mr. J. H. Roberts, M.D. (on account of his seniority) was first designated to perform the examination, but owing to the smallness of the fee, $5, he declines to do so.

Respectfully,
Ezek. E. Smith
U.S. Consul Gen'l.
