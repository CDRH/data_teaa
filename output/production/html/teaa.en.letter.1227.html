Subject

The Todd Children

 

No 4

Consular Series

Consulate General of United States
Monrovia Liberia June 3rd 1895

Hon. Edwin F. Uhl
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform you that one Julius C Todd and his wife both citizens of the United States and of Minneapolis, Min. died at Clay Ashland Liberia Jany 1894 leaving five penniless orphans, one has since died leaving four, whose ages range from Two to Twelve years. Mr Todd had property in Minneapolis Min Messrs Yale & co "Real Estate and Insurance Dealers" were his Agents.   Mr. B. Y. Payne Vice Consul wrote them in Sept 1894 But they have not replied. A Guardian should be appointed to look after these children. Mrs M. A. Williams and M. A. Foster of this city and Hon. T. C. Lomax of Clay Ashland Liberia have cared for the children Gratis to the present. Will your Honor call the attention of the authorities in Minneapolis Min. to this matter? I send Mr. Todd's local address and business card.

I am obediently
William H. Heard
Consulate General
