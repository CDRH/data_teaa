Subject.

Permission to bring household effects, library &c., house, without Customs duties.

 

No. 40. Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia;
Dec., 5, 1899.

Hon. David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State;
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to say, that in preparing to make my departure from America, for this post of duty; I packed and brought, with me, quite a number of volumes, from my library; besides many household effects; such as bedding &c, as I was expecting, as I did do, to bring my wife and child with me. We were shipwrecked, off the coast of Wales, on the ill fated Dahomey, April 9, 1898. The contents of two large trunks   trunks, including the foregoing effects, that were in the hold of the ship; being a week under water, before they were taken from the ship, were very badly damaged. The atmosphere, here, is very detrimental to books &c, in the most perfect condition. Hence, as my wife desires to return home, with me early in the Spring of 1900; as per my pending request for leave of absence, with permission to visit America; in 1900, I desire, if it please the Department, a release from the annoyance of the Custom officials, upon my arrival in New York. I hope to reach New York, from Hamburg, sometime, between the 25-30, April 1900.

I have the honor to be &c.,
Owen L. W. Smith,
M. R. and C. G.
