Instruction No. 16. received. Application has been made for Mr. Anmey to act as Vice Consul General. Will report result of application.

 

No. 40.

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia, Liberia, March 21. 1878.

Sir:

I have had the honor to receive your instruction numbered sixteen, and dated January the twentyfirst. It relates to the appointment of Mr. Anmy to be Vice Consul General of the United States at Monrovia. The Certificate setting forth that appointment has been received at the Legation and application has been made to the proper authorities agreeably with the Department's instruction numbered on hundred and fifty eight, for the necessary permission to be extended to Mr. Anmey to act in that official capacity. I hope to have Honorable Fredrick W. Seward
Assistant Secretary of State, Washington.   have the honor to report the result of that application at an early date.

I have the honor to be,
Sir:
With considerations of high esteem,
Your obedient servant
J. Milton Turner
