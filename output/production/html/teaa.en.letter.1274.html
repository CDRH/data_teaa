Subject

Cape Coast Castle Consular Agency.

 

No. 36– Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia;
Oct. 3, 1899.

Hon. Thos. W. Cridler,
Third Assistant Secretary,
of State– Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt Sept. 27, of your No. 22, "Consular," of Aug. 26, 1899. You desire an expression from me relative to the retention or discontinuation of the Cape Coast Castle Consular Agency. In making such an expression, I have to say, that whereas the late George E. Eminsang Esq., the late Consular Agent, died April 17, 1898; before I assumed the responsibilities of   of this Consulate General, I have not been in official correspondence with said Agency; and therefore, feel very reluctant to advise, in the matter.

While at said Agency, in February last, I was impressed, by the businessmen and others of reputation, of Elmina and Cape Coast Castle, to believe that it was all important that said Agency should be retained. Having no knowledge of the volume of business transmitted between the United States and the Gold Coast Colony, I used every possible means to obtain as full a report of the Commercial statistics, as possible, and transmitted same to the   the Department, as a basis upon which it could make up its conclusions, relative to the retention or discontinuation of said Consular Agency.

Since writing my No. 33, "Consular," of Aug. 1, 1899, I have heard from Fynn Egyr Asaam Esq., who seems to have so far recovered his health as to think himself able to discharge the responsibilities of said Consular Agency, if appointed. I have, therefore, this day, forwarded to him, for execution and return to this Consulate General, a blank copy of "Application for Office"– Form No. 205.

I have the honor to be your
obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith; M. R. & C. General.
