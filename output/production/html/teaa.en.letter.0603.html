"Loanda"
Department of State Received Mar 23 1893

118 E. 45 St New-York

March 21, 1893

To Hon. Josiah Quincy
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington

Sir:

Instruction no 19, of February 16, last, by Hon. Wm. F. Wharton, informing me that the property of the U.S. Commercial Agency at Loanda Africa, "may remain, for the present, in the custody of Mr. L. C. Burling of the M. E. Mission at Loanda" has in due course of time reached me here.

For the guidance of the Department, allow me to state that Mr. R. S. Newton, formerly U.S. Vice-Consul at Loanda died at Madeira on his way to England and that his firm at Loanda is being dissolved.

Through no longer connected with the department, I shall always be glad to Â  give the Consular Bureau any information it may desire on African affairs, which continue to be the special object of my studies.

The above address, or care of National Museum Washington, will always find me.

I am, Sir,
Your obedient servant
Heli Chatelain
Late U.S. Coml Agent, Loanda, Africa
