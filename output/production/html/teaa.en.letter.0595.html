Department of State Received Aug 24 1878

Sierra Leone of Africa
July 26th 1878

Hon F. W. Seward
Assistant Secretary of State Washington D.C.

Sir-

I have the honour to acknowledge the receipt of your communication of June the 18th informing me of my appointment to the post of U.S. Consul at this port, and inclosing forms of bond, oath of Office, and Allegiance.

I accept the appointment and herewith return the bonds and oath of office to the department executed; I was born in the state of Virginia U.S.A. My parent emigrated to Liberia while I was quite young, shortly after there death I returned to America and lived in Boston Massachusetts until 1870 at the end of said year I was employed by the American Col. Society as Doctor to attend Emigrants in Liberia at which place I remained until 1876, After which I came to Sierra Leone where I now reside.

I cannot say which state I have been appointed from unless I claim Boston Mass as it is the only state I have resided in since leaving Virginia excepting[?] the time spent on the coast of Africa.

I have never resided in Great Britain and have only lived in Sierra Leone one of her Dependencies[?] since January 1877.

I have the honor to be
Sir
