Subject.

Reply to your No. 23, of Sept. 2, 1899; relative to vacating the present premises occupied for United States Legation, Monrovia, Liberia.

 

No. 41. Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia;
Dec., 9, 1899.

Hon. Thos. W. Cridler,
Third Assistant Secretary;
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt- Oct., 13, of your No. 23, of Sept., 2, 1899; advising that I vacate the present premises, used as United States Legation- Monrovia; provided the administrator Hon., R. H. Jackson, will not sink a well on the said premises.

In reply, I beg to say, I have just, yesterday, succeeded in having an interview with Senator Jackson, relative to the   the advice of the Department. He says it is not practiceable to sink a well, on the said premises, owing to the rocky foundation, and the want of proper machinery for drilling &c.

He says he prefers that the United States, shall have the use of the said premises, but if it will not retain the use, thereof, without the sinking of a well, it will have to vacate.

Owing to the scarcity of respectable and well situated property that can be rented, I think it best not to hurry out. It is possibly probable that I can more equally as good quarters, with a well on the premises, for about the same, rent, though not as desirably situated, in about a twelve months   months time.

I have the honor to be
your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith,
M. R. and C. General.
