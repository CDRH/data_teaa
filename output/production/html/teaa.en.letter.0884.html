U.S. Consulate at
Monrovia March 5, 1862

Hon W. H. Seward
Secty of State U.S.

Sir,

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your Despatch of Nov. 15, granting me leave of Absence for six months to visit my family and recruit my health. I feel grateful for this permission.

As there is not an American Vessel on this Coast to sail from Liberia within six months, I am obliged to avail myself of the British Mail Steamer, and shall leave then in a few days for Liverpool, thence for New York, hoping – Providence permitting – to be in the U.S. the last of April   April.

I have procured a suitable person to perform the duties of the Agency during my absence for all of whose official acts I hold myself responsible.

I shall report immediately on my arrival, [unclear, one word] have the honor to be with profound respect

Your most obdt & Hlbl Servant
John Seys
U.S. Vice Com. Agt
