Department of State Received Jun 2 1879
No 10

John A. Parm
To Department of State

Subject
Mutiny

Abstract of Contents.
Informing the Department of the insubordination of the crew of the American Brig "Ann Elizabeth" of Boston, and the sending two of the crew in the American Barque "Liberia" of New York, for trial.

To

Washington D.C.

Sir

I have the honor to report to the Department, that on the 29th of April last, Captain T. J. Burgess, master of the American Brig "Ann Elizabeth of Boston Mass. then in the port; applied to this Consulate for protection; stating that a portion of his crew were in a state of mutiny, and had refused to do any more work, that the Mate had been severely cut and mutilated, & he himself had been struck twice with the windlas normon, and the men had threatened to kill the mate if he returned on board of the vessel, & that things were in such a state state, they did not feel safe to have these men on board. I immediately went on board of said Brig, and found matters just as the Capt had stated. I then applied to the local authorities for aid: The men were arrested and imprisoned.

I subsequently ordered the men to this Consulate for investigation, and received each man's statement upon oath, a coppy of which I herewith inclose. From what I could gather, it seems that the man Bennis was under the influence of liquor, and the 1st Officer had been on a spree over night, hence they were not in a state to [?] much. The row began between these two while the Capt was in the cabin, & the rest of the crew below, consequently those who came on deck were incline to take their own view of the matter as it appeared to them. the Capt seemed to be parting the two, when the news had been passed below that time bound for New York, I thought it best to send them by her.

I accordingly sent the two men James Carleslile & Joseph Dolan by the said Barque "Liberia" on the 2nd of May, J. B. Williams master, bound for New York: giving the Capt a certificate on the Treasury Department for sixty Dollars for their passage.

I have to state that I did not send them in irons, as the Capt of the Barque said he did not require it, and the men seemed perfectly willing to be sent home from trial.

The other two men Bennis & Relly were sent back on board of the Brig "Ann Elizabeth" as they shewed less signs of hostility, and seemed willing to return to duty.

The Brig "Ann Elizabeth" will probably leave this port for Boston in a fortnight from this date.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your Your Obedient Servant
John A. Parm
U.S. Consul.

No. 1

U.S. Consulate at
Sierra Leone Africa April 29th/79

"Capt T. J. Burgess's Statement

"This morning about 7 'Oclock while writing my letters for mail I heard a noise on deck, of some kind of row or fight, I proceeded to the deck at once. I saw Mr Fosdick the first Officer, and one of the men Jas Bennis clenched & rolling about on the deck: I took hold of Mr Fosdick and pulled him off from the man, & told that, that, was quite enough, I did not allow any such work, & they must stop it at once; while in the act of seperating the parties, the rest of the crew came aft, armed with the windlass normon & knives. One of the men named Jas Carlisle struck me on the head with the the windlass normon, and once on the shoulder, & was in the act of doing it again, when the normon was taken out of his hand by the second officer of the ship. As soon as I came to my senses from the effects of the blow, I proceeded to the cabin, & from thence to the deck being nearly blind from the effects of the Blow blow I had received. As I proceeded to the deck I met the 1st Officer coming down bleeding very badly from blows & stabs. After I got to the deck I found things quiet with the exception, that the men said they would do no more work while on board the ship. I at once sent the 1st Officer to the Doctor for medical attendance.

One of the men Jas Carlisle made the remark "If he came back again that he would fix him good. I sent the men to breakfast. After breakfast I went to turn them to their work. They refused to work. I then asked them one by one if they were willing to work? Their answer was "No! and wanted to know what I was going to do with them". I told them I had nothing to say, & proceeded at once to the American Consul for protection."

No 2

Statement of O. G. Fosdick 1st Mate

"This morning about 7'Oclock while walking on the quarter deck I percieved signs of intoxication in Jas Bennis, one of the sailors. I spoke to him and told him to hurry up & not be idling around when he replied that he was working as fast as he intended to, and would work no faster. I then advanced toward him and he made a rush at me, and we both clenched, and fell down on deck. While there the Capt ran up and ordered us to stop, and tried to part us. The remainder of the crew then rushed out of the hole armed with windlass normon, bolts, & knives, and rushed upon the Capt, struck him twice with an iron normon, stabed me between the eyes, in the fore head and in the arm.

I became insensible from loss of blood, a few moments of the recovery, was sent on shore to the Dr to be attended to."

No 3

Statement of Jas Bennis.

"This morning I was working on the quarter deck of the Annie Elizabeth and the chief officer came to me & began a growl with me about the sail, & said I was getting on dam slow with the sail. Said I no Sir I am getting on as quick as I can. The told me he would have none of my cheek. I told him I was not giving him any of my cheek. says he, "shut up you dam son of a bitch." I told him I was no son of a Bitch, and he jumped on top of me sitting on the sail below. he picked up a maullin[?] spike and struck me over the head. I was stupid. after I recovered I saw the rest of my shipmates taking my part. I was lying on the poop deck, and as near as I can remember the Capt caught me by the throat (and one of the men told me that he said to the mate to give it to me.) The next place I found myself was in the forecasel."

No 4

Statement of Jos Dolan

"This morning about 7 Oclock the Kroo boy sung out that "Scott is getting killed". I rushed on to the poop, I saw the chief Officer having Bennis under & striking him. The Capt had him by the throat. I took hold of the Capt & said knock off. The Capt said give it to him. I took the mate off of him. The mate said what the hell you want, then he put his hand for his revolver. I then struck him & knocked him down, and kicked him, he ran down to the after companion & asked the Capt for a revolver. I stood at the companion door waiting for him, but he did not come up. The second Officer told me to keep quiet. (Six days ago while on duty, coming in harbour, the 1st Officer damed my soul to hell) I went forward and said I would do no more work while the chief officer was on board that vessel. After breakfast the Capt came forward & told me to turn[?] too I said No."

No 5

Statement if Jas Relly

About 7 Oclock this morning I was stowing casks between deck on board the "Ann Elizabeth" when Joe sung out "come up Relly, the Mate & Capt is killing Scotty". so I jumped up the main hatch, I rushed to them[?] and took the Capt by the shoulder & told him to knock off. I hardly had the word out of my mouth when Carlisle struck the Capt with the normon. I jumped back myself, and then the Capt jumped down off the poop; then they sung out that he had gone for a revolver. I then went to the cabin door to prevent his shooting any one if he did come up. then I saw the mate coming up out of the cabin, & I saw a white handle revolver, in his pocket behind, so I was going to make a snap[?] for it and throw it over board, but he turned round & I missed my chance. The Capt came forward and told us to stop it he wanted no more of the row. We told him we were not going to work any more he told us to go to breakfast: after breakfast he came forward & asked us if were going to turn too. but each of us told him No, we would not work any more."

Statement of Jas. F. Carlisle

"This morning I was in Ann Elizabeth's hole about 7'Oclock, when I heard a sudden cry. "Come up on deck, they are killing Scott."

I jumped up the fore hatch & took the normon, and as I ran aft I saw the Capt have hold of Bennis by the throat. I struck the Capt with this weapon, but did not get a fair blow at him, for if I had, he would have been a dead man, which was my intention. Then the Capt went down in the cabin and the second mate took the normon from me. and the first mate went down in the after companion, and we took the man Bennis forward. I came aft again and the mate was on the top of the house, he used some foul expressions. I then took out my knife & attempted to stab him, but it broke.
