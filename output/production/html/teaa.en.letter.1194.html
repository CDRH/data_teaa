Department of State Received Oct 18 1892

No. 16. Consulate-General, U. S. A.,
Monrovia, Liberia, September 13, 1892. Mr William D. McCoy,
To the Department of State. SUBJECT:

Submission of a note found on the Beach.

ABSTRACT OF CONTENTS.

Inclosure–
1. Note found on the Beach.

  No. 16. Consulate-General, U. S. A.,
Monrovia, Liberia, September 13, 1892. Honorable William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
WASHINGTON, D. C. Sir:

I have the honor of submitting to the Hydrographic Office, through you, the inclosed note which was found inclosed in a bottle which had drifted ashore about twenty five miles North West of Robertsport, Cape Mount, Liberia.

I would respectfully ask whether the finder, William Moore, is entitled to anything for his trouble and if so, how much shall it be?

Very truly–
Your obedient servant,

William D. McCoy,
Consul-General.
