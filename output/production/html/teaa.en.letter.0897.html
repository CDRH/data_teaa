U.S. Commercial Agency
Monrovia Sept. 3rd. 1862.

Hon. W. H. Seward,
Secretary of State.

Sir,

I have the honor to inform you of my arrival, in safety, at my destination, and also that I have been duly received and accredited as U.S. Commercial Agent at Monrovia.

The officers of the Liberian Government have been prompt and cordial, to an extent far beyond the claims of my official rank; and, though I have studiously endeavored to avoid taking any steps which were likely to elicit special hospitality, yet, I have been met at every point by a marked attention, an agreeable courtesy, and a generous confidence; and I feel assured that every facility and opportunity will be at my command for the efficient discharge of   the duties of my office.

I beg leave to refer to this matter in this form, for the purpose of vindicating myself against any seeming obtrusiveness beyond my present, proper sphere.

As soon as I have had an opportunity to acquaint myself with the records of my office, and have rallied from the physical prostration resulting from eleven weeks of long & weary travel, by land and sea, I hope to communicate more at length.

I have the honor to enclose, herein, a copy of the inventory of papers, books, and other effects delivered to me by the person whom I found in charge of the U.S. Com. Agency, at this post and have the honor to remain

Your very Obedient Servant,
Abraham Hanson,
U.S. Com. Agent, Monrovia
