[17th August, 1872.]

An Ordinance to amend the Duties of Customs.

Whereas it is expedient to increase the Import Duties on Spirituous Liquors, Tobacco and Gunpowder, and to abolish the Duties upon articles of food and clothing, and also upon building materials and other goods.

Be it therefore enacted by the Government with the advice and consent of the Legislative Council as follows;—

I. The several Ordinances mentioned in Schedule A, to this Ordinance annexed and all other Ordinances and Acts now in force repugnant to or inconsistent with the provisions of the Ordinance, shall be, and are hereby repealed Provided nevertheless that such Repeal shall not be deemed   to extend to any dues, drawbacks, liabilities or penalties imposed, accruing, allowed or due under any of the said Ordinances: but notwithstanding such repeal, all remedies and punishments for levying recovering and enforcing such dues, liabilities and penalties shall still remain in full force and effect and be capable of being enforced and inflicted, and all drawbacks shall be allowed as if such Ordinances were still in the authority or in pursuance of the Ordinances hereby repealed shall be valid and effectual notwithstanding such Repeal.

II. In lieu and instead of all other Duties and Drawbacks of Customs now charged and payable, there shall be raised, levied, collected and paid unto Her Majesty her heirs and Successors upon goods which shall from and after the passing of this Ordinance be imported into any part of the Settlement or transhipped either for consumption or for the purpose of being carried up the Sierra Leone River for any use of purpose whatsoever the several Duties of Customs, and there shall be allowed upon goods exported the several Drawbacks as the same are respectively described and set forth in the Table of Duties following, that is to say:—

Table of Duties.

Ale, Beer and Porter the Gallon 6d., or 1/- per dozen quarts, or 6d. per dozen pints.
Cigars, Cigarettes and all Manufactured Tobacco, the lb 2/-
Gunpowder the Barrel of 100 lbs. ... ... 2/-
Spirits or strong waters not being sweetened mixed with any article so that the degree of strength can be ascertained by Syke's Hydrometer, for every gallon of the strength of proof by such Hydrometer and so in proportion for any greater or less strength of proof and for any greater or less quantity than a gallon, the Gallon ... ... ... 2/-
Spirits being sweetened or mixed so that the degree of strength cannot be ascertained for every gallon or any greater or less quantity than a gallon, the Gallon, ... ... 2/-
Spirits unenumerated, the Gallon ... ... ... 2/-
Tobacco (unmanufactured) the lb ... ... 4d.
Wine (except Claret) the Gallon, ... ... ... 1/6
Claret, the Gallon, ... ... ... ... 6d.

III. All other goods not enumerated or described in the above Table of Duties shall be free and exempt from duty on the Importation thereof.

IV. The Importer of any goods nor subject to duties of Customs or his Agent shall deliver to the Collector of Customs a Bill of Entry of such goods in the same manner and form   and containing the same particulars as are required on the entry of dutiable goods so far as the same is applicable, which entry so far as regards the goods shall be a Transcript of the Report and shall therein describe such goods according to the terms upon which such goods are free of Duty and the value of such goods as shall have been previously chargeable with duty at value: and such Bill of Entry when signed by the Collector shall be transmitted to the proper officer and be his warrant for the delivery of the goods mentioned therein: and the Importer, Owner or Consignee of such goods or his Agent shall within Fourteen days after the entry and landing thereof deliver to the Collector a full and true account of the goods so landed.

V. Ale, Beer and Porter, Tobacco, Wines and Spirits may be bonded in Licensed Ponded Warehouses for Home Consumption or for Exportation in puncheons, cases, barrels, half barrels, pipes or hogsheads; but no demijohns of spirits shall be received into such warehouses except for Home Consumption.

VI. No Salt, Slates, Bricks, Coals, Coke, Lime, Tiles, or Lumber shall be landed on the Government Wharf.

VII. From and after the First day of October next no warehoused goods shall be exported or transhipped for exportation except in registered decked vessels of not less than Ten tons burthen.

VIII. There shall be further raised collected and paid unto her Majesty, her heirs and successors, an export duty of Three farthings on every Imperial shipping bushel (or Five shillings per ton of Twenty hundred weight net weight) of Ground nuts exported from this Settlement on and after the 1st day of January 1873.

IX. The landing, shipment or transhipment of such Ground nuts shall take place if necessary under the supervision of a person to be appointed by the Collector of Customs who shall be entitled to receive Five shillings per diem for the services of the person so employed from the owner or shipper of the ground nuts to be paid to the said Collector before the vessel on board of which the said ground nuts are shipped shall be entitled to clearance.

X. The Master or Consignee of every vessel about to leave any part of the Settlement having ground nuts or any articles liable to Export Duty on board shall at the time of the master delivering his Content or Entry Outwards deposit with the Collector one copy of the Bill or Bills of Lading which shall be signed by the said master and the consignee of the said vessel or party or parties shipping any produce subject to   hundred pounds, and in default of payment shall be committed to the Common Gaol for any period not exceeding Six Calendar months, unless such penalty and the costs attendant thereon shall be sooner paid and satisfied.

XIV. This Ordinance may be cited for all purposes as "The Customs Ordinance, 1872."

Passed in the Legislative Council this Seventeenth day of August, 1872.

Jacob W. Lewis,
Clerk of Legislative Council.

Assented to in Her Majesty's name, this Seventeenth day of August, 1872.

J. Pope Hennessy,
Governor.
