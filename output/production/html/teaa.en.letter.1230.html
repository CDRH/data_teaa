William H. Heard
To the Department of State

Subject

Loss by Exchange by Vice Consul

 

No 6. bis

Consulate Series

Consulate General of the U.S.
Monrovia Liberia July 10, 1895

Hon. Edwin F. Uhl
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:–

I have the honor to enclose the explanation of Vice Consul Beverly Y. Payne for the Auditor of the Treasury. I find that to change drafts here one must loose 2 0/0, 3 0/0 and 4 0/0, never less than 2 0/0, and that an English Sterling is $4.80 only. Do we loose in all our transactions 86.65 on every sterling. But there   are a few persons who can furnish United States Currency, and it is dollar for a dollar. Every Gold dollar. Every Silver dollar and every paper note is worth 100 cents.

I am obediently
William H. Heard
Min Res. and Con. General
