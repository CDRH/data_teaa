He considers that the affinity of the respective Governments should encourage a disposition to strengthen this relation when an opportunity offers, so as to secure the mutual advantages which it is calculated to promote. He is not forgetful, in this solicitation, of the disparity, in point of ability, between the Government of Liberia, the offspring, and that of the mother Republic.

Nevertheless, he is of the opinion that the capability of Liberia to administer importantly to the commerce of the United States would justify an arrangement between the two Governments by which they   they could be mutually benefited to an unprecedented extent.
