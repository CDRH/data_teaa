Department of State Received Feb 3 1887

No. 41

U.S. Consulate,
Monrovia Dec. 23/86

Mr. S. S. Sevier,
To the Secty of State

Subject

Secretary's circular received.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform you that your circular of Nov. 18/86 conveying the painful intelligence of the death of Ex. President Gen. Chester A. Arthur was received.

According to special order I shall display   display the usual symbols of mourning for the period of thirty days.

Please find inclosed a copy of a despatch from the Department of State of Liberia expressing their sympathy which is felt in the loss of so distinguished patriotic man as Ex-President Gen. Chester A. Arthur.

I am Sir
Your obedient servant
Saml S. Sevier
Acting Consul Gen.

Inclosure

 

1. Copy of Despatch from the Secty. of State of Liberia.

 

Department of State,
Monrovia Dec. 24, 1886

S. S. Sevier Esq
Acting U.S. Consul Gen.
Monrovia

Sir,

Your letter of the 22nd inst. has been received in which you communicated the sad intelligence of the death of the late Ex-President of the United States– General C A Arthur.

Please accept assurances of my sympathy, and of the sympathy of my Government with that of the United States in the loss it has sustained   in the death of so illustrious and honored a citizen.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient Servant
E. J. Barclay Secty of State.
