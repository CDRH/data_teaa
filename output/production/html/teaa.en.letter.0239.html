Washington Feby 9th 1859

Sir,

I have been permitted to read the reply of John E. Taylor, U.S. Consul at Sierra Leone, now on file in your Department, to a complaint made against him, by me, for conniving to defraud the United States.

He admits the substantial facts charged by me, but endeavors, with great circumlocution, to convince you of his innocency in the transaction,â€”an attempt in which I think he has signally failed. He has not even the manliness to acknowledge his error, or volunteer a regret for his conduct.

That he is wholly unfit for the place he holds is an admitted fact by all I have heard speak upon the subject, and I trust his appointment will not be forwarded.

Very Respectfully Your Obt Servt
J B Danforth Jr.

Hon John Appleton
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington D.C.
