No 107

United States Consulate at St. Paul de Loanda
25th November 1875

Mr. Robert Scott Newton to the Department of State

Subject. Arrival of Lieut. Cameron.

Abstract of contents.

Arrival at Loanda, on the 19th Novbr 1875, of Lieut. Cameron and the East Coast Livingstone Expedition. The expedition a complete success. Cameron trying to purchase a vessel to convey his 57 men to Zanzibar.

 

United States Consulate Loanda
25th November 1875

The Assistant Secretary of State Washington D.C.:

Sir; I have the honor to report the arrival here on the 19th inst. of Lieut. Cameron, leader of the East Coast Livingstone Expedition, who has successfully crossed the African Continent from Zanzibar. He reached the West Coast on the 7th inst arriving at Benguella. He is accompanied by 57 natives of Zanzibar, all that are left of over 300 with whom he originally commenced the journey. I understand that the Expedition has been a complete success, and that he has made some   most important Geographical discoveries. I believe that he is in course of treaty for the purchase of a vessel to transport his followers to their native country; and as soon as possible after their departure he, himself will proceed to Europe

I am, Sir
Your obedient Servant
Robt S Newton
Vice Commercial Agent
