U.S. Consulate at Monrovia,
Augst 7th 1862.

Hon F. W. Seward, Asst. Sec. State U.S.

Sir,

I have the honor to acknowledge receipt of your despatch from the Department dated May 8th 1862, informing me of the appointment of Mr Abr. Hanson of Wisconsin to the U.S. Commercial Agency at Monrovia Republic Liberia and directing me to deliver to him on his arrival at the Consulate in Monrovia the records, archives & all property in my possession belonging to the U.S. Consulate. It will afford me the greatest pleasure to comply with your order when that Gent. arrives.

A certified copy of the arrivals & departures of American vessels at this port for the quarter ending June 30th I forward this day to the Department through John Seys over Â  Esqr of Springfield Ohio late Vice Com. Agent at Monrovia by order from that Gent. also a correct repost of Fees received at this Consulate for the said quarter.

And I beg to inquire of the Department wether any thing more than Fees will be allowed me for service rendered at this Consulate since the appointment of Mr Hanson to the time of his arrival at the Consulate in Monrovia.

Your most obt. Servant,
B. V. R. James
U.S. V. Com. Agent.
