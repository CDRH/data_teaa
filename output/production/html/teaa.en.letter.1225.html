Index Bureau Dept of State Rec'd May 22 1895

No 45.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, April 11, 1895.

Mr. Beverly Y. Payne,
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Arrival of Immigrants at Monrovia

 

No 45

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, April 11, 1895.

Honorable Edwin F. Uhl,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor most respectfully to inform the Department that the Daneish Steam Ship "Horsa" 377 tons net, arrived at this port on the 7th instant after a voyage of eighteen days from Savanah, Georgia, U.S.A. with one hundred and ninety seven (197) colored Immigrants from different parts of the United States.

The "Horsa" was chartered by the International Migration Society of Birmingham   Birmingham, Alabama, U.S.A.

These Immigrants have been landed at this port, and the Government of Liberia will have to provide for them, as they have not been by the society.

The "Horsa" cleared yesterday for Sierra Leone.

I have the honor to be
Sir:
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne,
Vice Consul.
