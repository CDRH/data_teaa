Department of State Received Oct 22 1887

No. 9

United States Consul General
Monrovia Liberia
Sept 14th 1887

Chas H. J. Taylor to Hon. Jas. D. Porter

Subject:

Reply to Instructions, 1, 3, 4 & 5.

 

No. 9.

Consulate General Series
Monrovia Liberia
September 14th 1887

Hon. Jas. D. Porter

Sir:

I have the honor to inform you, that owing to the irregularity of steamers plying along the West Coast of Africa, calling at Monrovia, that I am just in receipt of your No 5, dated July 11th 1887. The surf is often so very bad, at this place that the captains of steamers with mail, for Monrovia are forced to go on to Bassa. Mails left at Monrovia's next town Bassa is just as long getting back to Monrovia as it would take to receive mail from England. I have the honor to inform you, that your No 2, has never reached Monrovia. I have this day informed Mr S. S. Sevier of the contents of your No 5. as well as of the contents of the Comptroller of the treasury department's communication   dated June 23rd received here, August 13th A period of nearly three months in transit. Mr Sevier is not a citizen of the United States he is an African native, an American negro being his father. He holds land in the Republic of Liberia which no nonresident is allowed to do. He is a full citizen here exercising the right of suffrage at such election where present in this country. He is now in the United States on a visit, and informed me that his post office address is Hampton Va. Be pleased to inform the Comptroller, of the Treasury Department, that Mr Sevier ceased to be U.S. Vice Consul Gen'l on June 6th last past. With renewed assurances of my high esteem for your consideration,
I am, Sir, your obt. servt
Chas. H. J. Taylor
