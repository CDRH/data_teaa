Resignation of Office of U.S. Consul at St. Paul de Loanda

Abstract of contents:

Same as above.

 

U.S. Consulate,
St. Paul de Loanda
New York Aug 18, 1888.

Hon. G. L. Rives,
Asst. Secretary of State
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform the Department that I hereby tender my resignation, to take date from the receipt of this of the office of U.S. Consul at St. Paul de Loanda, Africa.

Explanation of this my action is found in the fact that the compensation of the office is such as will force any one no many embarrassments. This has, unfortunately, been my experience both in Africa and at home.

After having been incumbent of this office for longer than a year, I find   find that I am compelled to resign not only deeply in debt, but actually without means and with no prospect of immediate employment.

The poignancy of my sorrow is added to when I remember that the difficulties surrounding my situation does not alone touch me but, threatens also my wife and children.

Praying that my resignation will be accepted and thanking the Department for its patience and great consideration.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient servant,
Henry F. Downing Consul
