Index Bureau Dept. of State Rec'd 5 Sep '99

No. 34, Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia,
Aug. 3, 1899.

Sir:

I have the honor to say, that the recent and constant racial troubles in the United States, are having the effect to greatly alienate the African (Liberian) trade from them. The principal Liberian merchants are formerly American citizens, and since the frequent outbursts of racial disturbances, in the United States, they are seeking a closer business alliance with European merchants.
