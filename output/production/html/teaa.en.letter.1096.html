Department of State Received Mar 30 1889
No. 16 Consulate General, U. S. A.
Monrovia, Liberia, Feb'y 18th 1889. Mr. Ezekiel E. Smith
To the Department of State. SUBJECT:

Forms &c for Elmina.

ABSTRACT OF CONTENTS.

The Consular Agent at Elmina, Gold Coast Colony, wishes forms: 101, 103, 117, 120, 130, also, an American flag, or flag of the U.S.

  No. 16. Consulate General, U. S. A.
Monrovia, Liberia, Feb'y 18th 1889 Honorable G. L. Rives
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D. C. Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to forward, herewith, a communication from the U.S. Consular Agent at Elmina, Gold Coast Colony, requesting supplies for his office.

I have them not on hand, in this Consulate General.

I am, sir,
Your obedient servant,
Ezekiel E. Smith,
U.S. Consul Gen'l.

Inclosure, one.

1. Mr. Eminsang to Mr. Smith.

 

Consulate of the United States,
Elmina, Gold Coast Colony
December 10th 1888.

Ezekiel E. Smith Esqre:
U.S. Minister Resident & Consul General
Monrovia, Liberia

Sir:

Your despatch no. 1 to hand in reply I have the honor to state, that I am most in need for this Consulate the following Forms: No. 101 (the new form) & Nos: 103; 117; 120. 130.

I would feel much obliged if your Honor could supply me with the above; I should thank you also if you could supply this Consulate with an American Flag, which is very much needed.

I am, Sir,
Your most obedient Servant
G. E. Eminsang
U.S. Consular Agent
