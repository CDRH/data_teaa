No. 52. U.S. Consulate at
Sierra Leone March 24th 1881.

Hon John Hay
Asst. Secty State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to forward herewith an extract from the shipping news of the Semi Weekly New York Journal of Commerce (19 Feby 1881, see Enclosure[?] - regarding a statement which seems to be made by the Master & Mate of the Bark Hazard - which sailed from this Port bound to Boston on or about Dec 23rd 1880.

I would state that the representations therein made that they had but one boat as the other one was stone[?] on the West Coast of Africa and none other was   was procurable and as the wreck of it was useless & was sold for what it would bring &c is not borne out by the facts - but on the contrary the boat was sold to Samuel Lewis Esquire a Barrister of Law residing in this place and I respectfully call your attention to the Enclosure viz - his letter to me in answer to my inquires on the subject. He purchased the boat and with some trifling repairs has been using said boat frequently from that time to the present and the said boat is now in good condition

I remain Sir
Most Respectfully
Your Obt Servt
Judson A. Lewis
Consul

 

Oxford Street
March 23rd 1881

My dear Sir,

In reply to your note I have to say that Capt Roberts of the "Hazard" sold to me on the 23rd Dec 1880 a boat which was then in tolerably good condition but wanted some repairs

I am
Yours truly
Saml Lewis

J. A. Lewis, Esqre
