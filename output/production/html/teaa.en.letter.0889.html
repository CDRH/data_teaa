Kenosha, Wis. May 13th, 1862

Hon W. H. Seward,
Secretary of State,

Sir;

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of the notice of my appointment to the office of "Commercial Agent of the United States, at Monrovia." Also a blank Bond to be executed and returned. (no other enclosures)

I was born in England, but am now a naturalized citizen of the United States of America, and have been appointed to officer from the State of Wisconsin. Have not, at any time, resided in Liberia.

I have the honor to enclose my Bond, duly executed, with the certificate of the District Attorney thereunto annexed.

 

I also enclose a certificate copy of the certificate of my naturalization.

I beg leave to add that it is my purpose to commence my journey to the post assigned me, early in June, and, unless a more direct & expedition mode of conveyance presents itself, to sail by steamer to England, and embark from thence by steamer, for Liberia.

I have the honor to be,
Dear Sir,
Very Respectfully,
Your Obedient Servant,
Abm Hanson.

 

Certificate of Naturalization.

Be it Remembered, That at a Circuit Court, held at Milwaukee, for the County of Milwaukee, in the State of Wisconsin, in the United States of America, on the 30th day of January in the year of our Lord One Thousand Eight Hundred and Fifty Sixty two, Abraham Hanson a native of England exhibited a Petition praying to be admitted to become a Citizen of the United States, and it appearing to the said Court that he had declared on Oath before the Circuit Court for the County of Du Page State of Illinois on the 7 day of November A. D. 1842, that it was bona-fide his intention to become a Citizen of the United States, and to RENOUNCE FOREVER, all allegiance or fidelity to any Foreign Prince, Potentate, State or Sovereignty whatsoever, and particularly to Victoria Queen of Great Britain & Ireland of whom he was at that time a Subject and the said Abraham Hanson having on his solemn Oath declared, and also made proof thereof, by competent testimony of John W. Dunlap & A. W. Kellogg citizens of the United States, that he had resided one year and upwards in the State of Wisconsin, and within the United States of America upwards of five years immediately preceding his application; and it appearing to the satisfaction of the Court that during that time he had behaved as a man of good moral character, attached to the principles of the Constitution of the United States, and well disposed to the good order and happiness of the same; and having on his solemn oath declared before the said Court that he would support the Constitution of the United States, and that he did absolutely and entirely renounce and abjure all allegiance and fidelity to every Foreign Prince, Potentate, State and Sovereignty whatsoever, and particularly to Victoria Queen of Great Britain & Ireland of whom he was before a Subject.

Thereupon the Court admitted the said Abraham Hanson to become a Citizen of the United States, and ordered all proceedings aforesaid to be recorded by the Clerk of the said Court; and which was done accordingly.

 

State of Wisconsin
Circuit Court, Milwaukee County ss:

I, W. H. Jacobs Clerk of said Court do hereby certify that I have compared the foregoing with the original certificate of the Naturalization of Abraham Hanson, that it is a correct transcript therefrom and of the whole thereof as the same remains of record in my office

In testimony whereof I have hereto set my hand and affixed the seal of said Court this 13 day of May AD 1862

W. H. Jacobs Clerk
By W. L. Cleaver Dep.

Certificate of Naturalization
Abraham Hanson
Commercial Agent at Monrovia, Liberia
