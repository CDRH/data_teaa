No. 5.

U.S. Commercial Agency,
Monrovia, Dec. 26th, 1862.

Hon. Wm. H. Seward,
Sec. of State U.S. of A.,

Sir,

I beg leave most respectfully to inform the Department of State, that the Reports due from this office, of Dec 31st., 1862, together with other documents, will be forwarded by the ship "M. C. Stevens," of Baltimore, which sails hence shortly.

I beg also to ask, from the Department, some instructions in reference to the mode of transmitting Despatches &c from this office. The usage has been to send them exclusively by merchant vessels, which, though a cheap, is an uncertain & irregular way. I have, to some extent, ventured to deviate from former practice, but will, henceforth, follow the usage until advised.

My impression is that our interests will be promoted by correspondence thro' an expeditious, and regular route.

I have the honor to be, with great Respect,
Your Obedient Servant,
Abraham Hanson,
U.S. Coml. Agent.
