For your better information, I enclose Mr. Taylor's two letters to me, in which he offered me me   me the position with compensation at the rate of $300. per annum. You are aware I contracted with Mr. Taylor for this amount, but you will see by his letter and the contract that I was already employed, and would have nothing to do, but attend the mails, and keep in safety the Archives of the Legation: Since, a Bond of $3000. has been required of me, which I have forwarded to the Department.

I consented to receive the compensation offered by Mr Taylor, because he would be absent but twelve (12) weeks, as I had occasion to refer you to some time ago; now that Mr Taylor's functions has ceased, and I required to give give   give the Bond required of a Vice Consul General. I cannot serve for the compensation offered, hence, I do not sign the Draft for services to 31st March, at Mr Taylor's rate of compensation, but retain it, and await your advice, as I am quite satisfied you can see my position. I have in many instances lost in my other avocation to do justice and honor to the trust I have.

I do not object to receiving compensation at the rate contracted with Mr Taylor, for the time of his incumbency, therefore I have this day drawn my Draft No 1 on the Secretary of State for services according to the Contract Contract   Contract from October 3rd 1887, (the date of Mr Taylor's departure) to the 12th January 1888, when his official function, and my contract with him ceased.

I enclose Account [unclear, one word], as per copy or form transmitted to me.

Having forwarded my Bond to the Department, and reported, I consider myself directly responsible to the Government of the United States, and to it will I expect compensation for service from January 12th 1888.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
Beverly Y. Payne

Monrovia Liberia.,

Sir:

Without any Application having been filed by you, in the U.S. Legation, for any position within my gift, I do hereby say to you that if you feel disposed to take charge of the Legation in my absence, until my return to the country, I will immediately upon my return nominate you for the position of U.S. Vice Consul Gen'l for the Republic of Liberia. I would nominate you now, only I have declared to the Department at Washington, my intention of allowing the post of Vice Consul Gen'l to remain vacant, until the embarrassing situation of the late Vice Consul Gen'l here, shall have been explained, and passed on. You will please decide in reference to this matter on or before the 22nd inst. If you consent to accept the trust I will enter into a contract to pay you at the rate of $300 per annum, for the time I am absent. In

Monrovia Liberia
Sept 19th 1887

Hon Beverly Y. Payne.

Sir:

I am in receipt of your favor, of even date herewith, accepting the Position of Acting Vice Consul General, for the United States. Please find enclosed a "blank contract" which you will greatly oblige me to make duplicate (2) copies of. Return the then, (3) three copies to me. I will sign them with you, giving into your possession one copy, filing with the Department of State in Washington, one; and retaining one copy myself. The matter of your being from the date of my departure U.S. Acting Vice Consul General, is no longer a secret. You are at liberty to make statement of the fact, to anyone you please. At any time in the future when you may feel disposed, after your regular hours in the store, you may call on me, when I will proceed to give you such instructions as I may think proper for you to know, in order to insure a correct discharge of duty. I can assure you, that only two things moved me to appoint you. First, your conduct, as an upright young man, and the fact that you hold no civil office in the Republic. I predict, for you a "brilliant future" if you maintain through life, your present course.

I am sir, your obedient servant.
Chas. H. J. Taylor.

Monrovia September 27th 1887.
Liberia, W.C.A.

Hon: C. H. J. Taylor
U.S. Minister Resident and Consul General
U.S. Legation
Monrovia.

Sir,

I am in receipt of your communication of the 26th inst, in which you inform me that you "shall take your departure for America, via England or Hamburg, by the next vessel or Steamer thither bound"; and further informing me that "during my (your) absence, I (you) shall be pleased to have you consider Mr. Beverly Y. Payne as in charge of the United States Consulate General at this place", and that Mr. Payne "will act in the capacity and quality of U.S. Acting Vice Consul General".

In reply permit me Sir, to advise you of the consent of this Government to your proposed arrangement viz with reference to Mr B. Y. Payne's exercising the function of U.S. Acting Vice Consul General during your absence, and thanking you for your kind offer of services to the Government.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient Servant
E. J. Barclay
