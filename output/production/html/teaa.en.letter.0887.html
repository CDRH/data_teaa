Springfield Ohio
April 28, 1862.

Hon W. H. Seward
Sec. of State U.S.

Sir,

I have the power to inform you of my arrival from Liberia via England on the 24th Inst. at Jersey City, and at this place, my home, on the 26th.

My health is very poor, having suffered much during the voyages from Africa to Liverpool, and from thence to the U.S.

I have the honor to inform you that I have appointed as "Deputy Vice Com. Agent for the U.S. at Monrovia" The Hon. B. V. R. James of that City. Mr James is a Gentleman of Stirling Integrity, has been for years Financial Superintendent of the Presbyterian Missions Â  Missions in Liberia, Judge of the Court of Probate, and twice elected to the office of Mayor of the City of Monrovia, which office he now fills. I hold myself responsible for his official acts in my absence.

I have the honor to be
your most ob. & Hble Servant
John Seys
