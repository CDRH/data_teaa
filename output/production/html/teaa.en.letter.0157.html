Received Feb 10 1882
No 30

Consulate of the United States of America
at St. Paul de Loanda S.W.C. Africa

L. de R. du Verge
to State Department
Washington D.C.

Subject

Acknowledgment of Despatch No 10

Encloses

Reasons why agents were appointed at Ambriz. Benguella & particularly Mossamedes.

United States Consulate
St Paul de Loanda
December 14th 1882

Hon: Wm. Hunter
Asst. Secty of State.
Washington D.C.

Sir

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your despatch No 10, in regard to Agents appointed by me until approved by the Hon: Secty of State; it was and still is with the intention of promoting Am: Commerce in this part of the world, beside putting a stop to runaway seaman, for abuse received on board Am: whalers: and while enforcing the U.S. Law over this jurisdiction, to give prompt justice to seamen, masters or officers. "Mossamedes" will in a few years become important large fields of wheat, corn, sugar cane, and cotton are now in cultivation.

The Portuguese Govt. is organizing small colonies all over that district, The Boërs in "Whilloh" and "Humpata" are cutting conformity with the Portuguese a route from the interior; so that their crops, and the result of their hunting[?] such as Hides, Ivory, & Ostrich Feathers, can be carried to Mossamedes: These colonies as well as the Boërs; and even the natives of the interior who seem to follow now the example of the Boërs, will soon want Farming implements; and I do not know if any to be compared with those of the U.S. for simplicity, durability and cheapness. The merchants and large land holders will need also steam mills, and other machinery for making Rum, Sugar, grinding corn and wheat, cleaning and pressing cotton &a &a Am: Whalers seem to make their head quarters now at the place, although the best inducements have been granted to them by the Portuguese govt. at Loanda.

I understand that nine whalers were there during the last two months. From the "Eliza Adams" three seamen ran away, and from the " Alice Knowles" a man was discharged by reason of sickness; these seamen have been sent to me from Mossamedes at a great expense, and I find myself compelled to keep them until I am able to forward them to Santiago "Cape de Verde" by mail steamers, which only takes them occasionally, more as a favor, although I pay to the Capt. £5.0.0, for each man.

Mr. J. L. Torres that I appointed until approved by the Hon: Secty of State is the largest merchant of Mossamedes, a young man, full of energy, and ambitions to promote the commerce and development of Mossamedes.

It is only upon close inquiry, as to his capacity, integrity, capability &a &a that I appointed him. If the State Dept has any objections to these appointments, I will notify them at once as not having been accepted by the State Dept.

I have never seen either of the gentlemen at Ambriz, Benguella & Mossamedes; but I am confident that an agent is needed at Mossamedes for the interest of Am: Commerce and protection of Am: Seamen and others visiting that port.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient servant
L. de R. du Verge

N.B. This is the only kind of paper that can be obtained in Loanda.
