No. 20

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia Liberia

J Milton Turner
to the
Department of State

Subject:

Transmitting the Bond of Rudolph Hirsh Esq. to be Vice Consul of the United States at Monrovia Liberia

Inclosure No. 1.
Bond of Mr Hirsch

 

No. 20.

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia 12th March 1877

Sir:

I have the honor to transmit herewith, as instructed through your No. 10. the bond executed by Mr. Rudolph Hirsch, whom I have in my despatch of the tenth January, nominated to be Vice-Consul General of the United States at this place; instead of Gabriel David Moore Esquire. Mr. Brohm the Consul for the German Empire, and L. F. Richardson Esquire, Captain of the American barque "Liberia," have Honorable
John L. Cadwalader
Assistant Secretary of State   have kindly consented to become the sureties of Mr Hirsch, and have accordingly signed the Bond furnished me by the Department

I have the honor to be
Sir:
With high considerations
Your obedient servant
J Milton Turner
