Kenosha, Wis. May 2nd 1862

Hon. J. R. Doolittle
U.S. Senate

Dear Sir,

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your communication, enclosing also one from the Hon Sec. of State, the substance of which is an offer, to me, of the Commercial Agency at Monrovia.

Without hesitancy, and with sincere gratitude, I avail myself of this offer, and beg leave, through you, to convey to the Department of State, my prompt and cheerful acceptance thereof.

I do not see why this opening of providence should not lead me into a field which will, at once, incite a more rigourous, constant, and laudable activity of the noble attributes of the soul, and a more healthy exercise of the generous impulses of the heart.

 

When duly commissioned I shall hope to repair to my post of duty with a deep and steady purpose to aid in developing the resources of the young, struggling, but growing Republic; and if approved for diligence, fidelity, and competency, I may be permitted to indulge the hope that if an opportunity occurs for promotion I shall not be overlooked or forgotten.

I cannot deny myself the privilege of presenting to you my unfeigned thanks for the interest which you have taken in my case, especially amidst your multifarious cares and labors– and to the Hon Sec of State for the very considerate manner in which he has referred to me.

I have the honor to Remain
Your Obedient Servant
Abm Hanson
