Subject.

Cape Coast Castle, Consular Agency.

 

No. 33. Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia,
Aug., 1, 1899.

Hon. David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to say, that in my No. 21, Consular, of Feb'y 28, 1899, I gave an account of the condition of the Consular Agency, at Cape Coast Castle; adding a partial report of the exports and imports; including the number of American vessels, tonnage, number of crew &c, trading between the United States and the Gold Coast Colony, from July 1, 1897, to Feb'y 1, 1899, which partial report, I have, since,   since, supplemented, by an official statistical report from the Colonial Government, from July 1, 1897, to Feb'y 1, 1899. In my No. 21, Consular, I stated that if the Department, after a careful consideration of the bulk of the trade, between the United States and the Gold Coast Colony, decided it to be sufficient to justify the continuation of the Agency, I would undertake the responsibility of making a nomination for said Agency. In fact, in the absence of a proper blank application, for office, I did make a nomination; the which I have not, since, heard from. The party, thus recommended, I have recently heard from, who informed me that he has been   been having very bad health, for several months.

After waiting, a period of five months, for a reply; and not yet, being able to know the decision of the Department, in regards to said Agency; and knowing too, that I made only temporary arrangements for the care of the archives and other property of said Agency, I have thought it my duty to ask for a more definite understanding, relative thereto.

I have the honor to be
your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith,
M. R. and C. General.
