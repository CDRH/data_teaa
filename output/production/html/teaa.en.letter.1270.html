Subject.

The necessity for a Well, on the Legation premises.

 

No. 32, Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia;
July 25, 1899.

Hon. David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to say, that having experienced the difficulties, struggles and hardships of the last dry season here, without having a well, on the Legation, premises; in trying to obtain water from private wells, on other lots; and having some knowledge of the bad effects and dangers, in the use of impure and muddy water, I have requested of the Hon. R. H. Jackson the administrator of the estate of which the Legation premises, forms a part, to sink a well on the premises between this   this and the next dry season. He informs me, through his agent, Judge H. W. Travis, that if he sinks a well on the premises he will charge $350.00 per annum for rent, instead of $300.00 as now paid. The first years' rent contract will expire September 30, 1899. I would like to be advised before renewing. I will add, however, that as the location of the Legation is some what rocky, it is not certain that we can succeed in sinking a well, short of much blasting. In case we can it will hardly cost over fifty dollars; I would therefore, suggest the propriety of the Government authorizing me to have the well sunk, at a cost, not exceeding fifty dollars; as being much cheaper than having   having the administrator to have it done at an annual cost of fifty dollars, in additional rent.

Awaiting your advice and reply. I have the honor to be your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith,
M. R. and C. General.
