Department of State Received Nov 11 1879

No. 36.

Consulate General of the U.S.,
Monrovia, La, Oct. 7, 1879.

Mr. J. H. Smyth to the Ass't Sec'y of State.

Subject:

Circular No. 7.

Synopsis:

An acknowledgment of the reception of Circular No. 7 & reply.

 

No. 36.

Consulate General of the U.S.,
Monrovia, La, Oct. 7, 1879.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to inform you of the reception of your Circular No. 7, dated July 30, 1879, relative to "The Wealth of the United States", and to say in reply that, there does not exist in Monrovia nor any other portion of the Republic of Liberia, a board or boards of trade nor any like commercial institution or association.

While I cannot direct you to Hon. F. W. Seward,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.   to an organized commercial body to which such book should be sent, there are gentlemen engaged in commercial business here to whom the book should be sent when completed; since its influence in directing attention to the superiority of our products, and manufactured articles, would be advantageous to the increase of our commerce with Liberia.

With renewed assurances of esteem, I am, Sir,
Your obd't serv't
Jno. H. Smyth,
U.S. Consul Gen'l
