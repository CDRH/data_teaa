Department of State Received Aug 6 1887

No 4. Consulate Series
Monrovia June 24th 1887

Chas. H. J. Taylor to Hon Jas D Porter

Subject:

Reply to Consular Des. No (21) in ref. to Stephen Lynch.

 

No 4. Consulate Series
Monrovia June 24th 1887

Chas. H. J. Taylor to Hon Jas. D. Porter

Sir:

In reply, to your No 21. of date, May 17th 1887, in reference to Stephen Lynch I have to inform you, that he is dead. He arrived here in December 1855 1865 from Lynchburg Va, from this town he went to Careysburg where he lived until his death on a farm, belonging to him. He died in 1883 during the rainy season, of consumption. He had three boys living one named William aged 16, one Calvin aged 10 and one Stephen aged 8 years. His wife Jane Lynch, who came out with him from Virginia, is living, and has married   again to one Nelson Momen, who also came out with the emigration in 1865, from Lynchburg. She married this man shortly after her first husbands, decease. My informant is Robert Knuckles, who is the son of Jane Lynch, born in V'a and five years of age at the time he came here with his mother and stepfather.

Trusting that this may prove sufficient evidence, and information on the within subject, I have the honor to be, sir,
Your obedient servant
Chas. H. J. Taylor.
