No. 56.

U.S. Consulate
Monrovia Feb'y 10/87

Mr. S. S. Sevier
To the Ass't Secretary of State

Subject

Receipt of Cable Despatch. Bond Forwarded.

 

No. 56.

U.S. Consulate
Monrovia Feb'y 10/87

Hon. Jas. D. Porter
Ass't Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform you that your "Cable Despatch" sent to "Goree-Dakar" in order to reach me was received on the 7 of Feb'y 1887 after many days delay owing to the mail steamers which only stop at this port twice a month. I herewith inclose my   my bond, and would have sent it some time ago; but I thought it was not in my power to do so unless a regular appointed Minister was present to certify, I hope no harm will result from my delay.

Please intercede that I be detained during the two years administration of his excellency President Cleveland and I shall do my duty to the best of my ability. I hope my Bond will meet your   your approval.

I am, Sir,
Your obedient servant
Samuel S. Sevier
Acting U.S. Consul Gen.

Hon. Jas. D. Porter
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Inclosure

1. Bond of S. S. Sevier
