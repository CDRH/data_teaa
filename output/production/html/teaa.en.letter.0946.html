No. 29.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia
30. August 1877

J. Milton Turner

To the Department of State,
Washington

Subject:

Acknowledging the receipt of Consular instruction as contained in Despatch No. 12.

Abstract of contents:

Acknowledging the instruction No. 12. Expression of regret and assurances of increased dilligence in respect to the preparation of Consular despatches.

 

No. 29.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia
30. August 1877.

Sir;

I have had the honor to receive your No. 12. acknowledging my despatches Nos. 22. 23. and 24. and directing my attention to Article X of "Consular Regulations."

I regret that I may have been the unintentional cause of any delay or "embarrassment" to the routine of the Department and will hereafter observe the requirement Hon F. W. Seward
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington   of the Article of the Regulations to which your instruction makes reference.

I am,
Sir,
With expression of esteem,
Your obedient servant
J. Milton Turner

Enclosures
Nihil.
