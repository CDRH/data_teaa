Department of State Received Jan 29 1892

No 26.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, November 2, 1891.

Mr Beverly Y. Payne,
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Receipt of Circular.

 

No 26.

Consulate General of the United States.
Monrovia, Liberia, November 2, 1891.

Hon., William F. Wharton,
Acting Secretary of State.
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to acknowledge receipt of your Circular [SD, 8, '91 -1000] dated August 20, 1891, transmitting for my instruction and guidance a copy of an Executive Order, dated August 11, 1891, amending paragraph 664, of the Consular Regulations, relating to currency, copies of the paragraph and certificate (From No 144) adapted to the new requirement, all being in   in suitable shape for pasters[?] to go in their proper places in the respective copies of the Consular Regulation: which have my attention, and will be observed.

I have the honor to be,
Sir:
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne
U.S. Vice Consul.
