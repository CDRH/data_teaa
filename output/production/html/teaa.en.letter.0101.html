Department Of State Received Jul 20 1876
No 110

United States Commercial Agency
Loanda 20th May 1876.

Mr. Robert S. Newton
To the Department of State

Subject. Emancipation of Slaves in Angola.

Abstract of Contents

Abolition of the Liberto system and its probable effects, on the commerce of the Province. Its evil effects in St. Thomas, and introduction of Coast labour from Cape Palmas & other parts.

No 110

United States Commercial Agency
20th May 1876

The Assistant Secretary of State
Washington D.C.:

Sir,

I have the honor to report to the Department that Domestic Slavery, which has existed here for so long under the name of the liberto system; finishes here on the 17th July in accordance with the Decree published 12 months ago in the Bulletin Official of this Province.

The Portuguese Government published a Royal Decree in the "Diario do Governo" of 24 December 1875 making [illegible] certain arrangements for free labour for the future, which appears to me to imitate very much the Coolie system in use in India by which the planters of tea & indigo in Assam and the Northern provinces of India are supplied with labour and there is no doubt if the regulations set down in the Decree could be carried out here, that great benefits would arise to the whole Province, but unfortunately I have no confidence that such will be the case.

Several of the larger planters in the Cazengo District, with which I am best acquainted, have to a certain extent prepared their slaves by giving them their freedom and and allowing them so many days of the week for their own use, so that they can cultivate a piece of land for themselves, and as far as I can learn they seem perfectly satisfied and the Planters are not likely to suffer much in this part of the Province.

In the Island of St. Thomas, the case is very different; the wealth of that part of the Province consists entirely of coffee plantations which have been worked on the Liberto system or in plain words by slaves exported from Loanda and other parts. There, great discontent exists and the slaves who are not natives of the Island are not so easily dealt with They have lately nearly all left the plantations plantations and refused to work, so that the planters have been obliged to get Krooboys from Cape Palmas and other parts of the Coast. Nearly every English steamer has taken a large number of Krooboys to the Islands, who go there on contracts for 18 months and 2 years. No doubt the Krooboys will do well, but I am afraid that sufficient attention will not be given to their contracts, so that they are sent home when they finish their time.

The native slaves of the Province are naturally of a most careless and indolent disposition, and few will work unless compelled to do so by fear of punishment They They consider Domestic Slavery no disgrace, but rather the contrary especially when owned by a good master. I have known of instances where to keep them in order they have been threatened to be sent to the Government office to be made free, which has had quite the desired effect. No doubt this is the want of education, which up to the present has not been attended to in any way whatever.

As regards the actual and total abolition of slavery throughout the Province, I believe it to be for the moment impossible, as the principal wealth of the "Sobas" or Native Chiefs consists in the number of slaves they posses and the Portuguese Government have not sufficient power or or influence to make them resign what they consider their lawful property and the idea of a forced indemnification would not be listened to, as they would have no confidence in such an offer, even if they could understand it.

Often the slaves that are brought in for sale are the children of some one that has committed some small crime for which the family are condemned to death or to be sold so that in such a case it is a charity to buy them I am happy to say that, as far as my experience goes the greater part of the slaves in this part of the Province, are well treated which which no doubt is for the owner's interest.

When the Decree comes into force I will inform the Department further as to the effect of the same

I am,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
Robt S Newton
Vice Commercial Agent
