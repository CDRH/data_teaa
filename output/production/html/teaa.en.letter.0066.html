Recd Dec 22
No. 13

United States Commercial Agency
St Paul de Loanda October 19th 1865.

Sir

On the 24th of September, one Edward Baxter presented himself to this Commercial Agency and requested protection as a destitute American Seaman. He reported having deserted from the American Whaling Barque "Governor Carver" of Westport R.I. at Little Fish Bay for the reason that said Barque took in slaves at that port.

I called the attention of this Provincial Government to the case and requested the favor of the Government to send this Seaman to Little Fish Bay on board of the Portuguese Corvette "Infanto D. João[?]" that the Commander might Hon William H. Seward
Secretary of State
Washington, D.C.   might on the spot investigate the true merits of the case that both the honor of Portugal and the United States might be properly vindicated. My request was complied with and Baxter sailed in said Corvette on the 12th inst.

Notwithstanding my apprehension of the veracity of this Statement, I took all the necessary steps to sift out the truth and by the following month's mail, I will forward to the Department copies of all correspondence bearing on this case; In the meantime the Corvette will return better qualifying me to place before you reliable information.

I have the honor
To Be
Your most obedt Servant,
Augustus Archer Silva
Commercial Agent
