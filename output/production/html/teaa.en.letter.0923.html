No. 10.

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia December 22nd 1875

Sir

I have the honor to transmit marked inclosures Nos. 1. and 2. copies of correspondence with Mr Crusoe the Commercial Agent at Grand Bassa relating to the Register of the barque "Jasper" which was inadvertently left on a table at this Consulate, and which was transmitted to him for delivery to the master of said barque.

I have the honor to be
Sir,
With high consideration
Your obedient servant
J Milton Turner

Honorable
John L. Cadwalader
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington U.S.A.
 

Inclosure No. 1

Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia, December 4th, 1875.

Sir

I transmit herewith in care of Hon. Walker Brohm, Consul of the German Empire to be placed by you in the papers of the American Barque Jasper the Register of said Barque which J. B. Williams, Master of the Jasper left accidentally upon a table in my office on the evening of the 3d December after my delivery of the Jasper's papers into his possession. Should it transpire that you have delivered to the Jasper at Bassa, her papers, prior to the delivery to you of this note, and she has not yet departed for Cape Palmas, then you will be pleased to hand the Register into the possession of Capt. Williams and H. Lafayette Crusoe
U.S. Commercial Agent
Edina Grand Bassa   and exhibit to him this instruction. If the Jasper shall have departed from Bassa prior to your receipt of this despatch, you will be pleased to return to me by the safe means within your reach, without delay, this Register of the Jasper. In any case, it is desired that you acknowledge this despatch forthwith. It is desired if you have not already reported the same to the Government Washington, that you will compile and transmit to this Legation the report required by the paragraph numbered 380 of the Consular Regulations.

I have the honor to be,
Sir
Your obedient Servant
J Milton Turner
US Minister

 

Inclosure No. 2.

Commercial Agency of the U.S.
Grand Bassa Dec 15, 1875.
Hon. J. M. Turner
U.S. Minister & Con Genl
Monrovia, Liberia

Sir.

I beg the honor to acknowledge receipt of your despatch under date of the 4th inst. enclosing Register of the american bark "Jasper" which reached her 11th inst. in time to enable me to execute your instructions in respect to it. In obedience to your further instruction I shall compile and transmit to you the report required by paragraph 380. of the Consular Regulations. Finding it to be impossible, for want of time, to send forward my reports regularly I shall likely have to soon as I have reported fully resign the post of U.S. Com. Agt.

I have the honor to be, Sir,
Your obedient humble servant
(signed) H. L. Crusoe
U.S. Com. Agent.
