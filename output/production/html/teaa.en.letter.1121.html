Department of State Received Dec 16 1889

No. 40.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, November 5th 1889.

Mr. Ezekiel E. Smith,
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Archives of the United States at Grand Bassa and Cape Palmas.

 

No. 40.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, November 5th 1889.

Honorable William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to call the attention of the Department to the vacancies that exist in the Agencies of the United States at Cape Palmas and Grand Bassa.

It appears that formerly our Government had Agents at the points mentioned, and that at the present, there remains at them some of the Government's archives.

Having not visited these ports; nor having been advised respecting the quantity or condition of the archives, which there may be at Grand Bassa and Cape Palmas, I do not know in what they consist.

I am not informed as to the extent   of exports from the points in question to the United States, therefore, make no recommendation with reference to the appointment of Agents there.

I shall await the pleasure of the Department for instruction as regards the archives to which allusion is above made.

I am, sir,
your obedient servant
Ezekiel E. Smith,
U.S. Consul General.
