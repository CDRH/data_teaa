No 2

Sierra Leone Sept. 19th 1879.

J. A. Lewis To the Dept of State.

Subject. Acknowledgement. Condition of Consulate-Vice Consul. Department Dispatch No 3 duly received. The Consular Archives in a very bad and disorganized conditions No papers filed. No Furniture nor case for Books. Will furnish all and desire to know if Dept. will pay for same.

The present Vice Consul of no use to the Consulate and Capt. M. W. Tobey recommended for the appointment.

 
No 2.

United States Consulate at Sierra Leone Sept 19th 1879.

Hon F. W. Seward
Asst. Secy of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have to acknowledge the receipt of your Dispatch No 3. under date of the 15th Ultimo endorsing to me Special Passport No 538 and under separate cover a copy of the Consular Regulations 1874 together with a Register of the Department.

Having taken charge of the U.S. Consulate here on the 16th Instant. I have to Report, that I find the Archives of the office in a very bad and disorganized condition. No letters nor papers of any kind have been filed or bound in the past five years or more but thrown in a box like waste.   There are no chairs, table, nor desk, belonging to the Consulate not even a cheap case for the Books (which are large leather bound and expensive) but they are allowed to be thrown about and liable to damage in various ways. I purpose to have all papers properly filed and bound and to have a suitable case made for the protection of the U.S. Consular Books. shall also provide suitable table and chairs for the office will see the propriety of the above action on my part and allow me the actual expense attending the same. In accordance with Par. 353 of the Consular Regulations I would respectfully ask permission of the Dept for the procuring of these necessary articles to become permanently the property of the Consulate.

 

In accordance with the Regulations Par. 13. and 19. and instructions received from the Department under date of Aug 15th I would respectfully make the following statement and recommendation regarding the Vice Consul.

The present Vice Consul Mr. Rudolph Camelberg never has been and never can be of any possible use to this Consulate. from the fact he has not resided at this place during the past two years and is now residing at Sherbro more than 60 miles distant. I would therefore respectfully recommend for Vice Consul subject to the approval of the Department Captain Marcellus William Tobey an American citizen who has been in the Merchantile business here for the past four years. he is a ship Master 38 years of age   age and was born in Massachusetts (and appointed from same state) He is now absent in the United States but will soon be returning to this place. If the Dept. approves the above recommendation I would suggest the sending of his blank Bond and Instructions to his address No 115 Wall Street New York care of Mess. Yates and Porterfield and if he (Tobey) has not already sailed he will furnish the required Bond at once and in case he has sailed Mess. Y. and P. will forward the same to him.

I send you herewith Inventory of the U.S. Consulate at Sierra Leone.

I am Sir Your Obt Servt
J. A. Lewis
U.S. Consul
