Rec 29 Jany

Bathurst River Gambia
W C of Africa
22d December 1857

Sir

I purpose showing your Excellency that the commerce of the United States with this place & the welfare of American citizens require the establishment of an American Consulate at this port.

During the year about to end there have been Thirty one American vessels here & many [?] sharing the need of some one to care for & empowered to protect American interests.

Seamen have been discharged from their ships against their will & without their 3 months advance, have sickened & died, & once have been left behind in the Colonial Hospitals & upon their recovery have ascertained that their ship had left & in one case taking away the clothes of the seaman.

A case has thus come under my [?]. A Mr Lee 2d Mate of the Bark Turk of Salem Mass was discharged from his vessel without his 3 months wages. Came to me in distress. I gave him employment. it was during the sickly season. he was soon taken ill with the African fever. I obtained lodging for him, sent my own Doctor to treat him (whose Bill I now enclose hoping that I may be reimbursed) upon his recovery there being no American vessels here I obtained a passage for him to London.

The late Commander in Chief of the Squadron Commodore Crable[?] was[?] quite aware of the necessity of a Consulate & [?] me that he has communicated with your Excellency relative thereto & has recommended me for that post.

I [?] that your Excellency will deign to give your attention to this matter.

I am
Your Excellency's
Most Obdt. & humble Servant
Danl R. B. Upton

To His Excellency
The Honorable Lewis Cass
Secretary of State
