Department of State Received Jan 2 1885

United States Consulate
Loanda 24th October 1884.

Sir

I have the honor to report for the information of the Department that a contract was entered into between the Portuguese Government and Thadeu de Okska Orzechuostic on the 9th of July last to lay a Telegraph Cable between Senegal, Bolama, St Thomas and Loanda with a branch line between Bolama & Bissau. The whole to be completed within 18 months from date of contract. The line between Senegal Bolama and Bissau to be done within 6 months and the remainder of the line to Loanda within the following 12 months. The Contractor also obliges himself to lay a Cable from St Vincent to St Thiago, Cape de Verde   Verde Islands. I hear by last steamer that this is nearly completed.

The Portuguese Government guarantee the Contractor the exclusive privilege for 40 years and grant them the lands that may be required free of expense, also guaranteeing a subsidy corresponding to 46,000 words between Bolama and Europe and vice verse 14000 between St Thomas and Europe and vice versa and 90,000 between Loanda and Europe and vice verse, for one year. The charge for each word not to exceed 3/- for Bolama 5/- for St Thomas and 7/- for Loanda.

In the event of the French or Spanish Governments interrupting the communication by way of the line of Senegal and Canaries the Contractor obliges himself   himself to make another line in the smallest possible line, connecting the Coast with St Thiago, Cape de Verde Islands so as to put the communication direct with Europe Should the Government desire it they can oblige the Contractor to make the line touch at the Congo under the guarantee of 40000 words between that region and Europe at a charge of 6/- per word

The other items of the Contract are of no importance

I have the honor to be
Your most obedient Servant
Robt S. Newton
US Vice Consul

Assistant Secretary of State
Washington D.C.
