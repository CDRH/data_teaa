Department of State Received Aug 28 1890

Muscatine Iowa Aug 25, 1890

To the Secretary of State - or Acting Sec. Wm F Wharton

Washington DC

Sir; I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of the dispatch of the department of August 20, 1890 apprising me of my appointment as Minister Resident and Consul General of the United States at Monrovia Liberia.

In answer thereto I beg to express my acceptance of said appointment; agreeable to your request for such information I have to State that I was born in Washington County Washington State of Pennsylvania and am now a resident of Muscatine City and County State of Iowa from which latter State I have been appointed I have never resided in Liberia or any of its dependencies; I have this day subscribed and sworn to the Oath of office and beg to enclose the same with this dispatch I have also executed my official bond with the proper sureties and have this day forwarded the same to Hon Walter I Hayes member of congress for this district for his approval with the request that he will deliver that same to the department; awaiting the further pleasure of the department I have the honor to remain your obednt servnt

Alexander Clark

Daily Journal

Monday, August 25, 1890.

The Saturday Review of Des Moines prints a portrait and biography of A. Clark and calls him "the Fred. Douglass of the West." At the African M. E. Conference in Galesburg, Ill., last week, Mr. Clark, who was a delegate, was formally introduced for remarks by Dr. Derrick, of New York, the missionary secretary, and made a speech which the published report says showed that "he has the faculty of saying a great deal in a very interesting way." The conference adopted the following resolution:

Resolved. That this meeting heartily endorse the appointment of Hon. Alexander Clark, our distinguished fellow-citizen, as Minister-Resident and Consul-General to the Republic of Liberia, which is another evidence of President Harrison's intention to recognize the citizenship of the American people, regardless of race, color or previous condition.

Muscatine's Diplomats.

A. Clark and S. McNutt have received formal notice of their appointment and have executed and returned the necessary bonds. The bond in the case of Mr. Clark is $6,000 and of Mr. McNutt $5,000. The former has 70 days in which to prepare himself and make the journey to Monrovia and the latter fifty-five days to reach Maracaibo. This to date from approval of the bond. The regulations provide that the sufficiency of the bond must be endorsed by the U.S. Attorney for the district, one of the U.S. Senators from Iowa or the Congressman from this district. In this case each of the appointees forwarded his bond to Congressman Hayes for endorsement.

While Mr. McNutt's bond is a strong one and ample in every respect, Mr. Clark's is probably the strongest ever given by any one in Muscatine county, as its four signers represent considerably more than a million dollars.
