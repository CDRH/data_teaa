All these should be well starched or sized much more so than the finer goods as they are all for the Barter Trade. There is great competition in this trade, so often they come a yard or so shorter, and an inch or so narrow, but all doubled to imitate goods of 18 yards which used to be the regulation length

Great attention should be paid to the regularity of the pieces in each Bale, and to the packing.

Many qualities of Handkerchiefs also are in use, ranging from 12 to 15 Handkerchiefs per price. However I do not send sample of these as the quality is so varied, and nearly all styles and qualities pass.

The duty on the above mentioned Cotton Goods is as follows:

and 4% more on the value here, which is usually calculated at about 30% over the Invoice   Invoice price.

When these goods come via Lisbon or from Lisbon in a Portuguese vessel they pay 70% of the beforementioned duties. Manufactured in Portugal and bringing a Certificate to that effect they pay only 20% of the beforementioned duty.

Should they come in Foreign Vessels from any other Country they pay the full amount of duty as previously stated.

Shortly a new Tariff is to be brought into force which will increase the duty on Cotton goods considerably.

As soon as I can obtain a copy I will send it to the Department with a translation.

Nearly all cotton goods come from Liverpool and London in steamer to Lisbon and thence in the Portuguese steamers. At Lisbon they pay a small duty which allows them to pass here paying only 70% of the duty.

The expenses via Lisbon from England including carriage from Manchester to Liverpool Entry & Cartage, freight & [?] by Steamers to Lisbon, & afterwards to this port.

Duty in Lisbon without commission is about 14% to 15%.

All   All these goods are used in the Province of Angola, but at Ambriz and the North the sizes vary, and the trade is different.

My information is taken from different Invoices received from by Mercantile Houses established here.

The Portuguese Steamers leave Lisbon on the 5th of every month for this port touching at the other Portuguese Colonies.

There are also two lines of Steamers from Liverpool, but it does not suit to ship Cotton Goods by these on account of the extra 30% duty the Consignees would have to pay.

Nearly all goods that come here are in execution of orders on account and risk of the Merchants here.

Consignments are not large although a well assorted establishment might do a good trade, as often the traders run short of different qualities in their assortments.

The usual Commissions are 5% for selling and 2% for remitting in Bills and 4% for remitting in produce. Some Houses however do the whole business for 5% commission without guarantee & 8% with   with guarantee.

It is difficult to give the number of pieces used during a year as no statistical statement of same is kept in the Custom House.

However I know of Houses here that use from 40 to 50,000 pieces in a year.

The trade is nearly all in the hands of the Portuguese, and it would be difficult to get orders from this market.

Lately travellers have been here from England and Germany to Solicit orders but have done little or nothing.

The Exchange is Rs 4500 per £1 sterling, or 920 Reis per Dollar.
