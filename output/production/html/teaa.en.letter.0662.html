Department of State Received Jul 1 1884

United States Consulate
St. Paul de Loanda
3 May 1884.

Sir

I am advised of the non-payment of the two Bills No 7 $350 and No 8 for $174 drawn in my favor by Consul Du Verge on the Secretary of the Treasury on the ground that he was in a condition not responsible for his actions at the time of signing.

I beg to enclose copy of a despatch I find has been inserted in the Official Correspondence Book by Mr Du Verge advising the drawing of one of these bills.

These Bills were sent to me by Mr Du Verge from Banana in a letter of which I have the honor to transmit a copy.

While Mr Du Verge was in the Congo he ordered in his capacity as chief of the Station at Vivi, of the Belgians Association. Several articles which were charged to him and as it will be seen by the enclosed Copy of Account, there remained a Balance after payment of his Bills, which I did not for a moment doubt, due to him of Â£30:2:4 for which I remitted to him, by the packet of the 15th of last month month a Bill on my Agents. The payment of that Bill I have ordered to be stopped in the usual way until further reasons are given for the non-payment of the said two Bills by the Secretary of the Treasury.

There will I venture to believe be very little doubt in your mind that the allegation set forth by Mr Du Verge is a vile invention to serve his own ends and an act inconsistent with integrity and honesty. If therefore Mr Du Verge cannot be called upon by the Government to make good the Amount he is now indebted to me I shall be under the painful necessity of prosecuting him by law

I may state that there are several persons residents in this city who saw Mr Du Verge at Banana in the Congo previous to his departure for Europe as well as the Captain of the Steamer in which he took passage could state that he did not appear to be in any way irresponsible for his acts.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your most Obedient Servant
Robt S Newton
U.S. Vice Consul

To the
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington DC.

Black Point Nov. 26th 83

Robert S. Newton Esq.
U.S. Vice Consul
St Paul de Loanda

My dear Sir

I send this by the SS Benguella, Capt. Brown and hope it will reach you

Before my departure from Banana I gave to Mr Bloeme two cheques on Hon: Secretary of the Treasury No 7. $350 and No 8. $174 both to your order, after paying yourself of $50 per month for your trouble and deducting the amount paid to Marti and for the firebars piston cover lamp shade and seed you will please forward the balance to me in a cheque "30 days if you please" to No 1 Sefton Grove, Sefton Park, care of Chas Williams Liverpool as I will remain there for some time before going home. Dispose of my maxilla also, every cent I can get now is needed my trip and continuous sickness are breaking me down

Good luck to you and good health health

Ever Yours,
(SS) L. de R du Verge.

P.S.

Letters directed to me you will please also send to Sefton Grove, Sefton Park Liverpool
