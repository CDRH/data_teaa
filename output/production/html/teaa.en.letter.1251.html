Subject.
Cape Coast Castle Agency.

 

No. 12, Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia;
Nov. 16, 1898.

Hon. Thos. W. Cridler,
Third Assistant Secretary,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your No. 8, of Oct. 6, 1898, which was received Nov. 15, 1898, assuring me that the question of the retention of the Consular Agency, at "Cape Coast Castle," would be held in abeyance, until I shall have had opportunity to make a careful investigation, in order, to show the necessity, for a further continuation of said Agency &c. In reply, I beg to say, that in my No. 2, "Consular," of Aug., 29, 1898, I endeavored to   to show, that for the want of a correspondent, at Cape Coast, I was unable to give the desired information; but from prospective causes, expressed a hopeful future for said Agency.

In my No. 28, "Diplomatic," which should have been "Consular," of Sept., 22, 1898, I suggested the propriety of visiting said Agency, in person, during the stay of a United States' Cruiser, in these waters, provided the Department thought my suggestion to send one here, during the month of December, next well founded.

I am very reliably informed, that Cape Coast, is 11 days' distance or over 2000 miles from Monrovia thus   thus entailing an expense of $75.00 or $100.00. But in case the Department think it not wise to send a Cruiser to these waters, with the instructions to take me to Cape Coast, I will visit the said Agency, early in 1899, and will report according to my personal observations.

I have the honor to be your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith
