Liberia January 10th 1891;
Mr Alexander Clark:
To the Department of State;

Subject

recepts of Circulars and reply;

Synopsis

 

No 9. Consulate General Series;
United States Legation Monrovia.
Liberia January 10th 1891;
To the
Honorable William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington DC;

Sir;

I have the honor to respectfully acknowledge the recept of your circulars of November 3rd and 8th 1890 marked (SD. 11, 3:90-500) (SD. 11, 11:-90-1000) Agricultural Department with there accompanying Inclosures viz

Regulation for inspecting cattle;
" " " Salt meets;
Also circular marked (SD 11, 8-90-1000 viz. Street and Roadways;

In reply I am sorrow to say that there are no cattle or salt meets exported from Monrovia or any part of the Republic of Liberia as per circulars marked (SD. 11, 3, 90-500)   or (SD. 11, 11-90-1000;

Indeed live stock of all kind are of a very infearior kind; The cows weigh from 4 to 5 hundred giving from a pint to pint and one half of milk Sheep, goats are small weighing from 20 to 50 pounds. hogs weight from one pound to 125. chicken are small no horses in the country or a least in Monrovia;

Circular marked (SD 11, 8, 90-1000) Streets and roadways &c;[?]

Monrovia Liberia though the Capital of the Republic with a population variously estimated from 3000 to 5000 have not a Street or alley paved; The Streets and alleys are nicly covered with grass and weeds intermingle with beautiful wild flowers. the side walks are in places cleared by cutting out the grass The city of Monrovia is situated at an altitude of 300 to 400 feet above the level of the sea. on a rock covering the intire sight on which the city rests[?]   There are no waggons wheelbarrows; go-cart or wheel vehicle of any kind used in the city or country [unclear, one word] The houses are brick and fram in the city proper mostly two storys high, enclosed by stone fences The two Suburban towns, viz Kroo Town and Vie Town are a part of the corporate limits of Monrovia The people of these two precincts are Natives, viz Kroo-men and Vie-men; The Post office is in Kroo Town, which to reach it make a nice little walk of 2½ miles from the center of the city proper. The Kroo and Vie houses are made of thatches with dirt floors, and my opinion is that they are healthier than the brick home. The Legation building are the finest and best built house in the city but like all the brick houses it is damp and unhealthy and   and there are no exception to the houses being damp in the rainy season which commences in October and holdes till the first of April. I am satisfied that for central location looks and convienece no better house could be secured, and as the lease of it by Mr John H Smyth had expired some two years ago and no released renewal of said lease I have re-leased it upon the same terms for two years from Jany first (1) 1891, which I hope will meet your approval &c I have the honor to be your obedient servant

Alexander Clark
