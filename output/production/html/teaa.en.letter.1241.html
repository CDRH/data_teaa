William H. Heard
To the Department of State

Subject

Advice of the demise of Hattie Gibson of Martinsburg W. Va.

 

Legation of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, Dec. 22, 1896

Hon. Richard Olney,
Secretary of State
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to perform the painful duty of advising you of the death of Miss Hattie Gibson of Martinsburg, W. Va. U.S.A. who was sent out here in Oct. 1895, as a teacher by the "Colonization Society." She has spent much of the time at Muhlenberg Mission in Montserrado County on the St. Paul River, teaching sewing. The cause of her death, the certificate of the physician says was blood poisoning

I am obediently
William H. Heard
Minister Res. and Consul General

 

I am very obediently

Minister Res. and Consul General
