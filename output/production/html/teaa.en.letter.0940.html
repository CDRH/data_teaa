No. 23.

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia, Liberia, April 16th 1877.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform the Department that I have secured and occupied on the first day of April, at the rate of Twenty five dollars $25. per quarter, or one hundred dollars $100. per annum, two -2- eligible rooms for the office purposes of the Legation and Consulate General. This price is fifty dollars $50. per annum less than I have paid for rent during the past two years. Under Honorable John L. Cadwalader,
Assistant Secreatry of State,
Washington U.S.A.   under date the 9th instant I drew against the contingent fund allowed to this post to the amount of Twenty five 75/100 dollars $25.75/100 as shown by voucher No. 1., to defray the cost of moving the Archives. And, as may be seen by Voucher No. 2. I have drawn draft dated April 15. with which I have paid the rent of present quarter.

I have the honor to be
Sir:
With high consideration and esteem,
Your obedient servant
J Milton Turner
