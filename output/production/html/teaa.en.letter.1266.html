Subject.

An import duty of 8 cts per pound on American tobacco.

 

No. 27. Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia,
April 6, 1899.

Hon. David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

In response to Circular letter of Jan. 17, 1899, Department of State, Washington, relative to "restrictions put upon the sale of American tobacco," and in reply to my dispatch of Feb'y 28, 1899, to Department of State, Monrovia, I have the honor to herewith transmit to you the intelligence, that I am this day in receipt of a dispatch from the Department of State, Monrovia, informing me that "no restriction are imposed   imposed upon the importation of that article save the payment of an import duty of eight cents per pound."

I have the honor to be
your obedient servant,
Owen L. W. Smith
