Department of State Received Dec 27 1879

No. 40.

Consulate Gen'l of the U.S.,
Monrovia, Liberia, Nov. 15, '79

Mr. J. H. Smyth to the Ass't Sec'y of State.

Subject:

Circular.

Synopsis:

As to Circular numbered 16.

 

No. 40.

Consulate Gen'l of the U.S.,
Monrovia, Liberia,
November 15, 1879.

Sir:

I have the honor to state respectfully the receipt of your Circular No. 16, date Aug. 27, 1879, which directs that I furnish "the Department a brief statement of condition of the archives of my Consulate at the present time." In reply please accept the following:

1. To the first query, I answer– Yes. 2 To the second– Yes. 3 To the third– No. 4 To the fourth–that, there is no original Hon. F. W. Seward,
Ass't Sec'y of State,
Washington, D.C.   original correspondence here in any language other than English–except a translation from the Arabic–of a "book" (dispatch or letter), sent by the President of Liberia to Ibrahima Sissi, King of Musardu, the same having been prepared by Hon. E. W. Blyden. 5 To the 5th–Yes.

6 As to suggestions as to care of records–none.

I need one record book, "Notes to Minister of Foreign Relations," and two record books, one for diplomatic and one for consular dispatches

Not having received a copy of the "Revised Consular Regulations   Regulations", I cannot direct my attention to paragraphs mentioned.

With assurances of high consideration,
I am, Sir,
Your very obd't serv't,
Jno. H. Smyth,
U.S. Consul Gen'l
