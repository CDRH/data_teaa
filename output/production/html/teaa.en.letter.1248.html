Subject.

The nomination of William F. Hutchison, a British subject, of Cape Coast, Gold Coast Colony, Africa; to be United States Commercial Agent, at Cape Coast Castle, in lieu of the late George E. Eminsang Esq., deceased.

 

No. 9 Consular Series.
Consulate General of the
United States, Monrovia Lib.
Oct. 22, 1898.

Hon. J. B. Moore,
Assistant Secretary of
State; Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to say that in answer to a letter of July 8, 1898, from J. E. Eminsang Esq. of Almina Gold Coast Colony, Africa, informing me of the death of his father George E. Eminsang Esq., late United States, Commercial Agent, at Cape Coast Castle; and in the same letter petitioning to be appointed his father's successor, as United States Commercial Agent, at Cape Coast Castle,   I did on the 11th day of August 1898, appoint the said J. E. Eminsang Esq., temporarily; United States Commercial Agent, at the Cape Coast Castle, Agency, with a request that I hear from him at an early date. In reply to my request and appointment, I have not, yet heard from him; I therefore, think him either too slow or careless to fill so responsible position, and de herewith take pleasure in nominating William F. Hutchison Esq., a British subject of Cape Coast; Gold Coast Colony, Africa; a gentleman who is very highly recommended to me, as a man of fine parts, possessed of eminent business qualities qualities, to fit him for the position of United States   Commercial Agent, at Cape Coast Castle. I therefore, nominate Mr. William F. Hutchison, for the position and hope the nomination may so meet your approval, as to insure an early return of the blank oath of office, accompanied with a blank official bond.

I have the honor to be your obedient servant,
Owen L. W. Smith

Inclosure.
A letter from William F Hutchison, Cape Coast

 

15 Guilford Street
Russell Square W. C.
London
30th September 1898.

His Excellency
Owen L: W: Smith.
Minister Resident and Consul-General U.S.A.
Monrovia.

Dear Sir,

Your esteemed favor of the 15th August 1898 has been forwarded to me here.

Mr. G. E. Eminsang died since I left the Gold Coast for this country but I have been informed of his death by my friends residing in the same town, and by the notices in the local and English Press.

Mr. J. E. Eminsang, your correspondent, is the son of the late Mr. G. E. Eminsang, and is a youth of good character, but otherwise I know nothing of him.

I am willing to accept the appointment of United States Consular Agent for the Gold Coast Colony. I presume that the emoluments enjoyed by the late Mr. Eminsang will be continued to me.

I have the honor to be
Sir,
your obedient servant
W. F. Hutchison

Please address reply to me at Cape Coast to which place I shall shortly return.
