Department of State Received Jan 3 1872

Commercial Agency of the United States of America at Sierra Leone
25th November 1871

Sir

I have the honor to inform you that since Mr. J. B. Upton's departure for the United States for the recovery of his health which was much impaired by long residence in this country, I have acted by his appointment in his absence.

I have notified the Government of this Colony of my own intended departure for England and the United States, and named T. J. Aldridge Esqr to act in my absence. an acceptance of the appointment has been received, a copy of which I have the honor to forward to your Excellency.

I shall deem it a duty to call upon your Excellency at Washington, should I visit the United States as I intend to do.

I have the honor to remain
Your Excellency's Obdt Servant
William Hogan
U.S. Consular Agent

To the Honorable
Secretary of State
Washington.
