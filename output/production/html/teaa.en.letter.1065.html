Department of State Received Apr 28 1888

No 6.

Consulate General of the United States.
Monrovia, March 27th 1888

Mr B. Y. Payne to the Acting Secretary of State

Subject.

Receipt of Despatch No 11. Dip' Ser

Sir.

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your No 11. Diplomatic Series, of January 27th 188 with enclosures relative to the mortality among and the condition of Liberian laborers in the Panama Canal.

This despatch was delayed at the Leeward, but immediately after its receipt, I transmitted copies of your enclosures enclosures   enclosures to the Government of Liberia, and I take pleasure in enclosing to you, copy of Secretary Barclay's reply, which I am just in receipt of.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne.

Hon.,
Alvey A. Adee,
Act'g Sec'y
&c &c &c

No 3.

Department of State.
Monrovia, March 27th 1888.
Liberia, W. C. A.

Sir.

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your communication of the 20th inst., with enclosures relative to the mortality among the African laborers employed on the Panama Canal. Works, and the desire of the Liberian portion of said laborers to return to their homes as soon as possible as is evinced in their letter addressed to the United States Consul General resident at Panama.

In reply, I am to inform you, that this Department Department   Department is in receipt of information that measures are being taken for the speedy return of whole Liberian laborers from the Isthmus of Panama to their homes in this Republic.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
Sd, E. J. Barclay.

Beverly Y. Payne Esq.,
U.S. Act'g Vice Consul Genl.
Monrovia.
