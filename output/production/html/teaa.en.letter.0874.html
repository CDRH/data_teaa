Department of State Received Mar 5 1881

No. 47. U.S. Consulate at Sierra Leone Feb 5th 1881.

Judson A. Lewis - To Dept State.

Threatened Ashantee War

 

No. 47. U.S. Consulate at
Sierra Leone Feb. 5th 1881.

Hon Jay Hay
Ass't Sect'y State
Washington

Sir:

I have to inform the Department, of the threatening aspect of affairs on the "Gold Coast" - as the King of Ashantee has again declaired war on the English Colonial Government Headquarters at Accra.

It is generally thought in Some[?] official circles, that if the Ashantees fight again, they will be a still more formidable foe than before - for it is believed they are well armed with breech loaders and not a few pieces of artillery - they have the reputation of being brave - standing up and   receiving the bullets square in their breasts.

On Friday Jan 28th the Mail Steamer "Corisco" arrived in Port from the Gold Coast at least two days ahead of time - having been specially chartered by the English Colonial Government to come with all speed to Sierra Leone making no stop on the way in order to bring the news of the threatened attack by the King of Ashantee.

First outside of Cape Sierra Leone - the S.S. Corisco Signalled the S.S. Cameroon bound down the Coast to return to Sierra Leone for the purpose of taking all the available troops from this place - after stopping an hour at Sierra Leone for the mails - the Corisco hurried on to make the run as quickly   and place himself under British protection.

It is reported that the Gamans have paid more than £800. as ransom for this captive Prince on the repeated promises of the Ashantees to give him up, but no sooner do they receive the ransom money, than they break their pledges.

The Kings demands were refused by His Excellency the Governor and we understand a counter demand made upon the King to pay up the instatment of indemnity which it appears he has failed to pay.

Every war like preparation is now hastily being made and the prospects for war are as good as they have been in former times, when severe fighting was the result.
