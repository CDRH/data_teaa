No. 48.

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia Jan. 11th/87

Mr. S. S. Sevier
To the Sect'y of State.

Subject

Trouble in the Republic of Liberia.

Abstract of Contents.

Disloyalty of the native tribes at Half-Cavally. Action of the Twentieth Legislature. Peace or war at the will of the President.

 

No. 48.

Consulate General of the U.S.
Manrovia Jan 11th/87

Hon. T. F. Bayard
Sect'y of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to inform you that there has been considerable trouble in the Southeast Extremity of the County of Maryland which is one of the Counties of the Republic of Liberia, with the tribe called the Grebo dwelling at Half-Cavally. The trouble arises   arises thus–The natives at Half-Cavally refuse to be loyal to the government in the payment of Import Duties. They feel that they should not be made to pay on merchandise foreign adventures and merchants bring into there port of entry, nor do they feel they should pay on what they have imported into there own domain. The difficulties existing there have raised the feelings of loyal citizens very much–in so much that the setting of the Twentieth Legislature of the   the Republic steps were taken to quail their unwarrented privileges either by treaty of peace or by war as the President of the Republic sees fit. I have not yet fully been able to get into the truth of the difficulties existing owing to the irreguality of the maid steamers stopping at this port; but only here and there an outline I have been able to obtain. War I believe is inevitable. I shall inform the Department of future steps taken by this government I hope in my next

 

I am Sir
Your obedient servant
Saml S. Sevier
Acting U.S. Consul General.
