Dept of State Recd Nov 26 '66

U.S. Comcl Agency, Gaboon Sep 25 /66

To the Hon. Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir: I have the honor to transmit the accompanying "Circular", laws affecting the Commerce of this port, hitherto free.

I learn from an English trader, who yesterday had an interview with the Commandant Superior, that export & import duties of 4 per cent, Anchorage duty of one franc per ton, also Pilotage & light-house fees are to be imposed. The export duty, as explained by the Commandant, will apply to African produce, brought into Gaboon from other ports, in a ship coming here to complete her cargo, as many of the American vessels do.

I am very respectfully your obedient servant
J. M. Preston.
U.S. Vice Comcl Agent.

Circular.

Messieurs les commençants sont prévenus que par suite d'une réduction opérés sur la somme que la Colonie recevait de la Métropole pour ses dissendes locales, ils seront soumis a partir du 1er Janvier prochain au paiement de droits annuels de patente et de licence, dont le tarif doit être établi aux chiffres suivants;

Savoir:

Patente de Négociant 800 ₣
" de Marchand détaillant 400
Licence de Cabaretier et de tous autres vendant, au détail, des boissons dans leur établissements 600

Me arrêté qui interviendra ultérieurement déterminera les conditions du classement du Commerçants dans ces 3 catégories.

Gabon, le 21 Septembre 1866.

Circular

The Merchants are informed that in consequence of a reduction affecting the sum which the Colony receives from the Metropolis for its local expenses, they will be, from the first of January 1867, subjected to the payment of annual duties of patent and licence, the tariff of which has been established according to the following figures; to wit:

A patent for a wholesale dealer 800 francs
" a retail 400
A Licence for a tavern[?] or to retail liquors 600

An agreement which will follow hereafter will determine the conditions of classification of the Merchants in these 3 categories

Gaboon Sep. 21, 1866
