Commercial Agency
Kongo Free State
Bena-Kamba, Lomami River
April 17th, 1893.

To the Honorable
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to report my arrival at Bena-Kamba April 10th & that on the 14th joining the Expedition against the Arab Slave dealers. As I desired greatly to see the part of the Kongo Free State occupied by the Arabs, it being considered the richest and most populous and as it was impossible to visit this this section unless with a strong expedition, Commandant Chaltin invited me to go with him as a guest, and as stated in my letter of March 17th (no number) I accepted. Upon arrival at Bena-Kamba he told me three of his officers were obiged to return to Basoko, sick, one with small pox, one with dysentery & one with malignant hemateuric fever & asked me if, as a favor, I would act as one of his officers. Under the peculiar circumstances I accepted the proposition, and am commanding his two Krupp Field Cannon and one hundred soldiers. Of course it is quite understood that I shall not accept any remuneration from the Kongo Free State.

This Lomami River was opened last year in the month of March by the "Syndicat du Katanga" of Brussels with Mr. A. Hodister at its head. They established established. Trading Stations, or Factoryo, at the following [?] Yanga, Bena-Kamba & Lomo in the Lomami, and at Kibongo Riba-Ruba and Nyangone in the High Kongo, or Loulaba River. After these places were founded and stocked with goods to the value of over $250,000.00 the Arabs attacked them, killing the agents at Loma & Riba-Riba & Mr. Hodister with four white companions on the road from Bena Kemba to Riba-Ribe. The other agents at the different factories heard this and made their escape to Stanley Falls. The State agent at Riba-Riba was tortured to death. All the factories were burned and the cloths, beads, money etc taken.

This January they have killed the two State Agents at Kassongo, so the Government organized one Expedition from Lusambo in the Sankuru River to proceed against Nyangone & Kassongo & one to go from Basoko to the Lomami & destroy Chari, Bena Kamba & Riba-Riba & Kibonge. The situation now stands:

Lieutenant Dhanis from Lusambo, occupies Nyangone & is marching to Kassongo with four hundred soldiers and many hundreds of natives who have voluntarily joined him. Captain Chaltin has destroyed Chari, a town of 1000 houses and a population estimated at 15000 persons and on the 20th instant starts for Riba-Riba with four hundred soldiers & expects to be re-enforced by the natives on the road.

In the River Lomani we have been in many villages destroyed by the Arabs in their slave raids was, and in two places the headless bodies of men, women, and children were found, These people are simply devasting Central Africa, but I think the energetic measures the Government are now taking will put a stop to this nefarious traffic, at least for a long time, in the territory of the state.

At the present I can only give the merest survey to this slave business, but in my final report will dwell upon it to a greater extent.

I am, Sir,
Very respectfully
R. Dorsey Mohun
Commercial Agent
