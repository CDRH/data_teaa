Department of State Received Aug 6 1887

No. 6. U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia Liberia
July 1st 1887

Chas. H. J. Taylor to Hon Jas. D. Porter

Subject:

Transit from Wyandotte Kas, to Liberia taking 49 days, 43 days of which, was in actual travel and the other 6, caused by necessary delay. Praying permission to draw for.

 

No 6. U.S. Consulate
Monrovia Liberia
July 1st 1887

Hon. Jas. D. Porter
&c &c &c &c Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to call your attention to my No (1) Consular Series, of date May 31st You will find therein, an account of my transit from Wyandotte Kas., to Monrovia, consuming 49 days, I have drawn drafts on Secretary of State, as stated for 43 days of said time. I desire to respectfully call your attention to section 47 of the Consular reg. for '81, which I think justifies me in drawing for the other six (6) days at the rate   of $5000 per annum. I was forced to remain in Liverpool at the time I arrived there until the departure of (the Elder Dempster & Co.'s Steamship) the Roquelle. You will find that the Roquelle does not cull at Monrovia, but did so on their outward trip, at the time mentioned out of respect to the U.S. Government. If I had waited for steamer calling at Monrovia, I would not have arrived until June 9th instead of May 30th 1887. I hope that you may see that this added time, to the Hon. Secretary of State's maximum amount schedule, was caused by circumstances wholly beyond my control and therefore approve my "draft" for the am't of $82.42 making the total am't for "transit" $673.08.

I am your obedient servant &c.,
Chas. H. J. Taylor
U.S. Consul Gen'l
