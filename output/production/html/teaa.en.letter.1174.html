Department of State Received Feb 2 1892

Consulate General of the United States.
Monrovia, December 23, 1891.

Mr. Beverly Y. Payne,
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Transportation of the remains of Hon., Alexander Clark, late Minister Resident and Consul General of the United States, at Monrovia, Liberia. Account and vouchers of expenses involved.

 

Consulate General of the United States.
Monrovia, December 23, 1891.

Hon., William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

According to the instruction contained in your No 11, of July 21, last, I have to inform the Department that, the remains of Hon., Alexander Clark, late United States, Minister &c were shipped to Mr George W. Appleton, Muscatine, Iowa, to the care of Messers, Yates & Porterfield, Now York, per Bark "Liberia" on the 10th instant, and I have so advised the relatives of the late Minister.

I herewith enclose account   account and voucher for the expense involved, which I trust will prove satisfactory.

I have the honor to be,
Sir:
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne.
U.S. Vice Consul.
