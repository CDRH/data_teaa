No. 16.

Consulate General of the U.S.,
Monrovia, La., Apr. 28, '79.

Mr. J. H. Smyth to the Ass't Sec'y of State

Subject:

Death of seaman.

Synopsis:

The death of an American seaman connected with the Ticonderoga.

 

No. 16.

Consulate General of the U.S.,
Monrovia, La., Apr. 28, 1879

Sir:

I have to inform you that George Ross, one of the crew of our naval ship Ticonderoga, who was left at this place through his own negligence when the ship returned from Palmas en route for Solyma, contracted fever from the imprudence of indulging his appetite for liquor, or which he died on the afternoon of the 27th of April.

He was buried at 9 a.m., this 28th Hon. F. W. Seward,
Ass't Sec'y of State,
Washington, D.C.   28th day of April, in Monrovia.

I am, Sir,
Your obd't serv't,
Jno. H. Smyth,
U.S. Consul Gen'l.
