Department of State Received Aug 6 1887

No 7. U.S. Consulate
Monrovia Liberia
July 2nd 1887

Chas. H. J. Taylor to Hon Jas. D. Porter

Subject

Attention to Postage acc't for 2nd quarter ending June 30th 1887.

Enclosure

Postmasters Voucher

 

No. 7.

U.S. Consulate
Monrovia Liberia
July 2nd 1887

Hon. Jas. D. Porter
Assistant Sec. of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to call your attention to my despatch No 5, dated June 30th 1887, and to the enclosure of said despatch No 5 in reference to contingent expenses for 2nd quarter, in said reports returned the Postage acc't at $12.16. The Postmaster was in the country at the time, and upon his return, I am informed that instead of the legation (U.S.) owing $12.16 the am't should be $15.58/100. I write this as an explanation.   Please find enclosed the voucher from Postmaster for said am't, which I have this day paid, by draft on the Hon. Sec. of State for said am't and charged the same to the contingent allowance for the 2nd quarter. I hope that from now on, the business of this legation may be conducted in such a way as to gain the approval of the Department with great consideration &c., I am your ob't servant

Chas. H. J. Taylor
U.S. Consul General

P.S. It must be remembered, that Mr S. S. Sevier, setted all the expenses of the second quarter, saving the "Postage bill," and is therefore responsible. The making of the report, of course, devolved on me.
