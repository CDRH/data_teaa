Department of State Received May 23 1887

No 68.

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia April 19, 1887

Mr. S. S. Sevier
To the Secretary of State.

Subject

Service rendered the late Minister Moses A. Hopkins.

Synopsis

Service rendered the late Minister Resident and Consul General Moses A. Hopkins prior to his death. Attendance upon him during illness. Compensation for the same.

 

No 68.

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia April 19, 1887

Hon. T. F. Bayard
Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to refer you to my despatch No. 29 dated 29th of Sept. 1886. of which I had before written for compensation for service rendered the late Minister Resident and Consul General Moses A. Hopkins prior to his death.

I attended upon him during his illness–I was   was the writter of his Invoices when he was unabled to write. I performed the duties of this Consulate General before the Department of State gave me power to act according to the Consular Regulations; therefore I feel I should be compensated for part of the quarter ending June 30, 1886 and also for the month of July for service rendered the United States Government, I do not wish to be unreasonable nor do I wish to receive payment for service not justly rendered   rendered; but I am sure if the late Minister Moses A. Hopkins had have lived I would have received my pay. I want to do what is right and just.

I submit the above for your consideration and will be willing to abide by what you think is just.

I have the honor to be
Sir,
Your obedient servant
Saml. S. Sevier
U.S. Vice Consul Genl at Monrovia, La.
