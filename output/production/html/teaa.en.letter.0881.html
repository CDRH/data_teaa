Washington, 22 Jan '62

Hon Horace Maynard,

Sir!

I beg to call your attention to the fact that in April, 1859 the Brig E. N. Roye applied for entry at the Port of New York, under the Liberian flag.

Altho' American Vessels in the Ports of Liberia are not subject to discriminating duties of tonnage, the Roye was compelled to pay not only a tonnage duty, but also an extra duty upon her cargo (African Produce) in view of the fact that the Republic of Liberia was unrecognised by us.

By an act approved 24 May 1828, (See US Statutes at large vol 4, ¶ 308) the President when satisfied that no discriminating duties are levied upon American Vessels in the Ports of a Foreign Nation, may issue his proclamation, admitting the vessels of that Nation on terms of Equality with our own, in our own Ports, And I beg   to call yr attention to proof of that fact as furnished in a certificate of the US Commercial Agent at Monrovia, the Capital of Liberia, which I enclose, and also to state that further proof may be found in official despatches from Mr Seys, at the State Department.

I write this simply as one interested pecuniarily in the subject, as I have no doubt of retaliatory legislation on the part of Liberia, if justice be much longer delayed, by which I shall be obliged, with others, to abandon the field to English & Germans, whose constant aim seems to be to drive American Commerce from the Coast of Africa.

These facts I have laid before the Prest. in a communication handed to the Asst Sec'y of State, this day, & I entreat you to aid in any possible way, in rendering gracefully an act of International Comity, which w'd much better suit the Liberians than a bare "recognition" upon philanthropic grounds, as is proposed in some quarters   by coupling Liberia with Hayti, when almost the only interest in common is that of Colour.

I am, Sir,
Very respy yrs
Charles P. Clark
of Boston, U.S.
