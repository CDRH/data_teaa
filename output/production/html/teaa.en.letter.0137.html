Department of State Received Jan 30 1882
No 87

United States Consulate
at St Paul de Loanda

To the Department of State

Mr Robert Scott Newton

Subject. Advice of Small-pox having broken out at this port, and not granting clean Bill of Health.

 
No 87.

United States Consulate
St Paul de Loanda
23rd November 1881.

Sir,

I have the honor to report to the Department that Small-pox has broken out among the natives at this port, and there are great fears that it will extend to the interior. If it does it will cause great loss both to Merchants and Agriculturists as the natives have a great horror of this disease and will keep away. It was brought to Ambriz by the small steamer "La Plata" and has now broken out here and more or less at all the ports of the Province. The Doctors are doing all in their power to keep the disease from spreading, and try all they can to get all the cases into the Hospital, which is exceedingly difficult as the natives   natives hide their sick friends in their houses, as they have a great aversion to go to the Hospital. All the natives about the town and outskirts are being vaccinated, and with good results. It is very rare indeed when white people suffer from this disease in these climates. Any vessel leaving this port at present will not get a clean Bill of Health.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your most obedient Servant
Robt S. Newton
Vice Consul

To the
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington D.C.
