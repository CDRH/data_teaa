Assistant Secretary Department of State May 13 1896

No 14.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, March 30, 1896.

Mr. Beverly Y. Payne,
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Arrival of Immigrants at Monrovia.

 

No 14.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, March 30, 1896.

Honorable Edwin F. Uhl,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform the Department, that the American Merchant Steam Ship "Laurada" burdened 898 tons, arrived at this port on the 23rd instant, after a voyage of twenty days from Savannah Georgia, U.S.A. with Three Hundred and Eighteen colored Immigrants from different parts of the United States.

The "Laurada" was chartered by the same International Migration Company of Birmingham Alabama that sent out immigrants to Liberia in April 1895.

The immigrants this voyage were accompanied   accompanied by Mr. D. J. Flummet, President of the Company; she cleared Yesterday for Barbadoes, West Indies, leaving Mr Flummet at this port. Any further information relating to this subject will be duly reported.

I have the honor to be
Sir:
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne,
Vice Consul General.
