Department of State Received Feb 18 1871

Commercial Agency of the United States of America at Sierra Leone 7th Jany 1871

Sir,

I have the honor to report for the information of the Department of State that the Schooner "Nettle" of 31 tons Register of New York Arrived at this port on the 23rd December 1870 with a general Cargo Consisting of Among other articles 4,000 lbs of Powder and according to the Regulations of this Port proceeded to the Powder ground for the purpose of depositing the same in the Magazine before coming into harbour. Upon the following morning I was informed Honourable Hamilton Fish
Secretary of State
Washington D.C.   informed by the Customs Authority that it was their duty to seize her in conformity with an Act of the Privy Council dated 13th February 1849 which was accordingly done. I have the honor to enclose a copy of the Act under which she was seized together with copies of the correspondence relating to the matter and respectfully request you will be pleased to furnish me with Instructions as I have no Documentary evidence in the Consulate for any guidance Supposing a similar case should occur, also under what part of the International or other Law the Vessel was seized.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your most obt Servant
J.B. Upton
U.S. Commercial Agent
pro tem

Copy No 24.

Commercial Agency of the United States of America at Sierra Leone
December 27th 1870

Sir,

I have the honor to request that you will please place before His Excellency the Governor-in-chief the following remarks in regard to the American Schooner "Kettle" to my consignment now under detention in consequence of a violation of the Acts of 13th February 1849.

The "Nettle" is of 32 tons register has a cargo consisting of 600 Kegs of Powder besides some clothing and Provisions.

Arriving on the 23rd inst proceeded at once to the Powder ground where she still remains in charge of the Custom Authorities.

The Owner of the Vessel and Cargo Mr. Edward C. Mosser is also on board, who assures me that untill now, both he and the Master were quite ignorant of the existence of such an Act. Hon: J. J. Kendall
Colonial Secreatry
&c &c   I have besides consulted several of the Principal Merchants of this place and they as well as myself were also in perfect ignorance of such a regulation.

I therefore most respectfully request that His Excellency will take as lenient a view of this case as possible.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your Obedient Servant
J.B. Upton
U.S. Commercial Agent
pro tem

No 1069

Colonial Office Sierra Leone
28th December 1870

Sir,

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your letter No 24 of the 27th instant respecting the detention of the of the American Schooner "Nettle" by the Collector of Customs, in consequence of a violation of the order in Council of 13th February 1849.

Having laid the same before His Excellency The Governor-in-Chief, I am directed to inform you that His Excellency does not think it necessary to direct any proceeding in the matter, and the Collector has therefore been instructed to liberate the vessel, on payment of any expenses which have been incurred.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your Most Obt Servant
(sgd) J. J. Kendall
Colonial Secretary

J. B. Upton Esquire U.S. Commercial Agent
