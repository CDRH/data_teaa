No. 1

U.S. Consulate at Sierra Leone Nov 25. 1872

Honbl. Charles Hale
Assistant Secretary of State

Sir,

I have had the honor to receive your despatch No 1 dated 2 September, announcing that the President had been pleased to appoint me Consul at Sierra Leone West Africa. In accepting the appointment with which I have been honored, it will be most gratifying to be the representative, even in the humblest degree the Country where I spent so great a a portion of my life. I beg to inform you that I am a native of Newfoundland, and that I never resided in Great Britain. I have been a   resident on the Coast of Africa more or less for twelve years. I would also remark that there is not a single American Citizen in Sierra Leone. Inclosed you will find the necessary oath taken before the U.S. Consul at Madeira where I shall be a resident for a few weeks for the restoration of my health. The bond has been forwarded to my friends in New York for execution with direction to have it sent to the Department without delay.

I have the honor to be
Your Obedient Servant
William Hogan
