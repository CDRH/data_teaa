Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor most respectfully to again write you in reference to the matter contained in my last letter to, a part, of which you, so kindly, replied. You state that I have received all compensation due me to Jan 12th 1888. Of course the statement is true. It is also true that I am no longer connected with the "Service" in the quality and rank of U.S. Minister &c near the Government of Liberia. I am, however, custodian and keeper of your (U.S.) Archives in Monrovia, according to the decision of Dr. Sinclair, Chief of the Consular bureau. I am given by him to understand that Capt. Beverly Y. Payne the acting V.C. General for the U.S. will not be recognized by the U.S. Government, but that I will be held responsible for his acts, as   As I am, by contract (copy of which you have) for his salary. Now it certainly will be working a hardship on me, to be forced to pay Mr Payne, for his services to the U.S. Government, and in no way be remunerated or recompensated. It would also be wrong in my opinion for the U.S. Government to hold me on "my Bond" responsible for his acts, when I am in no way benefitted. I trust this will be considered in the spirit which it is written. I do not desire to have anything more than what is justly due me. What is Mr Paynes status or relation with the U.S. government? Will the U.S. government assume and carry out the agreement made by me with Mr Payne? Am I considered responsible for his acts on "my bond," until my successor takes charge of the Legation? I shall be thankful to receive full and complete answer to each of the three (3) within interrogatories. I take this opportunity to again express my high regard for your consideration and to assure you that I am your obedient servant.

Chas H J Taylor

 

Please forward reply at your earliest convenience to No 814 N 3rd St., Richmond, Va

Dr St Clair

Write to Mr. Taylor ack? Receipt, & answer his questions thus:

1. Mr. Payne is acting Vice Consul Genl and will be compensated by the Govt. from Jan 11th 1888.

2. Mr Taylor's agreement with Mr. Payne came to an end on Jan 11th 1888. up to that time Mr. Taylor must pay him.   The Govt will pay Mr. Payne after that time.

3. Mr. Taylor is not considered responsible for any act, committed by Mr. Payne after Jan 11th.

Consular Bureau
March 17/88

For the Secretary:

Mr Taylor has rec'd his salary in full to Jan'y 11, 1888, for all services as Minister Resident ad Consul General to Liberia. According to his agreement with Mr Payne whom he left in charge of the Consulate Gen'l, he must pay Payne at the rate of $300. per year up to that   date and not beyond.

Mr Taylor is not held responsible for Mr Payne's acts nor for his compensation after Jan 11:88.

The Dept is now considering the question of Mr. Payne's present and future compensation.

F. O. St Clair
