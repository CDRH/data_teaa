Department of State Received Apr 18 1876

United States Consulate
Sierra Leone W.C. of Africa
March 18th 1876

John L. Cadwalader Esqre
Assisting Secretary
Department of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to own receipt of your dispatch No 8 bearing the date of January 29th 1876 and referring to my nominations for Vice-Consul. You therein request me to inform your Department of the nationality, age and general fitness of the nominee and to state as well who is the particular person whose appointment as Vice-Consul I recommend Agreeable to these requirements I now beg to   propose James Frame Esqre as the proper person for the post in question. This gentlemen is a British Subject, being born in Manchester County of Lancashire, England. He is now 42 years of age, has been long time connected with the firm of "Randall & Fisher" and is a partner of that house. Having resided in this Colony for many years he has assigned a thorough Knowledge of all its institutions and particularities, qualifications which, together with his private character, render him to the best of my opinion, perfectly competent to fill the post of U.S. Vice-Consul during my eventual absence from here.

I note that I will be held   responsible for the official acts of my future Vice-Consul and in nominating Mr. James Frame I have been guided thereby.

I have the honor to be
Sir,
Your obedient servant
W H Randall
U.S. Consul
