No. 31.

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia, Liberia
18. Sept. 1877

J. Milton Turner
To the
Department of State,
Washington

Subject:

Concerning the U.S. Commercial Agency of Edina Grand Bassa.

Abstract of Contents.

Informing the Department of the reception of explanation from Mess Crusoe and Cheeseman relative to the Agency at Bassa, and recommending that Mr. Cheeseman be retained.

 

No. 31.

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia, Liberia
18. Sept. 1877.

Sir,

The subject of my No. 28 which related to the official incumbent of the U.S. Commercial Agency of Grand Bassa recurring, I have the honor to inform the Department that Messrs Crusoe and Cheeseman have written me notes furnishing the explanation required.

As Honorable
F. W. Seward,
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington   As there is now no American at Grand Bassa to whom the office would be acceptable, I would respectfully suggest that Mr. Cheeseman be retained the occupant thereof

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
J. Milton Turner

 
Copy
Enclosure No. 1. Despatch No. 31.

Legation of the United States.
Monrovia, Liberia
16. July 1877.

Sir.

Hon. H. L. Crusoe informed me a few days since that he has resigned the office of United States Commercial Agent at Grand Bassa, and that you are in charge of the Archives and, I suppose, acting incumbent of stat position.

You would oblige me by informing my by Mail if you have received from the government at Washington an appointment to that office, and if so then have you been officially recognized in that capacity by the Government at Monrovia?

I am, Sir,
Very Respectfully
Sd. J. Milton Turner
U.S. Minister

Hon J. J. Cheeseman
Edinia
Grand Bassa.
 
Copy
Enclosure No. 2. Despatch No 31.

Legation of the United States
Monrovia, Liberia
10. Sept. 1877.

Sir,

On the 16th of July, I wrote you a note of inquiry - having received information that you were appointed, or was acting as Commercial Agent at Bassa, asking information.

On the same date, I also sent you a note relative to the whereabouts of one John Smith. I have not been honored with a reply to either of the above notes.

It is very essential that both of these matters should be adjusted, as at least, it will save my having to come and make such inquiries at Bassa in propria persona.

I Hon.
J. J. Cheeseman
Edina   I am,
Sir,
Your obedient servant
(Sd) J. Milton Turner
U.S. Minister

 
Copy
Enclosure No. 3. Despatch No 31.

U.S. Commercial Agency
Grand Bassa
15th September 1877.

Honorable
J. M. Turner
U.S. Minister Resident & Consul
General, Monrovia.

Sir,

I have the honor to acknowledge receipt of your despatches under date 16th July.

In answer permit me to say that I have referred your communications, making inquiry as to my appointment as U.S. Commercial Agent, to the Hon'ble H. L. Crusoe, late incumbent, who will by this opportunity send you such a reply as I trust will be satisfactory to you. He will also send you a copy of his dispatch to the State Department at Washington, touching my appointment.

I hope the proceedings will be found   found to be regular and that they may meet with your approval.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
(Sd) J. Jas. Cheesman

 
Enclosure No. 4. Despatch No 31.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia
18th. Sept. 1877.

Sir,

Your two despatches of the 15th Sept. have been received. They relate to your authority to act as U.S. Commercial Agent and to the whereabouts of the children of John Smith, deceased.

The contents of those despatches is satisfactory.

I am,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
(Sd) J. Milton Turner

Honorable
J. Jas. Cheeseman
Actg. U.S. Commercial Agent
Edina, Grand Bassa
