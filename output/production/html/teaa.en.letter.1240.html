Receipt of Despatch.

 

No 16.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, June 27, 1896

Honorable William W. Rockhill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge receipt of your No 11, of April 30th last, enclosing a copy of a protest by O. A. Datow, Master of the Barkentine, "D. A. Brayton" relative to interference with his vessel by a British cruiser &c

In reply, I beg to say, that I am not in possession of any further information on the subject from our Consular Agent at Cape Coast. I have written him again, and if any is procured, it will be transmitted to the Department as soon as it is received.

I have   I have the honor to be
Sir:
Your obedient servant,
Beverly Y. Payne.
Vice Consul General.
