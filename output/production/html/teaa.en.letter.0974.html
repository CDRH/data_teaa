No. 15

Consulate General of U.S.,
Monrovia, La., Apr. 28, 1879.

Mr. J. H. Smyth to the Ass't Sec'y of State.

Subject:

Arrival of British naval ship Boxer & Ticonderoga in port of Monrovia.

Synopsis

The adjournment of the Mixed Commission; the arrival of the Liberian Commissioners on the Boxer & the President of Liberia on the Ticonderoga.

 

No. 15

Consulate General of U.S.,
Monrovia, La., Apr. 28, 1879.

Sir:

I have the honor to give information of the adjournment of the Mixed or Boundary Commission, at the place of meeting Solyma or Solyman.

The Liberian Commissioners were tendered passage in Her Majesty's naval ship Boxer, and accepted, and arrived in the port Saturday morning, Apr. 26, 1879.

The Ticonderoga called at Hon. F. W. Seward,
Ass't Sec'y of State,
Washington, D.C.   at Cape Mount; where the President had been visiting for some three or four weeks with the object in view of meeting and conferring with the Vey chiefs; and he was offered the hospitality of conveyance to Monrovia by Commodore R. W. Shufeldt.

His Excellency accordingly arrived at the capital at 4 p.m. Apr. 26, 1879, on board the Ticonderoga.

I am, Sir,
Your obd't sev't
Jno. H. Smyth,
U.S. Consul Gen'l
