Department of State Received Nov 4 1879

No. 35.

Consulate Gen'l of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, Oct. 2nd, 1879.

Mr. J. H. Smyth to the Ass't Secretary of State.

Subject:

Consul of the German Empire.

Synopsis:

Presentations of credentials of Max Köningsdorfer to Secretary of State of Liberia

 

No. 35.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, Oct. 2nd, 1879.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform the Dep't that the vacancy caused by the resignation of W. Brohm, Esq, German Consul, at Monrovia, has been filled by the appointment of Max Köningsdorfer, Esq., as the consular representative of the German Empire to the Republic of Liberia.

Mr. Köningsdorfer presented his credentials on the 26th Sept. 1879, and received from the Liberian government, at the hand of Mr. Secretary Gibson Hon. F. W. Seward,
Ass't Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.   Gibson his exequatur.

With renewed sentiments of esteem, I am, Sir
Your obd't serv't,
Jno. H. Smyth,
U.S. Consul Gen'l
