You will at once see Sir, that the position which I now hold as Acting Consul without appointment to from the United States Government, but merely by the nomination of the Commodore before-named invests me in strict law with no actual legal authority. I am recognized as Acting Consul by the local Government, as it seems to me, merely through courtesy, and I cannot close my eyes to the fact, that, had I attempted to make any stand in opposition to the Government in recent cases of aggression by British Cruizers on American Vessels in the Coast, any further recognition whatever of me even through courtesy would have at once ceased.

As you are aware, the several vessels seized on the Coast on the alleged ground of being engaged in the Slave Trade are all sent to this Colony for adjudication, or, I should rather observe for condemnation.

I stake the liberty of enclosing for your inspection a List of the Prizes taken by H. B. Majesty's Cruizers and sent into this Port. I will also mention that during the period I have been Acting Counsel there have arrived in this Port no fewer than 120 American Vessels comprising a Tonnage of over 25000 Tons, and with crews consisting of 1100 men, exclusive of three United States Ships of War.

You will bear in mind Sir, that the British Government do not fail, to maintain a Consul at Liberia, and it is I am sure almost unnecessary for me to remark that in point of comparison with Sierra Leone, Liberia sinks into insignificance.

Under the circumstances I have disclosed to you I venture to submit for your consideration my lengthened service as Acting Consul and the inadequacy of pay I have received through fees alone and which have been considerably reduced by recent Acts of Congress.

I earnestly trust Sir, that you will see the urgent necessity for appointing a Consul at this place, and that in the exercise of your patronage, you will be pleased to confer such appointment on me with such salary as you may think proper to maintain the dignity of our great nation.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
With high respects,
Your most obedt humble Servant
Jno E Taylor
Acting United States Consul.
