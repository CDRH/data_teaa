Consulate of the United States of America
Bathurst River Gambia
4th March 1867.

Sir

Herewith I have the honor to transmit the return of the arrivals and Departures of American Vessels and the statement of Fees for the half year ending the 31st December 1866.

The Fees, never a large item here, have considerably fallen off since the Rebellion of the Southern States in consequence of the bulk of the American Trade having since that period been carried on under the English Flag.

For the last half year the total is only $20.23 Â½ a sum insufficient even to pay a Man to attend to the Flag & Flag Staff.

Washington trade between this port & the United States has considerably increased tho' with much fluctuation.

I have not yet received the public acts for the 39th Congress and am therefore unacquainted with its provisions or into allowance if any has been made as compensation for services at this consulate.

The Office of the consulate is held at my own House which is rated at Twelve Hundred & fifty Dollars per annum and would readily let a Fifteen Hundred dollars I respectfully crave to be allowed Two Hundred and Fifty Dollars per annum for Rent of Offices

I crave reference to my letter of the 20th November last renewing application for a New Seal and I now further make requisition for a large Flag to hoist at the Flag staff as also a small Boat Flag.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your most obedient Servant
Thomas Brown
Commercial Agent
