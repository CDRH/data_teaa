Rec 27 Mar

Gaboon, Jan 24. 1862.

Hon. W. H. Seward
Washington D.C.

Sir,

I had the honor to write you the 23d Dec. respecting the death of Capt. R. E. Lawlin, an American trader on the River Fernan Vaz, about 150 miles South of Gaboon. Since the date of my last, Rev. J. M. Preston has been to Camma with the brig. Barque 'Minerva', & brought away the goods in the factory & as much other property as he could ship. It is all stored in the Gaboon under my care. A full report of all our proceedings has been made to Messrs James Bishop & Co. of New York, & I wait the arrival of their agent to deliver the property to him. I have taken considerable responsibility in removing the property & paying & discharging the employés of the Factory. But we have no doubt now that the course pursued has been the only one for the interest of the owners.   I have made no sales of property except to pay expenses. I have paid no doubtful debts. We trust that we shall be able to satisfy the Agent who may be sent.

Enclosed please find a copy of the form of the oath of allegiance forwarded to this Agency, signed, & certified to by the Commandant of Gaboon. As I remarked in my former letter; I do not know how far I am bound to act in regard to duties coming under this Agency. I know not the boundaries of jurisdiction. But I do not wish to act at all, except for the interests of humanity.

Allow me to suggest that I find no form of Passport, Certificate, Petition Protest, or any paper. No form of any kind, & no table to be filled of shipping or any thing else. If any such duties arise forms are safe, in law & convenient in practice.

I am, Sir, Your Obedient Servant
Wm Walker.
