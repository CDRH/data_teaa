No. I. Consular Series.

Consulate of the United States
Monrovia, Liberia,
March 31, 1892.

William D. McCoy,
To the Assistant Secretary of State.

Subject–

Request for Blanks, Supplies, and Newspapers.

 

No. 1.

Consulate of the United States
Monrovia, Liberia, March 31, 1892.

Honorable William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I respectfully request of you the following blanks, supplies, and newspapers for use in this Consulate:

Blanks.
Digest of the Invoice Book, Form No. 117.
Arrival and departure of vessels, " 120.
Record of Notarial service, " 159.
Summary of Consular Business, " 103.

Ruled paper, both letter and foolscap.
Envelopes, small, Legation and Consular.
Ink, both black and red.
Mucilage
Glass or stone water jar and filter. (If a glass or stone one can not be obtained, any kind will do as the filter accompanies it. The water here is very impure and no suitable filter can be obtained.)

(Over.)  

Sample – 1 Bracket and 1 Table Lamp.
Flag – 14 x 5 ft.
1 Rubber or Iron mat for outside door.
7 yds. oil cloth 1 yd. wide for hall.
12 yds. stair carpeting or oil cloth ¾ yds.
1 large carpet rug for middle of room
Tacks.

Newspapers–
1. Indianapolis Daily Journal.
2. Harper's Weekly.
3. London Times.
Would like to have either the New York Herald or Washington Post instead of the other two foreign papers referred to in Section 208 of Personal Instructions.

Your obedient servant,

William D. McCoy,
Minister-Resident and Consul-General to Liberia.
