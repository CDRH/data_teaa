No 2.

United States Commercial Agency
St Paul 'd Loanda January 26, 1859

Sir,

I last had this honor, under date of October 11th '58, informing you that I had arrived at this Port & had entered upon the duties of this office. I also informed you that the American Yacht Wanderer was then in the Congo River, during the latter part of the month She left the River & it is said, with a Cargo of Slaves.

Since the capture & burning of the Brig "Rufus Soul√©" off the Congo by H.R.M. Steamer "Viper", in October last, a statement of which was given to Capt. Totten of the U.S.S. "Vincennes" I have heard of four or five Shipments being made, the last one a few days since in which one thousand Slaves were placed on board one vessel. The Trade has now gone in part, from the Congo River to the North of that river. Owing to the French Apprentice System, Slaves are rather scarce & the price has advanced. It is said there are a number of vessels off the Coast waiting for their Consignees to arrange a Congo.

On the 8th December Sir Henry Huntley, HBM Consul for this Port arrived now. He came out as a Consul & Clerk to the English Slave Commission presuming that Mr Gabriel, HBM Arbitrator would return to England, & then he would be acting Arbitrator & Consul; but as Mr Gabriel does not intend leaving this at present, Sir Henry is officiating as Consul without accepting the post as Clerk to the Commission, & waiting for further instructions from his Government.

Accompaning this I enclose the Returns from this Commercial Agency for the Quarter ending December 31, 1858 & have drawn on Your Honor for Two Hundred forty-four & 07/100 dollars, being balance of my Salary for that Quarter. I am not able to fill up the blanks with the exact value of the Inward & Outward Cargoes, for the reason that a vessel entering or clearing at this Custom House is not obliged to place on her Manifest the value thereof. For a year estimate of the American Business done at this Port. I should say that at the present time it will not exceed One hundred thousand dollars. Two or three years ago it would amount to one hundred & fifty thousand dollars, but since that time the Crop of Palm Oil has failed & the Natives were obliged to eat the Palm Nut to keep from starvation. Palm Oil it is now thought will this year prove very fair. & if so it will enable the Portuguese Merchants here to pay their Debts, which in may instances have been standing very long. their debts once settled will cause the two American Houses here to order larger Invoices & then of course the Business will again increase.

It is the wish of the Governor General of this Province to take possession of the River Congo, but I understand the English Government will not allow them to go North of Ambriz. If Portugal should take that River, there will be no more Slave Trade there, & perhaps England may in time change her views in this respect as it will save her considerable expense in taking of the African Squadron.

The occupation of Ambriz by the Portuguese has not yet resulted favorably. They have been at considerable expense & there is now only one foreign House there & that an American. The two English Houses that formerly done business at Ambriz, have now left on account of the high duties (which are more than Mr O'Sullivan the late U.S. Minister at Lisbon was given to understand by the Portuguese Minister) & have gone to a place called "Kisembo", 7 miles north of Ambriz, together with a Branch of the American House & a Dutch House, where they get a clear of all Custom House charges & at the same time do considerable Trade with the Natives.

The Copper Mines at Ambriz have not proved so favorable as they were at first expected & it is said will result in a great loss to those concerned.

Some time since, I wrote the Department in respect to a Burial Place in Loanda for deceased Americans, & under date of February 15th 1858 I am informed by the Department that the United States Minister at the Court of Lisbon will be informed of my request. As yet nothing relating thereto has been received at this Agency, & I now take the liberty of again calling the attention of the Department to this subject.

Very Respectfully
Your Obt. Servt
John G. Willis

Hon Lewis Cass
Secretary of State USA.
Washington D.C.
