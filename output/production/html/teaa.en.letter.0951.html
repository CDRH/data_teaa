Consulate General, United States
Monrovia, Liberia
16th Octo. 1877

J. Milton Turner
To the
Department of State
Washington

Subject: U.S. Commercial Agent at Grand Bassa.

Abstract of contents:

The attention of the Liberia Govt. was called to the appointment of the successor of Mr. Crusoe according to 7th Article of Treaty. Mr. Cheeseman's right to act as such, witheld by the Lib. Govt: and recommending the appointment of H. A. Williams to be successor to Mr. Crusoe.

 

No. 34.

Consulate General, United States
Monrovia, Liberia
16. Octo. 1877

Sir,

The subject of my No. 31. which relates to the appointment of a gentleman to succeed Mr. Crusoe in the capacity of Commercial Agent at Grand Bassa, Liberia having recurred, I have the honor to state for the information of the Department, that in compliance with the provisions of the 7th Article of our Treaty with Liberia I Honorable
F. W. Seward
Asst. Secty State,
Washington   I addressed to the Secretary of State a note, copy of which is transmitted herewith, and marked "enclosure No. 1" in which I requested the permission of this Government to be extended to Mr. J. J. Cheeseman to be Acting Commercial Agent of the United States, instead of Mr. Crusoe, resigned. It appears from the reply of Secretary Blacklidge, Copy of which is marked "enclosure No. 2," – that there are on the part of this Government, objections to Mr. Cheeseman's being permitted to act in that capacity. My recognition of those objections may be found by reference to enclosures 3. 5. of this despatch Enclosure No. 7. relates to my tender of the position   position to H. A. Williams, Esqr. a citizen of Grand Bassa, engaged in mercantile business in that County. As soon as Mr. Williams can be heard from, I will, if he accepts the position, ask the permission of this Government to be extended to him to act in that capacity, and report the result to the Department.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient servant
J. Milton Turner

Despatch No. 34.

Department of State
Monrovia, 6. Octo. 1877.

Sir,

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your favor of the 28 ultimo, conveying the intelligence to my government of your appointing J. J. Cheeseman of the County of Grand Bassa temporary vice U.S. Consul, vice J. L. Crusoe resigned.

Absence from the City several days is the cause of not replying sooner. I hasten now to do so, advising you that I have the direction His Excellency
J. Milton Turner
U.S. Minister Resident
Monrovia.   direction of the President to inform you of a circumstance you may not have known or perhaps overlooked viz: the recent election of the Gentleman named for that appointment by you to the Honorable the House of Representatives of the Republic of Liberia: and the recognition of whose appointment would at least, appear incongrous having him acting in a double capacity or in other words, in the interest of each nationality

I have the honor to be,
Your obedient servant
(Sd) J. W. Blacklidge

Enclosure No. 6.
Despatch No. 34.

U.S. Commercial Agency,
Grand Bassa, Octo. 16. 1877.

Sir,

I have the honor to acknowledge receipt of your dispatches under Sept. 18. & 9th. inst. both of which reached me to day. Copies of your correspondence with the Government of Liberia respecting my appointment as U.S. Com. Agent. I have perused, and the reply of the Honorable Secretary of State, directed to you in which he makes reference to the incongruity of serving in a two fold position I have especially noted. The honorable Secretary was pleased   pleased to remark in his dispatch to you that "The recent election of the gentleman named by you for that appointment to the Honorable the House of Representatives of the Republic of Liberia, would at least appear incongruious having him acting in a double capacity," permit me to remark that my being a member of the Legislature of Liberia lies peculiarly within the knowledge of the Government. And it is indeed very strange that no such notice has come to hand, and consequently I have no authority to say that I am a member of the House of Representatives of the Republic of Liberia.

I   I have the honor to remain
Sir,
Your Obedient Servant,
(Sd) J. Jas. Cheeseman
