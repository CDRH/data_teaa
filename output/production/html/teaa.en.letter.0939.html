No 22. Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia, 31, March 1877.

Sir

I have the honor to transmit herewith my Report for the Quarter ending this day. Upon return to Liberia I find no change in the commercial or agricultural affairs worthy being pronounced of a decidedly progressive nature, except, that much attention from "Corea"[?] has been attracted through the American Centennial Exhibition to the superiority of the Coffee of this Country. Several countries in which coffee is grown, have made considerable orders for Liberian coffee for seed purposes. And in almost every instance, it has come to my knowledge that those orders are resultant upon the introduction which the Liberian Honorable John L. Cadwalder
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington D.C.   Liberian coffee received at Philadelphia during the Centennial Exhibition. The Government adheres to her proverbial neglect to compile or collect statistics: yet it is tolerably certain that the exportation of coffee has increased this year between one and two hundred thousand pounds. It is proper that I should state that I have charged Government with my salary from the tenth day of January; when my leave of absence, as extended expired: and my return to Monrovia began.

I have the honor to be
Sir:
With high considerations
Your obedient servant
J Milton Turner
