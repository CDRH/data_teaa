No 11.

United States Consulate
Sierra Leone 24th May 1876

John L. Cadwalader Esquire
Assistant Secretary
Department of State
Washington D.C.

Sir,

In the absence of W. H. Randall Esquire, the duty devolved on me of opening your dispatches numbers 9 and 10 addressed to the Consul of the U. States and accompanied by the official form of Bond to be signed by myself & two sureties. I have now the honor to return the same duty signed and certified to & trust the same will meet with your approval. Mr Randalls health, since his departure for Madiera will require your granting him an extension of leave beyond the sixty days usually allowed allowed.

This Consulate requires a fresh supply of stationary including the following list of forms Forms numbered
13. 19. 31. 32. 33. 34. 37. 38. 40. 43. 44. 45. 144.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your most obedient
Servant
James Frame
Acting U. State Consul
