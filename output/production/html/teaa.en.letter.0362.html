Zanzibar,
July 8th 1892.

Sir,

I have the honour to inform you that, acting under instructions received from Her Majesty's Government, I have caused to be closed several shops, belonging to Goanese subjects of Portugal, in which an unlicensed sale of liquor was being carried on in defiance of the regulations issued by the Protecting Power with a view to fulfilling its obligations incurred under Article XCI of the Brussels Act. The owners of these shops had been several times warned of the consequences of offering defiance to the Brussels Act and had been advised

Jones, Esq.
Consul for the United States of America
Zanzibar. [illegible]
