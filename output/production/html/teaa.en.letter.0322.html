No 5.

Vice-Commercial Agency of the United States of America.
Sierra Leone August 1st 1868.

Sir,

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of a despatch from the Department of State dated June 26th, 1868 - No. 7 - informing me that my assumption of the charge of the Commercial Agency's Office at this settlement had been approved of by the Department, - that accordingly I had been appointed Vice Commercial Agent, subject to the conditions of law and the Consular regulations, and that the certificate of my appointment had F. W. Seward Esqre
Assistant Secretary had been sent to the Legation of the United States at London for the purpose of obtaining my official recognition by the British Government.

I have also to acknowledge the receipt of the Forms of Consular Bond, oath of Office and allegiance and Circulars N: 68, N: 69, N: 70.

I beg to thank the Department for my appointment and will to the best of my abilities endeavor to carry out it's instructions and guard the interest of the United States Government and it's Citizens.

I am a native of Hamburg in Germany, but resident in this settlement since January 1847; my appointment emanated from the the despatch to which I have the honor to reply at present.

Not knowing anybody in the United States sufficiently to ask to become bond for me I have written to my Bankers in London to procure me the requisite Bondsmen, citizens of the United States, conceiving that such only the Department requires, on the receipt of the names of my Bondsmen I shall forward the Bond forthwith.

Being a naturalized British subject I cannot consistently take the oath of allegiance as prescribed in the printed form, having in substance subscribed to a similar oath to the British Government.

Not knowing whether the Department keeps other forms of of oaths I have taken the liberty to alter the form sent, consistent with my conscience, sworn to it before a Police Magistrate of this place, and enclose it.

I have communicated my appointment to Sir Arthur E. Kennedy of the Governor-in-Chief of the West African Settlements, who provisionally recognises it subject to it's approval by the British Government.

In conclusion I beg to inform the Department that I am Consul for the Netherlands and Consul for the Republic of Liberia for for both of which Offices I hold the Exequatur of Her Majesty the Queen of Great Britain.

I have the honor to be
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
Rosenbush
U.S. Vice-Comm: Agent.
