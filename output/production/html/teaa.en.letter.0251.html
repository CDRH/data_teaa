Ls de R. du Verge

Subject

Slave trade and departure of Mrs. L. de R. du Verge

Encloses

Statement of the same

 

United States Consulate
at St Paul de Loanda
March 18th 1883

Hon. John Davis
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir

I have the honor to state that Despatch No 12 is received and contents carefully noted, for which I thank the Department.

I have been following a real slave business going on here and carried out by Portuguese from Lisbon and both whites and mulattos of this country. I wanted and still intend to follow them on that business up to the near interior where the purchase is effected by Barter, &c &c as is the case, I am told, throughout almost all the trading posts, not only of the interior but near the seaboard and rivers: Such is the case at the most important town of Dande on the Quanza river: Ambriz and   and specially at the southern ports of Benguella and Catumbella where large caravans of two and three thousand negroes are constantly arriving from the interior, bringing along with their produce numbers of young blacks for sale or barter; they average in ages from infancy to full grown adults, and always find a ready market with brisk competition.

From the small seaport of Novo-Redondo South of Loanda, an export trade has been regularly carried on for more than five years by the agents and on account of the Bank Alltramarine of Lisbon. The agent from this port is a Mr Patricio Alvares, and although certain forms are carried out as prescribed by their emigration laws, it is well known that as soon as they are landed at St Thomas, and that by the hundreds. they are treated, conducted and consi- dered   dered as slaves; and not one has ever been known to have returned, and most probably never will be.

But I have been now for a long time very sick, and this month hardly able to write, even my wife Mrs Du Verge has left for the United States via Liverpool: she has with her a live specimen of slave traffic in Africa; a stolen child given to her by a respectable white merchant for the simple reason that she was too young to be sold.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient servant
L. de R. du Verge
United States Consul
