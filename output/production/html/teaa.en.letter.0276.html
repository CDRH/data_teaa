Rec'd March 25 '64
No. 3

Consulate of the United States
of America at Sierra Leone
19th February 1864.

Sir,

I have the honor to acknowledge a communication from you desiring me to obtain for the United States Government a parcel of the "Bean of Calabar" also a similar parcel for Doctor Albert Dung of New York.

The article does not grow in or adjacent to this Colony and is little known here but I have been fortunate enough to meet two gentleman, by this packet form down the Coast (Calabar) of whom I have obtained some information with regard to the article. It is not cultivated at F. W. Seward Esq
Actg Secretary of State
&c &c &c   at present but there beginning to be a demand for it it will probably attract the attention of the natives very soon as an article of trade, it is used by them in some of their ceremonies and is know by the name of the "Ordeal Bean" investigation has proved it of great value in many other diseases as well as those of the eye.

One of the Gentlemen kindly gave me a letter through the influence of which I hope to obtain a parcel to send as requested, I have also written Capt Alexander of the bark "Thos Pope" bound for Fernando Po requesting him to endeavor to get me some of the beans.

The beans are sold in England   England at half a crown an once for medicinal purposes.

Mr Baillie the gentleman from whom I received the letter has kindly promised to send me from England an essay on the article by a Doctor Fraser who has given great attention to it and through whom it was brought to the notice of the medical world Hoping I may soon be able to report success in my efforts

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your ob'd't servant
Henry Rider
U.S. Consular Agent
