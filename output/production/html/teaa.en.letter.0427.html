Department of State Received Jul 22 1873

United States Consulate
Sierra Leone June 12/73

Your communication of the 14th March has been received.

I beg to inform you that the records and Archive of the Consulate as well as the seals &c &c &c mentioned by you are now in my possession

I find the Office with but very little stationary such as Official paper large & small also envelopes to correspond. Envelopes with address of State Department as well as short and long ones addressed to Collectors of Customs. Red tape, sealing wax suitable to the tropics and large wafers to use with gum[?]. The flag is pretty well worn out and requires renewal.

Inclosed I forward my provisional recognition until the arrival of the Exequatur.

I Am, Sir
Your Obedient Servant
William Hogan
United States Consul

Honble J. C. B. Davis
Assistant Secretary
Department of State
Washington

OFFICIAL GAZETTE.

In accordance with instructions received from His Excellency the Administrator-in-Chief, His Excellency the Administrator has been pleased to recognize William Hogan, Esquire, provisionally, as United States' Consul at Sierra Leone, until the arrival of the Exequatur.

By His Excellency's Command,
John Ashwood,
Acting Colonial Secretary.

Colonial Secretary's Office,
Sierra Leone,
26th May, 1873.
