Department of State Received May 6 1889

No 24 U.S. Consulate at Sierra Leone March 30th

J. A. Lewis To Dept State

Receipt of Dept Despatch No 9 with Enclosure - Copy of Letter from Cornelia W. Martin

 
No 24

U.S. Consulate at Sierra Leone
March 30th 1880.

Hon John Hay
Asst Secty State
Washington D.C.

Sir

I have to acknowledge the receipt of Your Dispatch No 9. Feb. 25th with Enclosure Copy of Letter written by Cornelia W Martin relative to the death and obsequies of the late Revd Albert Bushnell at this place Dec. 3rd 1879. I am very glad indeed of the services of the U.S. Consul Ameliorated in any degree the pain of the saddened hearts of Mrs Bushnell or Miss Cameron on this sad occasion and I hereby extend my sympathies to the good ladies of Auburn in   their affliction.

With regard to the removal of the remains to Gaboon. the Agent of the Sacisty Mr Christy in Liverpool has written me that the [?] will not under any circumstances take the remains however Securely encased they may be so the second proposition of erecting a stone - prepared in the states will be best. And I have written Mrs Bushnell this day to this effect promising to bring the stone in one of Yates and Porterfields vessels from New York and superintend the placing on my return in the fall.

I have the honor to remain
Your Most Obt Servt
Judson A Lewis
U.S. Consul
