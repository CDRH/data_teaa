No. 8

Consulate General of the U.S.,
Monrovia, La., Feb'y 17, 1879.

Mr. J. H. Smyth to the Ass't Sec'y of State.

Subject:

Receipt of consular arms &c and request for blanks.

Synopsis:

As to the receipt of the coat of arms for Consulate, seal for Commercial Agency at Cape Coast Castle, and request for complete set of Consular & Legation blanks.

 

No. 8.

Consulate General of the U.S.,
Monrovia, La., Feb'y 17, 1879.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to acknowledge the receipt of the coat of arms & ruler, a seal for Commercial Agent at Cape Coast Castle, but no information as to any action of the Dep't as to the appointment of such officer.

You would oblige me and satisfy the needs of the Legation and Consulate by sending at the earliest convenience a full and complete set of blanks, as by Hon. F. W. Seward
Ass't Sec'y of State,
Washington, D.C.   by deficiency of them, I am frequently under necessity of copying forms needed from the "Consular Regulations."

The blanks here, are, in consequence of their former depository, dampness peculiar to the rainy seasons, and I doubt not, neglect of the attendant or attendants of my predecessor, in very bad condition, and by no means complete.

I am, Sir,
With high considerations of esteem,
Your obd't serv't,
Jno. H. Smyth
