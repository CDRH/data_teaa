Department of State Received Jan 6 1887
No. 37

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia Dec. 2, 1886.

Mr. Samuel S. Sevier–
To the Secy of State

Subject

The celebration of the 1st of Dec. '86.

Synopsis

An allusion to sentiments expressed.

 

No. 37.

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia Dec. 2, 1886.

Hon. T. F. Bayard
Secy of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to inform you that the 1st of Dec. 1886 was celebrated by the authorities and citizens of Liberia as a public holiday on account of the achievement obtained by the early settlers of this city (Monrovia the capitol) over the Day-tribes once the most powerful tribe on the West Coast of   of Africa. The day was pleasantly spent. The President of the Republic of Liberia had public reception in the capital at the exective mansion. I had the honor to be present and took occasion to pay tribute of respect to the memory of the early heroes of those days who fought manfully to gain this Republic. His excellency the President H. R. W. Johnson proposed the health of the President of the United States which was received cheerfully. In recognition of which I   I spoke of the President Grover Cleveland; his zeal, his earnestness in the performance of his weighty responsibilities and of the strength and grandeur of the government of the United States which will ever live as ages roll on into eternity. I pray the government of the United States may ever live to take active part in the concerns generally in my race in this dark and benighted land.

I remain Sir
Your obedient servant,
Samuel S. Sevier
Acting Consul General
