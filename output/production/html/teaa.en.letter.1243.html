Acknowledgement of the reciept of your dispatch No. 5 of July 15, 1898, with Inclosure.

In reply to dispatch and Inclosure, you are respectfully referred to my dispatch No. 21, of Aug., 11, 1898, with Inclosures.

Would not, at present, recommend a discontinuation of the Cape Coast Castle, Agency.

 

No. 2, Consular Series,
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia;
Aug., 29, 1898.

Hon. Thos. W. Cridler,
Third Asst. Secretary,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your dispatch No. 5, July 15, 1898, with Inclosure of a letter and petition of Pet. A. W. Renner Esq. of Cape Coast Castle; with thanks, for consideration & c.

Your dispatch reached me, Aug. 27, 1898. In reply to which I most respectfully refer you to my dispatch No. 21, Aug. 11, 1898, to Department of State.

In reply to your instruction, that I report   upon, the necessity of a further continuation of the Agency, at Cape Coast Castle; would say, first, that the Gold Coast Colony, Africa, of which Accra is the Capital, is a British possession; and according to Walker's Almanac of British possessions, in Africa, Cape Coast Castle has a population of 11,614, while Elmina, the home of the late George E. Eminsang, has a population of 10,530, hence, from the population of the two cities, must of necessity, command a considerable commercial entercourse. As to the matter of trade from the Gold Coast, to the United States, it seems to have been greatly neglected. And until it shall have been properly, worked up, I would probably be premature, in recommending recommending   recommending a discontinuation of the Agency. I would, therefore, the rather, recommend, that said Agency, be continued, until opportunity enables me to learn more of the place, or proper attention and arrangements, by American merchants, shall have been more fully made, in order, to increase exports to, and emports from the United States.

As to the application of Pet. A. W. Renner Esq. for the Commercial Agency at Cape Coast Castle, I learn, by inquiry, that he is a Sierra Leonean, a man of much ability, but for some cause was for some time disbarred from practice, but has subsequently been restored. Secondly; As it is now currently circulated, here, that peace has been declared, between   between the United States and Spain, it will doubtless, give an opportunity for American vessels, to again, with the Coasts of Africa, the better enabling us to decide upon the necessity of the Agency.

Thirdly: I will say incidentally as well as confidentially, that judging from an informed interview, believes the Secretary of State – Monrovia, and myself, on the 26, instant, I am led to believe that it will soon be my privilege, as well as duty, to communicate to the Department of State – Washington, the expressed desire of the Liberian Government, to have the Government of the United States, to extend a protectorate over the Liberian Republic! And   And, in the event, that the American Government, accedes to such a request; as American goods and manufactures, have, even now, quite a demand, in Liberian markets; and as both the Liberian merchant and farmer, are very anxious to open up a market, in America, for the exchange of their products; as an evidence of which, I have already succeded in securing several consignments of Liberian productions, to New York, Baltimore, and Boston: notwithstanding, these products had to be shipped, by way of Liverpool, owing to existing hostilities between the United States and Spain.

Upon the cessation of   of these hostilities; and the return of peace; when shipping, by American vessels, can again, be resumed, without fear of being seized, by enemies' ships; it is more than probable that such a protectorate would be the means of a more extended commercial entercourse, between the United States and Liberia: thus necessitating not only, the retention of the Agency, at Cape Coast Castle, but possible, the establishment of one at Accra, the Capital of the Gold Coast Colony, with a population of 16,276, as well as at Sinoe, Harper, Cape Palmas, and the restoration of the Agency, at Grand Bassa, Liberia.

Awaiting further developments of affairs, between the two Governments, I   I do not, at this writing, feel myself, justified to recommend a discontinuation of the Cape Coast Castle Agency.

I have the honor to be your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith

P.S. My dispatch – No. 21, Aug, 11, 1898, to Department of State should have been Consular Series, No. 1.
Owen L. W. Smith.
