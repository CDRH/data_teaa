Department of State Received Apr 4 1888

Franklin Co
North Carolina
April 1st/88.

Hon. G. L. Rives
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

My business with the Department of State was not fully accomplished before I had to take my leave. I was anxious to see you, but could not, now therefore I write; to see if justice can be given me. I know you will do what is just and right. Of the amount due on my salary as U.S. Vice Consul Genl in the place of Moses A. Hopkins who died at this post of duty in Liberia your   government disallowed me $411.88 which is unjust. I did not spend that much money [unclear, one word]. I buried Moses A Hopkins U.S. Minister Resident and Consul Genl at Monrovia who when he died had not penny with which to lay him away to rest, for which amount of $95.95 I paid out of my pocket to have him bury with honor to your government and your government has disallowed me that amount.

I paid messenger of the Legation $15. per quarter during my stay at that post of duty which also your government has disallowed, because I could not present a vouchers. Your honor the messenger of the Legation was a native boy and could not write and therefore I   was unable to obtain vouchers. Further still I could not draw drafts in his name for such a small sum as $15. per quarter (and often I had to pay him monthly.) therefore I got the firm of G. Moore & Son to pay him and I drew drafts in their name at the end of the quarter. I claim, sir, that at any rate $135.95 if not all of $411.88 which is due me should not be disallowed. I did not do anything while U.S. Vice Consul Genl which my predecessors did not do. I followed them according to the record they left behind. Further more your honor. I took charge of the Legation at Liberia under peculiar circumstances, for I was not acquainted with its laws and regulations, and further more when I took the Legation   in charge I had not filed my bond in the Treasury Department. All I did I did for the honor of your government regardless of feeling for the bereaved family. I want just. I cannot depend upon the widow of Moses A. Hopkins for my pay for what I have done to honor your government in a foreign land. If your government has decided not to pay me for burying Moses A. Hopkins please inform the widow of such in order that I might get the money I have spent. I am a poor man and I need my money, therefore your honor please see that justice be given me. Hoping my humble cry will meet your earnest attention

I remain, Sir,
Respectfully
Sam'l S. Sevier
Ex-Vice Consul Genl.
