No. 3

Consulate General of the United States Monrovia Liberia
J Milton Turner
to Department of State
Washington

Subject

Nomination to the Department of Mr. Gabriel Moore Jr. to be appointed Vice Consul of the United States at Monrovia Republic of Liberia.

 

No. 3

Monrovia May 10th 1875

Sir,

I have the honor now to comply with the instruction conveyed to me by the Department's despatch marked "Seperate," and dated the 25th of February 1875; desiring me under the Regulations, to nominate a suitable person to be appointed Vice-Consul of the United States at this point, to which instruction, I had the honor to allude in my No. 1. to you under date of April 15th 1875. The person whom Honorable
John L. Cadawalder
Assistant Secretary of State   whom I have determined very respectfully to nominate for the purpose mentioned: is named Gabriel Moore Jr.–a man of respectable connections and responsible standing here. Mr Moore is a citizen of Liberia, was born at this place, and is about 22 years of age.

I have the honor to be
Sir,
with expressions of high esteem
Your obedient servant
J Milton Turner

P.S.

Since writing the above, Mr Moore informed that he is named Gabriel David Moore; I have to request that the difference be observed

Respectfully
J Milton Turner
