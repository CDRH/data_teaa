Department of State Received Sep 17 1889

United States Consulate,
Monrovia, La, August 12th 1889

Hon. William F. Wharton
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington, D.C.

Dear Sir,

I have the honor to call your attention to the non-payment of my Draft of October 2nd 1888 for $990.38, being the balance due me for Consular series rendered from April 1st 1888 to June 30th 1888 as per advice of your Predecessor’s No 10 of July 19 1888, and account rendered and adjusted.

Under date of December 11 1888, the Department informed informed   informed me, that the appropriation against which the Draft was drawn was exhausted, and that "the Secretary of the Treasury will make the necessary request to Congress during the present session for an appropriation to pay it, and you will be notified of the result." Since which, I have no advice in reference thereto, therefore I beg to call your attention to same, as well as to the fact, that my Draft was drawn against the salary of the Consul General, as per advice of the Department referred to (July 19th 1888) in which was stated, "The Department has concluded to recognize recognize   recognize you as Vice Consul of the United States at Monrovia, from January 12th last, and to allow you, the full salary of the Consulate General, at the rate of $4,000.00 per annum, from that date until the arrival of Mr Smith, the new Minister Mr Taylor's functions as Consul General ceased January 11th 1888, and Mr Smith's commenced July 10th 1888, the intervening time, I served, and am entitled to the salary as per advice referred to.

If the Draft will be paid, please inform the holders of same, Messers Yates & Porterfield of 19 William Street, New York New York   New York City.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient servant,
Beverly Y. Payne
U.S. Vice Consul
