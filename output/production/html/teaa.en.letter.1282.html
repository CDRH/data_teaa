No. 43. Consular.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia;
Jan. 15, 1900.

Hon. David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to say, that neither the city, nor the Government, has a health officer; nor a board of health; from whom or which we can obtain the desired Sanitary information, demanded in the weekly Sanitary Report, to the Secretary of the Treasury. It is with the utmost vigilance, that we are enabled to make any trustworthy report. We obtain our information, principally, Â  principally, by a constant watch, inquiry, funeral notices, and processions. In these it is very difficult for us to learn of the diseases or causes of death.

The above, I have not communicated to the Secretary of the Treasury.

I have the honor to be
your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith,
M. R. and C. General.

Inclosure.
Dispatch No. 42, Jan. 13, 1900.
