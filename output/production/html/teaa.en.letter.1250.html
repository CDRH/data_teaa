No. 11. Consular Series.
Consulate General of the
United States, Monrovia,
Liberia, Oct. 31, 1898.

Owen L. W. Smith
To
Hon. J. B. Moore,
Assistant Secretary,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to transmit to you the intelligence, that while writing my dispatch No. 10, Oct. 29, 1898, the German Steamer, referred to arrived. Preceding the arrival of said Steamer, the Cabinet was in session. So strong was the opposition, of Hons. G. W. Gibson, Secretary of State, and ex-President, Johnson, now Â  Postmaster General, to the banishment of, and the hireing out of the prisioners of war, that the Cabinet finally decided not to send the prisoners away, but to return them to the country, up the St. Paul's river, and at present leave them in the hands of the President.

Secretary of State and Postmaster General, both declared that they would resign the Cabinet if the prisoners were thus, as at first proposed dealt with. I think the continued existence of the Republic, for any considerable length of time is doubtful. I have the honor to be your obedient servant,
Owen L. W. Smith.
