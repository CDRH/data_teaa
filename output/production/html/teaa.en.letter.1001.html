Department of State Received Oct 19 1886
No. 28.

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia, Sept. 16, 1886.

Mr. Sevier to the Secretary of State, Washington.

Subject

Acknowledgement of Despatch No. 6, dated July 15, 1886, and of flags, seal, and blank drafts.

Abstract

Despatch No. 6 dated July 15, 1886 to late Consul Genl. Moses A. Hopkins acknowledged; also two flags, a seal and press, and blank drafts. Request for estimate of cost of furniture noted, and paragraph of Consular Regulations referred to.

 
No. 28

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia, September 16, 1886

Hon. T. F. Bayard,
Secretary of State,
Washington

Sir,

I have the honor of acknowledging your despatch of July 15th last (No. 6) addressed to the late Consul General Hopkins, advising the sending to this Consulate General of two flags, a seal and press, and some blank drafts: all of which have been duly received.

I note your request   request for estimate of cost of furniture; and the paragraph referred to in the Consular Regulations.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
Samuel S. Sevier
Acting Consul Gen.
