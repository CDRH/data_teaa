Department of State Received Jul 9 1889
No. 23 Consulate General, U. S. A.
Monrovia, Liberia, June 1st 1889 Mr. Ezekiel E. Smith,
To the Department of State. SUBJECT:

Small Pox in Liberia.

ABSTRACT OF CONTENTS.

Cases reported in New Georgia. New Georgia is about 3 miles distant.

Â  No. 23 Consulate General, U. S. A.
Monrovia, Liberia, June 1st 1889 Honorable William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D. C. Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to inform you that cases of Small Pox are reported to exist in New Georgia, a distance of some three miles east from Monrovia. Two deaths, so far, are said to have occurred from the effects of the disease.

The American barque, "Monrovia," of New York, H. J. Rogers master, cleared on the 28th ult, with a "clear" Bill of Health, no official report having reached this office.

I am, sir,
Your obedient servant,
Ezekiel E. Smith
U.S. Consul General.
