Subject

Acknowledgement of the receipt of Dispatch No 3-4 with inclosed pasports
Delivery of the Records & Archives.

Abstract of Content

To inform the Department of the delivery of Records, Archives &c of the U.S. Consulate to me by Mr Broadhurst, and a copy of an inventory of same.

Enclosure

Copy of inventory.

 
No 2

United States Consulate
at Sierra Leone Africa
Oct 12th 1878

Sir-

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your Dispatch No 3-4 with special pasport, under date of the 28 ultimo. The copy of Consular Regulations with a Register of the Department under seperate cover have also been received.

I beg to inform the Department, that on the 4th inst Mr Broadhurst the Acting Consul, delivered to me all of the Records, Flag, Books, and papers he acknowledges to be in his possession, belonging to the U.S. Consulate.

Mr Broadhurst refused to give one an inventory of the Articles delivered, and in order that the Department may see what is now in the Consulate, I have taken an inventory of every[?] thing delivered to me by said Gentlemen as belonging to the U.S. Consulate. A copy of which I have inclosed.

In Entering upon my duties as Consul, I have deferred informing the Authorities here, Officially of my Appointment, until I have received my Commission and Exequatur.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your Obedient Servant,
John A. Parm
U.S. Consul

Hon. F. W. Seward
Acting Secretary of State
