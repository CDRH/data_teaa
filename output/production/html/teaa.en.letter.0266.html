Recd June 27

Gaboon April 23. 1861.

To the Hon.
The Secretary of State
U.S. America.

Sir,

I have honor to inform you that I have the keeping of some U. States property connected with the Commercial Agency of this place. It was left in my care by the late Henry A Ford M.D. and has been in the Mission Store till the present time. About one month since I opened two of the boxes to see whether the climate may have injured the contents, I only inspected them enough to know that neither climate or insects had touched them, & closed them as before. One box seemed to contain books &c. the other copying press. There is a third box (small) & a package or book in a brown paper envelope. This property is as safe as the property of the Missions of the Am. Board of Commissioner for Foreign Missions.

I write this simply to inform the Department of the condition of the property and its location.

As our latest dates from the United States are only to Feb. 6th, & not being entirely certain that your locum tenens is the City of Washington. I enclose this to a friend A. [unclear, one name] Esqr. Bible House, Astor Place N. York

Very Respectfully Yours
W. Walker.
Missionary of A.B.C.F.M.
