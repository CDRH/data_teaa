Washington D.C.
March 23, 1888

Hon. G. L. Rives
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I am informed by the accounting offices of the Treasury, that a portion of my salary account is suspended on the ground of exhaustion of the appropriation for the payment of Consular offices, not citizens of the United States, and I now have the honor to request that you cause the Auditor to be informed that such an objection cannot properly be urged in my case, for the reason that I am now and ever have been a citizen of the United States. I have exercised the privileges of a voter in Penna. and have never Â  exercised like privileges or attempted to do so anywhere outside of the United States.

Requesting early and favorable action by the Department in this matter.

I have the honor to be, Sir,
Your Obedient Servant
Saml S. Sevier
Ex-Vice Consul Genl.
at Monrovia
Liberia Africa

Hon. G L Rives
Secretary of State
Washington D.C.
