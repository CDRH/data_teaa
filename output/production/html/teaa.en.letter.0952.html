No. 35.

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia, Liberia, 22. November 1877.

Sir:

I had the honor in No. 34. dated October the sixteenth to explain the circumstances which influenced me to offer the position of Commercial Agent at Grand Bassa, to Henry Augustus Williams Esquire, instead of Honorable J. J. Cheeseman as suggested by Mr. Crusoe and approved by me in my despatch No. 31. I have now to inform the Department that while at Bassa, a few days since, Mr. Williams having accepted my offer of Honorable Fredrick W. Seward
Ast. Secretary of State, Washington.   of the Agency, I took occasion to receipt to Mr Crusoe for the Archives thereof and to deliver the same into the possession of Mr Williams, with instructions to discharge the duties of the office until the pleasure of the Department be expressed with reference to his more permanent occupancy thereof. Mr Williams' receipt to me for the Archives is marked enclosure No 2.; and his Bond for Three thousand dollars ($3,000) as required by law, is also transmitted herewith, and marked enclosure No. 3.

My reason for inviting attention to enclosure No. 4. will be apparent upon the reading thereof: That enclosure is a copy of my despatch to the Government of Liberia requesting   requesting permission for Mr Williams to be Acting Commercial Agent at Grand Bassa. The Liberian Government was pleased to extend to Mr Williams the necessary permission as may be seen by reference to enclosure No. 5. which is a copy of the Secretary of State's note upon that subject. It may not be amiss for me to express my my hope for the Department's approval, and that the designation of Mr. Williams for that office may prove satisfactory.

I have the honor to be
Sir:
Your obedient servant
J. Milton Turner

Enclosure No. 4

Legation of the United States
Monrovia 20 November 1877.

Sir:

I have the honor to regret that the pemission of your Government be extended to Henry Augustus Williams Esquire, of Edina Grand Bassa County, to be Acting United States Commercial Agent at that point, until such time as my Government shall think proper to appoint a successor to H. Layfayette Crusoe, Esquire, resigned. Referring, respectfully, in this connection to the reasons assigned by your Government, in your note of the sixth ultimo, for Honorable J. W. Blackledge
Secretary of State, Liberia.   for withholding from J. J. Cheeseman Esqr. the necessary permission to act in the capacity herein referred to, it is desired to explain: that the change from Mr. Cheeseman to Mr. Williams was influenced by the belief that my Government does not desire that any gentleman, a citizen of Liberia, should become the acting or commissioned incumbent of the office of United States Commercial Agent at Grand Bassa, provided such gentleman cannot enjoy in that capacity the pleasure of the Government of his own country. However, in view of the stipulation between your Government and that of the United States, to treat each other upon the basis of "the most favored nation   nation," it is my duty, Mr. Secretary, to point respectfully to reasons why the reason you give in your note of the sixth ultimo, withholding from J. J. Cheeseman Esqr. the necessary permission to act is believed inapplicable. I beg, Mr. Secretary, that you will allow me to refer you for illustration of this position to the following facts: The late Consul for Sweden and Norway, near your Government, W. F. Nelson, deceased; was a citizen of Liberia, who at his death was both the Consul for the power mentioned, and Mayor of the city of Monrovia: And, I may allude, respectfully, to the present venerable and distinguished Consul for Hati, near your Government, who is also the present Judge of the Court of Probate   Probate of Montserrado County. It is deemed unnecessary to mention several additional precedents of like nature, which are believed to be at hand. Doubtless, the instances cited will be regarded sufficient to sustain the belief referred to as relating to the reason assigned for withholding the permission necessary for Mr. Cheeseman to act in the interest of the United States while he holds an office in the Government of the Republic of Liberia.

I have the honor to be
Sir:
With high considerations and esteem
Your obedient servant
J. Milton Turner

Enclosure No. 5.

Department of State
Monrovia November 21, 1877.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your despatch of yesterday's date; informing the Government of Liberia, of the appointment - temporary - of Henry Augustus Williams of the County of Grand Bassa, as Acting U.S. Commercial Agent, until such time as the U.S. Government may deem it proper to appoint a successor to H. L. Crusoe Esqr. resigned. In reply, I beg to assure you that the Government of Liberia has no objections of offer to the appointment.

I have the honor to be
Sir Your obt. Servt.
(sd.) J. W. Blackledge

J. M. Turner
U.S. Min. Res.
Monrovia Liberia.
