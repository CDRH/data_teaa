U.S. Consulate General.
Monrovia July 1, 1878.

Sir.

Having been left in charge of this Consulate, by his Excellency J. M. Turner and instructed by him to report directly to your department, I therefore have the honor to inform you that his Excellence has left Monrovia on the 7th of May inst. for Cape Palmas to meet the bark Fury. Capt. Hubbard - foresaid vessel would sail from Cape Palmas direct for Boston U.S.A. - from the date his Excellence left Monrovia, I have enter on the discharge of my duties as Vice Consul General Hon. F. W. Seward
Assist Secretary of State
Washington   I have to acknowledge since the absence of J. Mn Turner Esq, the reciept of the dispatches No 19 - 165. U.S. official register 1877, and the Congressional records from April 6th to May 11, 1878.

I have the honor to transmit also, the reports for the quarter ending June 30th 1878 - according to the instructions of his Excellence J. M. Turner. I have drawn a draft on the Hon. Secretary of State, for the amount of disbursements ($50.14) in favor of Messrs Dennis & Bro at Monrovia. (Acceptance Waived)

There is in my hands the amount of $29 20/100 collected as Consular fees, but have not made a quarterly return of this to the department, the rason herefore is, that I beg to be informed by your honor to what amount I am allowed to draw for my compensation   compensation, and shall than make a correct account in full. I see in the Consular Regulations that the fees will be aplied first to the payment of the Compensation. I beg to mention here, that there is no contract excisting between his Excellence J. M. Turner and myself, regulating my salary. It is also my duty to call you honors attention to the fact that this Consulate is entirely out of Cap paper, penholders pens ink etc. and the only flag in the Consulate possession is so badly torn up that the same is unserviceable.

Seizing this opportunity to assure you of my highest consideration and esteem
I have the honor to be Sir
Your obedt servant
M. A. Aenmey
