No. 5
Department of State Received Jan 28 1889

Consulate of the United States,
Loanda, December 5, 1888

Mr. R. S. Newton
To the Department of State

Subject.

Leave of Absence

Abstract of Contents

Requesting leave of absence, and authority to nominate E. Bannister to assume the duties during such absence.

 
No. 5

Consulate of the United States
Loanda, December 5, 1888

Honorable G. L. Rives,
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform the Department, that having been here over 20 years, it has been my custom of late to leave to leave Loanda for two or three months about February or March for a change of air, this being the commencement of what is considered the most unhealthy season; and although I have only just resumed my duties as U.S. Vice Consul I have the   honor to request the Department to grant me three months' leave of absence dating from or about the months above mentioned.

I have also to request the Department to allow me to nominate my clerk, Mr Edward Bannister to take charge of the archives and assume the duties during my absence He is well up in the duties of the Consulate and acted for me in 1887. His Bond will no doubt still exist in the Department

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient servant
Robt S. Newton
Vice Consul
