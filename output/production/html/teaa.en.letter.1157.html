Department of State Received Mar 5 1891

No 10 Dispatch Consulate Series;
Legation of the United States;
Monrovia Liberia January 29, 1891
Mr Alexander Clark;
To the Department of State;

Subject:

Transmitting of Quarterly Returns from Elmina;

Synopsis;

No 6. Consulate at Elmina January 6th 1891; viz

Enclosures 12

3 copies of Arrivals and Departures of American vessels;
3 copies of Summary of business for the quarter ending December 31st 1890
3 copies of Return of Trade with the United States with the Gold Coast Colony for the year 1890
3 Detailed Reports of Official Services; & &c. &c.

 

No 10 Dispatch Consul Series;
Legation of the United States;
Monrovia Liberia January 29, 1891;

To the Honorable;
William F. Wharton;
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington DC;

Sir;

I have the honor respectfully to transmit the accounts and returns of the Consul Agent at Elmina (Mr Geo E. Eminsang) for the Quarter ending December 31st 1890 Viz; 12 enclosures in Triplicates as follows Enclosures

No 1 Arrivals and Departures of American vessels. one copy I enclose to you one copy to the 5th Auditor of the Treasury and one copy reserved for the Legation

No 2. Summary of Business for the quarter, do.

No 3. Return of Trade with the United States Showing the Imports.   And exports between the United States and the Gold Coast Colony for the year 1890
Do "

No 4. Detailed Report of Official Service &c Marked "B"
Do "

I further enclose a verbatim copy of Consul Agent Eminsang letter accompaning the enclosure Per No. 5.

No 5 enclosures and call your notice to the fact that said letter was dated at Elmina Jany 6st 1891, and received at this Legation January 29 inst which shows the poor and irregular mail facilitys we enjoy having only one mail inward and outward, bound every fortnight and oftener every three weeks.

I have the honor to be Sir:
Your Obedient Servant
Alexander Clark Minister Resident and Consul general of the US
