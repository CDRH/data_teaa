Department of State Received Oct 6 1888

Monrovia, Liberia,
August 31st 1888.

Mr B. Y. Payne, to the Assistant Secretary of State.

Subject.

Receipt of your No 10 of July 19th 1888 with inclosures.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your above noted, with inclosure of Statement of account, and blank Draft, for service rendered from January 12th to March 31st last; informing me that the Department had concluded to recognize me as Vice Consul of the United States at Monrovia, from January 12th last, and to allow me full salary of the Consulate General from that date date   date until the arrival of Mr Smith: for which you have my sincere thanks.

I beg to state again for your information that the two messengers were employ'd by Mr Taylor, and left at the Legation. I am surprise at his not informing the Department of the fact. I was of the same opinion of the Department, that one messenger was sufficient, and, I discharged one at the end of March, as you will have observed by my June Quarter return.

My claim of $82 50/100 has been demanded of Mr Taylor by the holders of my Draft, and I have been informed of Mr Taylor's refusal to settle it.

As my account for contingent expense for June quarter contained nothing but that was not approved in previous previous   previous account, I shall draw in due course on the Department for services from March 31st to July 10th last, the date of Mr Smith's arrival.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne.

Hon., G. L. Rives,
Assistant Sec'y State.

Inclosure,

Statement of Account to March 31st 1888.
