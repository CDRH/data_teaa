No 29

Consulate of the United States,
St. Paul de Loanda 15 August 1889

Honorable Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington D.C.

Sir:

Having been unwell for some time and it being absolutely necessary that I should take a trip to Europe. I have the honor to inform the Department that I have taken advantage of the leave of absence granted one in your Despatch No 24 of Feby 12th 1889 and I purpose leaving to morrow morning by the Portuguese mail packet "Ambaca" for Europe I have handed over to Mr. Bannister the Archives of the Consulate who assumed the duties of Acting Vice Consul during my absence.

My address in London will be care of John M. Grant Esqr 63 Cornhill, London

I have honor to be
Sir
Your obedient servant
Robt S. Newton
Vice Consul
