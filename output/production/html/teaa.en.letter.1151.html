Department of State Received Jan 16 1891

No 5 dispatch Consulate Series,
Legation of the United States
Monrovia Liberia December 3rd 1890

Subject

Recept of Parcels.

Synopsis:

 

No 5 Consul Series,
Legation of the United States
Monrovia Liberia December 8th 1890

To the Honorable William F. Wharton
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir;

I have the honor respectfully to acknowledge the receipt of several parcels addressed to the Legation containing viz.

1 copy Foreign Relations of the United States for 1889.

2 copies Special consular reports on Cotton textiles in foreign countries.

2 copies Special consular reports on Malt and Beer[?] in Spanish America.

2 copies Special consular reports on Carpet[?] manufacture in foreign countries.

[unclear, one number] copies consular reports 2. each May, June, and July Nos 116-117 and 118 of 1890.

 

2 Copies Message from the President tracking[?] the Subject in dispute between the government of the United States and Grate Britain in the Behring Sea.

4 Copies No 330 Act to reduce the revenue[?] and equalize duties on imports and for other purposes.

2 Copies Report on condition of crops for 1890

2 copies of the inestimable Report of the International American Conference relative to intercontinental railway line and several forms Nos, 138 - 139 - 140 - 171 and 172.

I have the honor to remain
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
Alexander Clark
Minister Resident and Consul General of U.S.
