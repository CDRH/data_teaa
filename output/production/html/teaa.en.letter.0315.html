No 1.

Commercial Agency of the United States of America at Sierra Leone

May 15th, 1868

Sir,

I have the honor to inform you that Mr. Henry Rider, late United States Commercial Agent at this place, is compelled by severe illness, under the urgent advise of his physician to leave the Colony by this Steamer, the "Mandingo".

Mr. Rider has requested me to perform, during his absence, the duties of the United States Commercial Agency, which to the F. W. Seward Esqre
Assistant Secretary
etc. etc. etc. the best of my abilities I promise faithfully to do.

Enclosed I beg to hand you the transfer of Archives and all other properly belonging to the United States.

His Excellency Sir Arthur Edward Kennedy, Governor-in-Chief over the West African Settlements has, upon Mr. Rider's request, approved of my being appointed acting Commercial Agent of the United States.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
Rosenbush.

Commercial Agency of the United States of Sierra Leone
May 15th, 1868.

We certify, on this the fifteenth day of May 1868, the services of Henry Rider ceased, and he is entitled to his Salary, or fees, including said day; and the services of Theodore Amandus Rosenbush, acting, commenced the day following, he having received the archives, a full and complete inventory of which is hereto annexed, as required by the Consular Regulations, Chapter XXIII.

(Seal)

Rosenbush, Acting U.S. Commercial Agent
Henry Rider
late U.S. Commercial Agent
