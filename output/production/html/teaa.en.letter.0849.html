Department of State Received Jul 10 1880
No 27

U.S. Consulate at
Sierra Leone June 5th 1880

Hon John Hay
Asst Secretary of State
Washington DC

Sir,

I have the honor to acknowledge receipt of your dispatch No 14th of April 28th, wherein you acknowledge Nos 19, 20, & 21 from this office.

Inclosed herewith please find Copies of Declaration, Inventories, and distressed seamans expense a/c.

I have to report the loss of Brig "Ann Elizabeth" of Boston, at Opubo W. C. Africa, and the death of Capt E. G. Joy and E Johnsen seaman. their effects were forwarded to the collector of customs Boston, under seal, for Bark "Jury" on 2nd inst. to be handed over to their proper representitive

Â 

One of the seamen was sent to the Consul General London, one cared for himself and four were sent to Boston per Bark "Fury"

I have drawn on the State Department at 15 d/s for $356.10/100 favor of Messrs Yates & Porterfield for a/c of distressed seamen which I trust will be protected.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obt Servant
M. W. Tobey
U.S. Vice Consul
