Recd Jan 31

U.S. Commercial Agency
Gaboon River Dec 1st 1862

Hon William H Seward
Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Dear Sir

I have the honor to inform you that I arrived here upon the 3d day of November 1862.

The vessel upon which I took passage from the United States was unavoidably delayed, causing me to be much longer reaching this place than I anticipated when I left New York.

In order to account for any informalities that may exist in the present correspondence permits me to state that the book forwarded me from the departments of state entitled Instructions to Consuls and Commercial Agents was mislaid in America and has not yet reached me. I would respectfully request that another book be sent me   I have received information through Commodore Baron Didelot of the French fleet upon this coast that there are two iron clad rebel gun boats at the islands of Cape De Verde, which had succeeded in destroying a number of American Merchant vessels and were now in wait for the United States ship of war Saratoga supposed by them to be bound up the Coast. I have no information in regard to the whereabouts of the Saratoga. I have written to the Comml Agt Monrovia and to Cape Palmas as they are the only points upon the Coast where she would be likely to stop if bound towards the De Verde islands from down the coast Commodore T.[?] B. received his information through the Portuguese mail that runs from Lisbon to St Paul de Loando.   By information received from the same source, (Commodore D.) from the Spanish authorities at Fernando Po I have reason to apprehend that the same difficulties that existed between the governments of the United States and Spain in the years 1859 and 1860 in relation to Spanish interference with American Missionaries upon the island of Corisco are likely to be revived.

A copy of the regulations of the Hon Secretary of treasury to which reference is made in Circular No 17, of July 31st 1862, has not reached me

I have the honor to be your most obt servant
Henry May Com. Agt.
