No 30.

United States Consular Agency at Elmina
Dec. 20, 1886

Hon. Saml. S. Sevier
United States Vice Consul
Monrovia Liberia.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your despatch dated Monrovia Oct. 27/86, with the enclosed copy of a despatch No. 224 from the Department of State written by the Vice Consul Gen. of Sierra Leone making complaints against me. The above arrived on 19th inst. In reply I   I beg to say–

1st That Jacob Smith was one of the crew of the brig Giles Loring of Portland Me. Geo. Klose master. the vessel arrived here in distress, being in a leaky condition.

2d That men from ashore were put on board to keep the pumps going and the ships crew to look after ship and cargo whilst the ship was lightened to discover the leak if possible.

3rd That said Jacob Smith run away during the night and stopped on shore for several days and nights after the report of the master   to this Consulate I send in search of said Jacob Smith.

4th That he was found in a disorderly condition in the Street and the Police brought him to this Consulate.

I ordered him to go on board the ship and found a canoe to take him on board.

5th That when he got to the beach or warf he refused to go off and after making some trouble the Police locked him up.

6th That the next day he was brought to this Consulate I tried my best to explain to him that he better go on board the ship and attend his   his work and that as soon as the ship was found to be unseaworthy they all would be paid off and sent home but if he did not do so he will be taken as a deserter this was in presence of the master; he still refused to go on board the vessel, saying he had found some of his relations here and he is not an American and does not intend to go to the ship again nor to Boston and I had nothing to do with him.

7th That it was found out that the said Jacob Smith had smuggled   his personal property on shore long ago.

8th That after he had told me he was not an American subject and had nothing to do with United States Consulate I did not trouble myself any more about him.

9th That he Jacob Smith enlisted himself with the British Government out here.

10th That after some months he employed a Lawyer to sue the Captain for his wages. the Lawyer finding it was a bad case and that he Jacob Smith did   did not tell him the truth gave it up.

Hoping the above explanation will be satisfactory.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your obedient servant
G. E. Eminsang
U.S. Consular Agent.
