Index Bureau Dept of State Received May 27 93
No. 24. Consulate-General, U. S. A.,
Monrovia, Liberia, April 13, 1893 Mr William D. McCoy,
To the Department of State. SUBJECT:

Arrest of Loui Songninett, Cook on Brig Manson.

ABSTRACT OF CONTENTS.   No. 24. Consulate-General, U. S. A.,
Monrovia, Liberia, April 13, 1893 Honorable William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
WASHINGTON, D. C. Sir:

I respectfully report that Loui Songninett, Cook on the Brig Manson, was accused of assaulting one Charles Sumner McGill, a Liberian subject, while the vessel was lying in this harbor.

Upon the proper warrant being issued I ordered the Captain of the vessel to take the accused over to the proper authorities.

I met him immediately upon his arrival on shore, accompanied him to the Magistrate's Court, and after considering the matter advised him to plead guilty which he did. There were mitigating circumstances in his favor but probably of not sufficient weight to have secured an acquittal.

The fine was $10.00, and cost $3.15. The   The fine was paid in Liberian currency making the actual cost $7.50, & $3.15 the cost = $10.65 the actual amount in coin.

Captain Reeve paid the amount, charging the same to Loui Songninett's account and I then had him returned to his vessel.

I have the honor to be, Sir–
Your obedient servant,

William D. McCoy,
U.S. Consul-General
