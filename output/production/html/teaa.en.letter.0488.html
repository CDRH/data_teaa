Department of State Received Apr 18 1876
No 9.

United States Consulate
Sierra Leone W.C. of Africa
March 18th 1876

Sir:

Requiring a temporary change of climate I beg to inform you that I intend soon to proceed to Madeira for a few weeks residence there.

During my absence I have in charge of this Office with the Archives etc. thereto belonging James Frame Esqre, who was also my representant on the occasion of my former absence from my post.

The Government here has been duly notified of the above fact.

Trusting that you will approve of and sanction my temporary leave leave

I have the honor to be
Sir,
Your most obedient servant
W H Randall
U.S. Consul.

To the Honorable
The Secretary of State
Washington D.C.
