Department of State Received Nov 26 1890

Consulate General of the United States
Monrovia, October 18th 1890.

Mr Beverly Y. Payne,
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Receipt of Despatch.

 

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, October 18th 1890.

Honorable William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge receipt of your Despatch (no number) of the 16th ult., with enclosed copy of Mr John F. Brook's letter of 11th ult., to Hon. William Cogswell, urging the appointment of a United States Consul at Saltpond: the subject of which has had my careful attention, and I have the honor to report that Saltpond is about the centre of the Gold Coast under English Protectorate, and Mr George E. Eminsang, the United States Consular Agent residing   residing at Elmina, is a native African but has become a Naturalized subject of Great Britain. If he has been in anyway partial to the interest of American Citizens at his post, this Consulate is not aware of the fact.

In regards to establishing a full Consulate on the Gold Coast, may be decided by the Department consulting Mr Eminsang reports which have been transmitted.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne
U.S. Vice Consul General.
