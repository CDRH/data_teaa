Commercial Agency of the United States of America at Sierra Leone 5th November 1869.

Sir,

Your despatch No. 11, dated 25th August last and covering Circular No. 71, has been received.

Mr. Henry Rider the Commercial Agent has returned to this settlement; I enclose copy of a letter of that Officer requesting me to continue to discharge the functions of this Commercial Agency, until further directed.

I also enclose copy of a letter received from Captain W. Graham of Her W. B. Davies Esqre
Acting Secretary
Washington D.C.   Britanic Majesty's Ship "Danae", together with copy of my reply thereto.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
Rosenbush.
U.S. Vice Comml Agent

H. M. S. "Danae"
Sierra Leone, 19th October 1869.

Sir,

On the occasion of my visiting the Island of Anno Bon on the 25th August 1869, the Master of the American Whaling Schooner "Herald" informed me that an American Citizen, by name Claude Payrott had been left on the Island sick and in a destitute condition, there being no constituted authority on the Island, although claimed by Spain I took the man on board this ship for Medical care. He was then in a very emaciated condition but is now convalescent, but hardly fit to be landed at an African Port; I await your decision as to The Consul for the United States at Sierra Leone   his disposal.

I am bound for Halifax, Nova Scotia; if you make me a requisition to that effect, I will convey him to Halifax and place him in the hands of the United States Consul at that place.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
"signed" "W. Graham"
Captain.

Commercial Agency of the United States of America at Sierra Leone 19th October 1869

Sir,

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your communication of this day's date informing me of your having on board of your ship an American seaman by the name of "Claude Payrott", who had been left on the Island of Anno Bon in a sick and destitute condition

In reply I beg to thank you for your kind and humane attentions to the destitute man I am also extremely obliged for your considerate offer to convey him to Halifax, which I gladly accept and beg you will be Captain W Graham
Commanding H.M.S. "Danae"
Sierra Leone   pleased to place him in the hands of the United States Consul on your arrival at that port.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
"signed" "T. Rosenbush"
U.S. Vice Commercial Agent
