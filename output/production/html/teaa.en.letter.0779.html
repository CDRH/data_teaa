No 196

U.S. Consulate Goree-Dakar
November 17th 1896

To the Honorable W. W. Rockhill
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington

Sir: In the "Review of the Worlds Commerce", years 1894-1895, it is stated that, "The United States is represented by a Consul at Goree-Dakar, but no recent Commercial Reports of the Colony of Senegal (French) have been received from this Officer." Remarks from other scources to the same effect have from time to time been brought to my notice, and it is to explain that there has been no wilful negligence in the matter that I am writing this Dispatch.

It requires care, trouble, and some expense to prepare Commercial Reports for such a Colony as this which will be accurate and not misleading: and for such Reports this Consulate, as at present organized, affords no adequate compensation. For the June Quarter of this year my salary from Fees amounted to only $2.79, and I have been to expense and trouble for things connected with the Consulate which actually makes the office a burden, which I have often thought seriously of relinquishing, and yet the Commercial importance of this colony is immensely greater than when the Consulate was first established. England, which has no greater interests here than what the United States might have, gives her Counsel here one thousand pounds annually. I have several times asked for one fifth of this sum with the right to do business, but so far without success. But without as much as this I am confident that no blame will attach to me because I cannot occupy valuable time in preparing Reports I am Sir
Very truly Yours
Peter Strickland, U.S. Consul
