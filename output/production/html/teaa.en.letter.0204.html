Hon John Davis
Asst Secty of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to state that by the British Mail S.S. Bosmy which arrived on the 4th inst 3 Men a young boy and a girl all from Dondo; and who accompanied the German explores Lieut Weissmann in crossing Africa from Dondo; have returned in good health, they report that their voyage across was very pleasant, and that they never had nay troubles with the Natives of the interior. that all the chiefs received them very well; although they had no presents to give them. and that from tribe to tribe they were accompanied by some chiefs. They went to Zanzibar where they were put on board the British S.S. "Chittagong" with about 250 more Zanzibarians under the command of a Belgian called Capt. Cambier, for Mr. Henry Mr. Stanley expedition and where landed at Banana on the Congo and proceeded at once to Mr. Henry M. Stanley Head quarters and.

The British Mail S.S. Bosmy brought Mr. Weissman followers here.

The strong impression here is: that a great disturbance is expected on the upper Congo very soon.

Eleven New German House have established themselves between Gaboon and Ambrizette and it is reported that another monthly line of Steamer from Hamburg will run between that place and the West cast as far as Ambrizette. Five Belgian houses are also established since my arrival here.

With the expectations of the troubles in the interior, the Ivory and others produce have been keep back, and freight on the coast very hard to be obtained for the present. which leave some of the large commission merchants in a very bad state and I am expecting daily to hear of some failures in Loanda.

It would be in my opinion the best moment for Americans enterprising man from some good firm in America to come and start four trading post on the Coast and making its head quarters at Loanda. With money now and Am: goods a firm can triple her capital in two[?] years. But they must recollect that Americans coming here, must have a good knowledge of the Portuguese language.

With this qualification, of course, a thorough business man would soon become familiar with the business methods of the place, peculiarities of tariffs &ca &ca

I would also state that Vice Consul Robert S. Newton has returned form England as the British Vice Consul, and has not yet reported himself to me.

I have the honor to be
Sir,
Your obedient servant
L. de R. du Verge
US Consul
