No. 6

Department of State,
Washington, August 15, 1889.

Emory H. Taunt, Esquire,
Commercial Agent of the United States, Boma.
(now in Washington.)

Sir:

I have to acknowledge the receipt of your dispatch No. 12, dated at Culpeper, Virginia, on the 5th instant, in relation to the relief of certain citizens of the United States now on the Congo River, belonging to a "faith cure sect," and to say that there is no appropriation from which the passage home of these people could be paid, destitute seamen being the only class of American American citizens entitled by law to such aid.

I am, Sir,
Your obedient servant
William F Wharton
Acting Secretary.
