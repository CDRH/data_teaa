Department of State Received Jun 2 1888

No 10.

Consulate General of the United States.
Monrovia, Liberia, April 30th 1888.

Mr B. Y. Payne to the Secretary of State

Subject:

Official visit, and reception of Governor Rowe.

Sir.

Referring to my No 9 of the 26th inst., I have the honor to inform you, that, Sir Samuel Rowe, Governor, and Commander in Chief of Her Britanic Magesty's West African Settlements, was escorted to the Executive Mansion by a Military escort, and members of the Municipal Government Government Hon., T. F. Bayard,
Sec'y of State   Government and received by His Excellency the President of Liberia at 10 O'clock A.M. on the 27th inst., in the presence of the Members of his Cabinet Foreign Representatives and distinguished Gentlemen; at which time and place, Governor Rowe, stated the cause of his visit being official, the Exchange of the Ratification of the North Western Boundary Treaty of 1885; which was concluded during the day.

His Excellency the Governor was entertained at 4 O'clock at the Executive Mansion, by his Excellency that President of Liberia, and distinguished distinguished   distinguished guest, and by the Mayor in behalf of the Municipal authority on the following morning. The Governor left the same day (the 28th) for Sierra Leone in Her Magesty's ship of war "Royalist" he having completed his official business.

I have the honor to be,
Sir
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne.
