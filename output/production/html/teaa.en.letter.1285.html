No. 46, Consular Series,
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, May 14, 1900.

Mr. James Robert Spurgeon,
To
Hon. David J. Hill, Asst. Secty of State,
Washington.

Subject.

U.S. Pensioner's mail robbed.

Synopsis.

Isaac Ketter, pension certificate #54773 and Julius C. Taylor, pension certificate #909720 not having received their returns institutes an investigation which discloses the fact that their checks had be extracted from the envelope etc.

 

No. 46, Consular Series,
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, May 14, 1900.

Honorable David J. Hill,
Asst. Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform you that the mail from Mr. S. L. Wilson United States Pension Agent at Washington, containing checks from the Treasury Department and vouchers for Pensioners in Liberia has been repeatedly tampered with from time to time. Sometimes Pensioners would receive the envelope containing the voucher but the check would be missing. During the Past two months there has been repeated inquiries as to the reason of Pensioners not having received any returns at all from the Pension Agent.   It appears now that we have a clew to the matter. An under clerk in the local post office is suspected and he has left for parts as yet unknown. We do not know to what extent this robbery of the mail has gone on but of the pensioners, Isaac Ketter, certificate 54,773 and Julius C. Taylor 909,720 certificate appear to be the known loosers. They employed attorneys and instituted an investigation. The result is they are on the track of parties who have been seen with the checks.

I would suggest that in the future all mail from the department at Washington containing drafts or checks or Treasury notes be registered and sealed officially. There is great regard here for a letter impressed with the U.S. seal.

I have the honor to be, sir,
Your obedient servant,
James Rob't Spurgeon
Chargé.
