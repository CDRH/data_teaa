Old and Useless Official documents in the Legation, especially those of date prior to the Year 1885; &c

No 11 Dispatch Consulate General Series
United States Legation Monrovia
Liberia February 12th 1891;

To the Honorable William F. Wharton
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington DC;

Sir;

I have the honor to acknowledge the recept of your circular (SD, 12- 12- 90- 400) from the Department of State under the date of December 12th 1890; In reply to said circular I respectfully submit the following:

1st There are a goodly number of old unbound or untied dispatches in the Legation of date prior to the year 1885 which seemingly can never be of use to the department or this office;

2nd There are ten (10) packages of dispatches and printed circulars dating back and down to this date which are neatly bound or tied with paper covering   covering by the Honorables John H. Smyth Samuel S. Sevier and M. A. Anemiey; and a large number of unbound dispatches of the Honorable J. Milton Turner and others which are in a condition that makes them unfit for binding. The Hon E. E. Smith left quite a number that are unbound but can be tied or bound easily and they are worth preserving;

3 There are 15 Record books from Grand Bassa and Cape Palmas and two (2) worn out seals and flag which are mentioned in my dispatch Consular Series No. 2. of November 20th 1890 as Inclosure No 2. inventory;

4 There are quite a number of old letters and domestic Dispatches that may be of use.

5 There are 100 blank drafts in the safe that are not negotiable here without considerable expence and trouble see inclosure marked No 1. for sample of said drafts

 

All the aforementioned can be kept without any additional expence as the messenger boy has time to care for them in common with the Library books which must be seen after once or twice a fortnight and there are sufficient room in the Legation rooms without any inconveniences &c Yet if it is thought advisable by the department that those dispatches that are untied, dirty and mildewed Should not be kept please so instruct me, with reference to drafts please send by mail blank form of my first draft draft No one (1) dated November 20, 1890 for Salery transmit &c

Sir; I have the honor
to be your obedient Servant Alexander Clark;
US Minister Resident & Consul Gen
