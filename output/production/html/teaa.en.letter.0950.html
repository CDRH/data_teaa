No. 33.

United States Consulate General,
Monrovia, Liberia
1st Octo. 1877.

Sir,

I have the honor to transmit herewith my Report for the quarter ended 30th Sept. 1877.

I regret that I am compelled by the difficulties attendant upon the collection of facts and figures necessary to ascertain the commerce at state Honorable
F. W. Seward,
Asst. Secretary of State,
Washington   state and relations of Liberia for the fiscal year (which closes, in this country, on the 30th of September) to delay my report thereof for some days. However, I hope to have the honor to forward a statement upon that subject at an early day.

The 30th of September having occured on a Sunday, my Report bears date as above.

I have to be,
Sir,
Your obedient servant
J. Milton Turner
