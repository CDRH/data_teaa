Department of State Received Aug 25 1892

No. 10.

Consulate-General, U. S. A.,
Monrovia, Liberia, July 19, 1892 Mr William D. McCoy,
To the Department of State. SUBJECT:

Report upon the use of Electricity as a Power and in the Propagation and Growth of Plants.

ABSTRACT OF CONTENTS.

No Electric plant in Liberia.

 

No. 10.

Consulate-General, U. S. A.,
Monrovia, Liberia, July 19, 1892. Honorable
Assistant Secretary of State,
WASHINGTON, D. C. Sir:

In compliance with your Instructions to report upon the use of electricity as a power, and in the propagation and growth of plants, I have to say:

There is not an electric plant of any description in Liberia.

Very truly–
Your obedient servant,

William D. McCoy,
Minister-Resident and Consul-General
