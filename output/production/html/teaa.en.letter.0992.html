Department of State Received Oct 22 1879

No. 23. 33

Consulate General of the U.S.,
Monrovia, La, Sept. 17, 1879.

Mr. J. H. Smyth to the Ass't Sec'y of State.

Subject:

Reception of dispatch.

Synopsis:

As to dispatch marked "Separate," date June 24, 1879 & pk'g

 

No. 33.

Consulate General of the U.S.,
Monrovia, La, Sept. 17, 1879.

Sir:

As to furnishing the National Board of Health statistics, I have to respectfully acknowledge the reception of your instruction as contained in your dispatch marked "Separate", dated June 24, 1879, received and blanks.

The difficulty of compliance with this request–save in a very general way–is due to the fact of the absence of my statistics except such as each of the three doctors (here) keeps of his patients, and my general knowledge Hon. F. W. Seward,
Ass't Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.   knowledge of the place.

I will consult with these medical gentleman, and such data as I can secure from time to time as to the health of the district I will gladly forward.

I am, Sir,
Your obd't serv't
Jno. H. Smyth,
U.S. Consul Gen'l
