Department of State Received Feb 3 1887
No 43.

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia Dec. 24/86

Mr S. S. Sevier,
To the Secty. of State.

Subject

Arrival and departure of the U.S. Naval ship "Quinnebaug".

Abstract of Contents.

Arrival of the ship. official visit of Lieutenant Commander upon the President of the Republic. Visit of his Excellency the President and his Cabinet to the Naval ship. departure of the ship.

 

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia 24/86

Hon. T. F. Bayard
Secty of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to inform the Department of State that the U.S. Naval Ship "Quinnebaug" arrived at this port of Monrovia on the morning of Dec. 20/86 A boat was sent a shore with two officers and tendered me a passage to the ship which I   I was courteously received by Commander G. W. Coffin. The followering day the Lieutenant Commander who was unwell, was officially received at the Exective mansion by his Excellency the President of the Republic, where changes of ordinary civilities were performed.

Wednesday Dec. 22/86 the President and his cabinet paid a visit to the Quinnebaug in company with me where we were cordially received.

 

On the 23/86 She left for the Congo with intention of stopping at all the Liberian ports. Please find inclosed a copy of a despatch from the Secty of State of the Department of Liberia.

I am Sir
Your obedient Servant
Sam'l S. Sevier
Acting Consul Gen.

Inclosures

1. Despatch from the Secty. of State of Liberia.

 

Department of State
Monrovia Dec. 21/86.

Mr Sevier

Sir:

I am in receipt of your communication of yesterday, in which you were kind enough to inform this Department of the arrival of the U.S.S. "Quinnebaug" Commander Coffin and of the intention of the Commander to call on his Excellency The President of Liberia at 2'oclock which I inferred meant 2. PM today. In reply I have the   honor to inform you the President will take great pleasure in receiving the visit of the Commander at the hour indicated in your communication.

I am Sir
Yours Respectfully
E. J. Barclay Secty of State.

Hon. S. S. Sevier
Acting Consul Gen. of U.S.
Monrovia
