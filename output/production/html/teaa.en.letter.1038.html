Department of State Received May 23 1887

No. 65.

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia April 19, 1887.

Mr. S. S. Sevier
To Asst. Secy of State.

Subject

Receipt of Despatch No. 18. with inclosure.

Synopsis

Despatch No. 18 received– acknowledgement of despatch No. 31. by Hon. T. F. Bayard Secretary of State Nov. 30, 1886

 

No. 65.

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia April 19, 1887

Hon. Jas D. Porter
Ass't. Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your despatch No. 18. of March 4, 1887 with inclosure of a circular from the Secretary of State of which the Fifth Auditor of the Treasury refers to the inconvenience of accounting for losses by exchange. In regard to my No. 31 of Oct. 13, 1886 of which you   you state was not received. I have the honor to say it was received and acknowledged by the Secretary of State November 30, 1886.

I have the honor to be
Sir,
Your obedient servant
Saml. S. Sevier
U.S. Vice Consul Genl.

Hon. J. D. Porter
Asst Sec'y of State
Washington D.C.
