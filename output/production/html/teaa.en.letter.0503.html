Washington D.C.

Sir

I have to inform the department that as Mr Henry F. Downing failed record his official correspondence in the despatch book I am unable to number my dispatches and I consequently leave them un-numbered until the numbering is correctly adjusted by the department.

Since the receipt of the Consular regulations from the department, I have learned that the Consulate is, with the exception of the archives (an inventory of which I forward here with) wholly without the proper legal belongings of the office

As I informed the department in my communication of the 15th ultimo all belongings of this Consulate were seized by Mr Henry F. Downings creditors, between the 15th and 30th of last March, while he was in transit to America, and before I assumed the duties of the office.

After presenting my credentials to the   the Governor General of the province I requests his interference in relation to the delivery of the Consular effects into my hands, and his advice to me was to the effect that I should pay the indebtedness and take charge, as he had no grounds for interference considering the fact that Mr Downing had led his creditors to believe that the United States were responsible for his debts and doings out here.

Having no other alternative I went so far as to redeem the archives, as the whole affair was being very disgracefully talked about and knowing also that the state archives were not for the hands and eyes for the outside world. For the redemption of the archives I paid one hundred and forty seven dollars and sixty eight cents $147.68.

I have here at present one discharged seaman in the hospital and one deserter also besides another deserter whom I will send to the Cape Verde Islands by this mail.

I have also ordered for the Consulate four (4) chairs, one ordinary table, one large receptacle for archives to protect them from insects which are very destructive here until the department furnishes something better. I have also ordered a flag staff   flag staff to be erected, the cost of which is 116 mil reis or $125.28. I have an old flag of my own which I will use until the department furnishes me with another more appropriate.

The department will please also furnish me with stationary and office furniture, as it cannot be purchased here, as cheaply as in the United States, transportation and all considered.

I desire to have a boat also, if the department will be good enough to supply one, as it costs over $1.50 for the hire of a boat every time I visit a ship.

I drew on the treasury in last month for $50 a draft in favor of John Hass but not having any regulations I find it was improperly made out but from the nature of the circumstances I cannot but hope that it was honored never-the-less.

I have the honor
to be Sir
Your obedient Servant
Julius L Judson
Vice Consul
