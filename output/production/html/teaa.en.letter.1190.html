Department of State Received Aug 25 1892
No. 12. Consulate-General, U. S. A.,
Monrovia, Liberia, July 20, 1892 Mr. William D. McCoy,
To the Department of State. SUBJECT:

Rent of Building for Legation and Consular purposes.

ABSTRACT OF CONTENTS.

1. Notification that the building now occupied will soon be needed.

2. Other buildings suggested.

  No. 12. Consulate-General, U. S. A.,
Monrovia, Liberia, July 20, 1892 Honorable
Assistant Secretary of State,
WASHINGTON, D. C. Sir:

I have the honor of submitting to you the following:

The building now used for Legation and Consular purposes is in charge of the Administrator of J. K. Sharp's estate and he informed the Vice Consul as well as myself that sometime in the near future the widow will want the building for her own use.

In view of this fact I have in contemplation the renting of one of two buildings more centrally located, on higher ground, and much healthier than the one we now occupy. One is two story brick owned by the M. E. Missionary Board, and is the more desirable; the other is the residence of Ex-President Roberts, a two-story   a two story brick, and only two doors from the one owned by the Missionary Board. Am satisfied that I can get Ex-President Roberts's house for the same rent we are now paying but do not yet know whether the other can be rented. If so, the rent ought to be about the same in either case.

Do you wish to advise me in regard to the matter or shall I go ahead and make the best arrangements possible?

I send a copy of this Despatch to the Diplomatic Bureau also.

Your obedient servant,

William D. McCoy,
Minister-Resident and Consul-General
