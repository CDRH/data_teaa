Kenosha, Wis., June 9th, 1862.

Hon. W. H. Seward,
Sec. of State,

Sir;

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of my Passport to Monrovia, Liberia.

And also to inform you that it is my purpose to commence my journey to my post of duty, tomorrow, (the 10th inst.) by way of New York.

If I fail to find a vessel bound directly for Monrovia, it will be my aim to sail for England, by the steamer from New York, on the 18th inst.: and by steamer from England, on the 20th prox; unless I have the honor of receiving advices from you, which would conflict with this arrangement.

 

Communications or Despatches for me will find me, until my departure, by being addressed to me, in care of
Oscar Irwin, Esq.,
U.S. Despatch Agent,
34½, Pine St.,
New York.

Permit me to express my unbounded joy at the consummation of an act of justice long delayed, in the recognition of Liberia and Hayti, as independent Powers.

I go to Africa under a strong conviction that, through the blessing of Almighty God, I shall be permitted and enabled to advance, and secure the honor and interests of our government, and of humanity at large.

I have the honor to remain,
Dear Sir,
Your very Obedient Servant.
Abm Hanson.
