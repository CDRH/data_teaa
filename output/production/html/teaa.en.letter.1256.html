Subject.
Advancements in local Consular Grades.

 

No. 17. Consular Series.
Legation of the United States
Monrovia Liberia,
Jan. 20, 1899.

Hon. David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to transmit to you the intelligence, that the Belgium Government, during the year 1898, advanced its Vice-Consulate, here, from that of a Vice-Consulate to that of a Consulate, and appointed W. E. Dennis Esq. of Liberia Consul.

The Imperial German Government, which recalled her Vice-Consul, here, early in 1898, who had became gratia non persona, from efforts made by him, in the claims of his   his Government for indemnity, in December 1898; has recently advanced her Vice-Consulship to a Consulship, and has appointed Augustus Humplemayer Esq., a millionier; Imperial Consul to Liberia.

The Ecuadorian Government has recently appointed Hon. Thos., W. Howard, Treasurer of Liberia, Consul-General for Ecuador to Liberia.

I have the honor to be your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith
M. R. and C. General.

Inclosure.
Letter of advice, as to drafts, Nos. 22, Jan. 19, 1899 and 23 Jan. 20, 1899.
