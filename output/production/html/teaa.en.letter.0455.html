Honorable Wm F. Wharton
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

There are certain citizens of the United States now on the Congo River. Missionaries, belonging to a "Faith Cure Sect," Who, I fear, may become destitutes, Without means to return home.

As will be seen by my despatch No 11, these people are now in anything but a prosperous condition. And as the members of this same mission in /85 or /86, were thrown upon the charity of the English Baptists, and by that society provided with passages to Europe, I, anticipating their coming to me for relief, respectfully ask instructions from the Department as to what action I am authorized to take in the matter. I am Sir:
Your Obedient Servant
Emory H. Taunt
U.S. Com. Agent Boma
