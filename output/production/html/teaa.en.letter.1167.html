Department of State Received Sep 26 1891

19

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, August 14, 1891

Mr Beverly Y. Payne,
To the Department of State.

Subject:

Wreck of the American Bark "Sea Gull" of Boston.

 

19

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, August 14, 1891.

Hon., William F. Wharton,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to announce to the Department, the wreck of the American Bark "Sea Gull" at the port of Addah on the Gold Coast, as per information from the Consular Agent at Elmina and enclosure.

I have the honor to be,
Sir:
Your obedient Servant,
Beverly Y. Payne,
U.S. Vice Consul General.

Enclosure.
1. Copy Consul Eminsang's No 5.

 
Department of State Received Sep 26 1891

Consulate of the United State at Elmina,
July 23rd 1891.

Alexander Clark Esq.,
United States Minister Resident and Consul General
Monrovia, Liberia.

Sir:

I have the honor to report that the American Bark "Sea Gull," of Boston, O. W. Junkins, Master, has wrecked at the port of Addah on the Gold Coast few days ago.

I am expecting the crew by the next S.S. "Congo." I shall try my best to send them on.

I have the honor to be,
Sir:
Your obedient servant,
G. E. Eminsang
U.S. Cons Agent.

 

Enclosure No. 1.
With Despatch " 19.

U.S. Consulate General
Monrovia, Liberia.
