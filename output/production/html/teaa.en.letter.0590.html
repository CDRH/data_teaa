Honorable Wm. F. Wharton

Washington, D.C.

Sir:

Six weeks ago a partner of Newton, Carnegie & Co. showed me in a clipping from an English paper a Washington telegram purporting that Congress had for the current fiscal year reduced the income of this Consulate, as that of other minor ones in Africa, to the fees.

At the same time I was advised not to be such a fool as to stay in Loanda spending money for a government that ceased to support me.

There being a probability of retrenchment in the budget of this Congress, I somewhat expected that my salary would be stopped after the June quarter, and realizing the impossibility of my continuing long to be Consul without any support; was awaiting the official announcement of the reduction, in order to tender my resignation, and shut up this Consular officer, for without remuneration as English-speaking person would take it, as, however, the official advice of the curtailment has not yet come, nor do American papers confirm that news, I have begun to doubt its correctness.

Still, as this question has set me a-thinking, I have come to the conclusion that, whether the salary be continued or not, its would only be just, in presence of the peculiar circumstances of this port, of the Department would assume the expense of office-rent, not to exceed 7 dollars per month. This is the request I now respectfully submit to your decision.

Considering further the fact, that I am not engaged in business and give all my time to the collection and distribution of non-paying, yet highly useful, information on this vast Consular district, an appropriate increase of salary, which would per se settle the office question, would appear yet more just, and ensure the better standing of this post.

I am, Sir, your obedient servant
Heli Chatelain
U.S. Coml Agent
