Mr Alexander Clark

Advice of Drafts

Sir:

I have the honor to advise that I have drawn Draft No. 2. for contingent expenses $228 37/100 Dollars on the Secretary of State in favor of R. A. Sherman; also Draft No 3. on account of salary $478 Dollars on the Secretary of State in favor of myself endorsed to George W. Appleton[?] All of said Drafts for the Quarters ending Sept. 30/90 and December 31 1890[?] per accompanying accounts. I have further to request that you suply the legation with some blank Drafts as there is not one left. and I was compeled to use. Dole of exchange for my Sallary draft, &c. also there is no abstract blanks left except 2. and sallery. blanks and[?] fees[?]. I am satisfied the department can furnish them by mail cheaper than they can be printed because they would have to be printed in Liverpool or London as there are no printers established here &c & & I have the honor to be your obedient servant, Alexander Clark Minister Resident CG
