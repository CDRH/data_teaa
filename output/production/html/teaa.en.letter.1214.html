Subject:

Voucher for Window Curtains.

 

No 35.

Consulate General of the United States,
Monrovia, Liberia, June 6, 1894.

Honorable Edwin F. Uhl,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor most respectfully to transmit herewith the voucher for window curtains charged in my account for Contingent expenses, September quarter 1893. The cause of not having transmitted it before, has been explained in my despatch of February 6, last.

I have the honor to be
Sir:   Sir:
Your obedient servant,
Beverly Y. Payne,
U.S. Vice Consul.

Inclosure
1. Receipt from Celeste H. McCoy (widow of W. D. McCoy late U.S. Consul General) dated Indianapolis, Ind. April 16, 1894.
