Translation of Despatch received from the office of the Secretary General of the Province of Angola on the 29th September 1876.

Illustrious Sir,

His Excellency the Governor General has requested me to inform you, that in accordance with the tenor of the Order No. 404 of the 21st instant, and published in the Official Gazette No. 39, at six months from that date foreign vessels cannot be employed in the Coasting Trade between any of the Ports of this Province whether they be occupied by the Portuguese Authorities or are comprised within the territory over which which the Crown of Portugal has reserved its rights.

God guard you.

Office of the Secretary General in Loanda, 28th September 1876.

Signed Custodio Jose Duarte
P.[?] G.

Illustrious Senhor American Consul

Translation of Order No. 404 dated, 21st September 1876, issued by the Colonial Government of the Province of Angola, and published in the Official Gazette No. 39. dated 23rd September 1876.

It being acknowledged that the Coasting Trade, between the Ports on the coast of any Country, belong without exception to the National flag, and it being equally a fact that the Crown of Portugal in the treaties concluded with the various Foreign States, has not, as yet, relinquished this restrictive right.

Considering that, the navigation between the Ports of the vast territory of this Province whether it be occupied or, notwithstanding the indisputable right of the sovereignty of the nation, not occupied, ought in every way to be considered as Coasting (Cabotagem), and as such must be exclusively carried on under the Portuguese flag:

Considering that at present, part of the commerce between these ports is being carried on by small vessels of different nationalities, through an abuse of toleration, which without rights being given them, or ours being diminished, constitutes, nevertheless, a certain usurpation of rights legally guaranteed to the Crown.

As it is important that the rules which govern navigation should be be maintained in all their integrity in their recognised forms, as a means of avoiding confusion as to rights, and perhaps disputes.

With the unanimous vote of the Colonial Government in Council and, unless the Government of His Majesty, The King ordains the contrary, I have found it expedient to determine the following:

>Article 1st. The Coasting Trade between the Ports comprised within the territory of this Province, occupied or not, but which belong by right to the Crown, is reserved exclusively for the Portuguese Flag.

Article 2nd. Foreign Vessels which are now trading between the Ports of of this Province, can continue in this traffic, in order that they may liquidate invested interests, during the space of six months counting from this date, on the expiry of this period, this concession will cease.

Let the Authorities and others who are interested take note and obey.

Palace of the Government in
Loanda, 21st November 1876.

Signed
Caetano Alexandre de Almeida e Albuquerque.
Governor General.
