No. 30, Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia;
May 31, 1899.

Owen L. W. Smith,
To
Hon. David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Subject.

Change of Schedule of Steamers from Liverpool, for Liberia.

Synopsis.

Only one Steamer per month from Liverpool to Monrovia. Four per month from Hamburg to Monrovia.

 

No. 30, Consular Series.
Legation of the United States
Monrovia Liberia;
May 31, 1899.

Hon. David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to transmit to you the intelligence that the schedule of the English Steamers, from Liverpool to Monrovia, has been recently, so changed that we shall now have only one Steamer per month from Liverpool. At the same time we have a Steamer sailing weekly from Hamburg, calling at Monrovia. The German Steamers make, by far the fastest time. If it is possible that you can so arrange with the   the Post Office Department at New York, so as to forward mail matters for Liberia, by way of Hamburg, you will greatly facilitate, as well as, accelerate its transit.

I have the honor to be your obedient servant,
Owen L. W. Smith,
M. R. and C. General.

Inclosure.
Dispatch No. 31. May 31, 1899.

Subject.

The arrival, suffering and condition of these families of the late Oklahoma emigration, stranded in Jersey City.

Synopsis.

Anderson White, William House and Joe Brown and families, late of the King Fisher County, Oklahoma, arrived April 29, 1899; three dead

 

No. 31, Consular Series.
Legation of the United States,
Monrovia Liberia;
May 31, 1899.

Hon. David J. Hill,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to transmit to you the intelligence, that three families; viz, Anderson White, and wife; Joe Brown and wife; and William House, wife and two children and his wife's stepfather, Mann Hart consisting of nine persons of the Hawes Emigration; late of King Fisher County Oklahoma, that was stranded at Jersey City, recently; reached Monrovia April 29, 1899. Since their arrival the   the entire nine persons, 7 grown and 2 children have been critically ill with the African fever. On May 16, one of the number; a four year old child of House's family died. At the time of the death of the child, both the father and the mother of the child were helplessly sick in the same room. On May the 24th the mother of the child, House's wife died. On May 25, 1899, Mann Hart, stepfather to House's wife died! House and small son in same room are improving. Joe Brown and wife; Anderson White and wife, are improving. White, though very sick has been able to keep his feet. I have the honor to be your obedient servant;
Owen L. W. Smith,
M. R. and C. G.
