Department of State,
Washington, Jany 22d, 1862

George G. L. Wright Esqr.
Appointed United States Consul Commercial Agent at the Gaboon

Sir:

The President having appointed you Consul Commercial Agent of the United States at the Gaboon

I transmit you a printed copy of the Consular Regulations, to which your immediate and careful attention is invited; a form of the Consular bond, to be executed at once and sent to this Department, and other documents for the use of your Consulate, of which a list is subjoined.

As soon as your bond is received and approved your Passport will be transmitted to you, and your Commission will be sent to the Legation of the United States at       with instructions to apply to the       Government for the usual Exequatur, which, when obtained, will be forwarded to you at       with the Commission Certificate of appointment.

You will communicate to the Department the name of the State or Country in which you were born, also of the State from which you have been appointed, and if you have ever resided in Gaboon. or any of its dependencies..

I am, sir, respectfully,
Your obedient servant,
F. W. Seward
Assistant Secretary
