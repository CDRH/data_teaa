Department of State Received Oct 28 1887

Franklinton
Franklin Co. N.C.
Oct 26/87.

Hon. A. A. Adee
Second Asst
Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

Your favor of the 22nd inst came duly to hand and was glad to hear from you. Please permit me to intrude upon your time by asking you for questions in   regard to my account with the State Department as U.S. Vice Consul Genl when I was in Liberia. In the month of July I was at the Department of State and I was told that many of the items which include my report were disallowed. Such items as for messenger at the Legation and many others. Please allow me to state that I cannot get   vouchers for such items because I am now living in this country and no hopes as far as I know of going back. I have written to the Secretary of State all about the matter of which I hear speaks.

My fourth quarter 1886 in which I paid messenger $15. was disallowed. My first quarter 1887 in which I paid my messenger was also disallowed. Sir permit me to say   I paid the messenger every quarter during my service as U.S. Vice Consul $15.00; but could not get a voucher; because the boy was a native boy who could not write; and further I did not [unclear, one word] fully that I had to send voucher for every little sum. I hope the Department will consider these items for messenger charged against me which I have paid. Further more I hope the Department of State   consider that I took the Legation in charge render many many disadvantage not being acquainted with the work and its rules.

I am willing that the Department of State disallow the funeral expenses of Moses. A. Hopkins; but for the rest which I think is due me should be paid. I paid $44.89 to Mrs. E J Harmon of Cape Palmas as I was told to do. Please look at one of your   Consular Series and you will find where I was told so to do by the Asst Secretary of State, and that has been charged against me. There are many items charged against me which I have paid in order to keep up the credit of the U.S. Legation.

The reason G. Moore has many of my drafts is because when I had small returns to pay and had not the money to do it   with their firm always would advance me the money until I would afford to draw a large draft. I could not draw a draft for every little item; so I got them to pay off what I owed and and at the end of each quarter I paid them in draft that is the reason you see such a large draft or amount disallowed. I have written to Secretary of State about these things or facts as named   but I have not heard from him. I want matters all fix up; therefore I again write. Please try and explain matters to the Department of State for me. I do not wish to cheat the Government out one cent more than what is due me. I inclose a letter from C. H. J. Taylor U.S. Minister at Monrovia which I cannot understand toward the last where he speaks of my character through life would be darkened.   Am I indebted to the U.S. Government or is the Government indebted to me? Why should he speak in that way? I dont understand him. I did my best in serving the U.S. Government and if I am short I am willing to forego what little the government is indebted to me. I know the Department of State will do what is best. Out side of the funeral expenses of the late Minister Moses A   Hopkins I hope the Department will disallow nothing. Indeed I had trouble in Africa in doing what I did do for the government, after Hopkins death. Please Sir, just think of my situation at that time when not being acquainted with such a position. Hoping to hear from you soon

I remain
Dear Sir
Your obedient servant
Samuel S. Sevier

Franklinton
N.C.

 

Monrovia Liberia
September 14th 1887

Rev. S. S. Sevier;

Sir:

I have to inform you in obedience to instruction No 4, Consular series, received from Washington dated June 7th 1887, in answer to your No 68 of April 19th, that the Department refuses to interfere in any agreement between you and Mr Hopkins, during his life time for services rendered by you to him. Using the language of the Department I have to say "Mr Hopkins was paid in full for the term of his service and Mr Sevier must look to the estate of Mr Hopkins for payment of his claim if he means to press it."

Letters received here, on the 13th instant, informs you, that your draft of April 26th 1887, for $1700.00[?] drawn against salary account is disallowed on account of the insufficiency of the appropriation to pay non residents. This information is received from the office of the comptroller of the currency Washington D.C. and carries date June 23rd 1887.
