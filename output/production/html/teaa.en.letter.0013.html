United States Commercial Agency
St Paul 'd Loanda May 5, 1859

Sir.

On the 26th January last, I had the honor of addressing you, enclosing the Returns of the Commercial Agency for the Quarter ending Dec 31st 1858 & at same time drawing for $244.07 being balance of my Salary for that Quarter.

In that Despatch I gave some particulars respecting this Coast, the arrival of Sir Henry V Huntley, HBM Consul for this Port, Slave Trade &c. which I trust has been received.

Accompaning this I enclose the Returns from this Commercial Agency for the Quarter ending March 31st 59 & have drawn on your Honor for $238.89 being balance of my Salary for that Quarter.

In my last I gave an estimate of the American Trade at this Port, which I trust will serve the same purpose as if I entered the amount of the Inward & Outward Cargoes in the Returns, which estimate can be now placed at One hundred thousand dollars per annum.

It is reported now that the U.S.S. "Marion" has taken as Prizes in the Congo River a Bark   called the "Orion" of New York & another Bark name not reported. HBM Steamer "Triton" Comr Burton had the "Orion" in tow, one or two days previous to the arrivals there of the "Marion", while at the same time the "Orion" had the American Ensign flying. Capt Brent of the "Marion" will undoubtedly inform his Department of the particulars in this case.

It is also reported that the U.S. Government have made a proposition to England, that they will place on this Coast Ten Steamers of two Guns each, instead of the Eighty Guns in large vessels. If so, it will do much in stopping the Slave Trade on this Coast.

As a Depot for Coals or Provisions, Loanda has not its equal on the Coast of Africa. For further particulars respecting the advantages of this place, I respectfully refer you to my former despatches.

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of a despatch from your Department under date of Dec. 13th 1858 advising me of the resignation of Mr Miller & requesting me to continue in the discharge of the duties of the office, till the further pleasure of the President is ascertained, which request I respectfully comply with.

I have the honor to be
Your Most Obt. Servt
John G. Willis.

Hon Lewis Cass
Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

 

May 6.

A small topsail Schooner name not yet given, was towed in here yesterday by HBM Steamer "Triton", which vessel was taken off Mazula about 40 Miles to the North of Loanda, without flag or papers. She was 3 months from Havana & had Six hundred doubloons on board. I have not seen any person belonging to her; but get my information from HBM acting Commissioner. The "Triton" after coaling here, will leave with her Prize for St. Helena.
