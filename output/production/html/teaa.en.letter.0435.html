Colonial Secretary's Office
Sierra Leone 31st Jany 1874.

Sir

In reply to your letter of the 22nd instant, relative to the detention by Mr. Hird of Consular archives for rent. I am directed by His Excellency the Governor-in-Chief to inform you that the proper course would be for you to apply to the legal tribunal of this settlement, for the purpose of determining the question whether or not Mesrs Harris & Co's agent was justified in detaining the consular property referred to, for their rent, as this is a legal question, which the Judge, and not His Excellency is to decide.

His Excellency therefore cannot interfere with Messrs Harris &Co's agent under the circumstances disclosed in his assertion of a legal right.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your most obdt Servant
(sigd) John Ashwood
Acting Colonial Secy.

N. Lyons Esq.

Oxford Street, Freetown,
4th Feby. 1874

Sir

I am instructed by Nathaniel Lyons Esq. Acting U.S. Consul, and Acting Vice Consul for Spain &c &c to ask that you will be good enough to deliver to him certain flags, archives, printed books, crate-of-arms, furniture, blank forms &c supplied by and belonging to the Government of the United States of America, Spain, Portugal, Italy, Greece, Austria and the Netherlands, as well as the personal effects of the late Charles Theodore Julius[?] (a deceased American Citizen) the whole of which one now in your possession.

I am further instructed to ask from you for an account for rent, by which you alleged a right to detain the goods and chattels above specified accrued to by you

I am
Sir
Yours truly
(sgd) Francis Smith

Reuben Hird Esq:

Freetown Sierra Leone
6th Feby. 1874

Dear Sir

In reply to your letter of 4th instant making certain claims on behalf of a person whom you style "Nathaniel Lyons Esq. Acting U.S. Consul, and acting Consul for Spain &c &c."

I beg to inform you that I do not recognize such person as having a claim to the "Flags, Archives, Printed Books", Crate-of-arms," Furniture, Blank forms, supplied by and belonging to the Government of the United States of America, Spain, Italy, Greece, and the Netherlands, as well as the personal effects of the late Charles Theodore Julius, a deceased American citizen alleged by you to be in your [?] possession.

But at the same time I am willing to deliver these things up to any person applying for them in the name of the respective Governments on payment of our claim, for which an account is in accordant with your request herewith enclosed.

I am
Yours truly
(sgd) Reuben Hird.

Francis Smith Esq.

OFFICIAL GAZETTE.

His Excellency the Governor-in-Chief has been pleased to recognize J. N. Lyons, Esquire, provisionally as Acting Consul for the United States, and Acting Vice Consul for Spain during the absence on leave of William Hogan, Esquire, proceeding to England.

J. J. Kendall,
