Department of State Received May 14 12 1888
No. 1 Consulate General, U. S. A.
Monrovia May 12th 1888.
(Written (at Washington) Mr. Smith
To the Department of State. SUBJECT:

Acceptance of office

ABSTRACT OF CONTENTS.

Accepting appointment.
Enclosing bond and oath.
Answering enquiry as to birth

  No. 1 Consulate General, U. S. A.
Monrovia May 12th 1888. Honorable Geo. L. Rives
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D. C. Sir:

I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of the Department's communication of May 1st (No. 1 Dip. Series) apprising me of my appointment as Minister Resident and Consul General of the United States to Liberia, and enclosing blank forms of official bond and oath to be executed.

I hereby accept the appointment with which the President has honored me; and I return herewith the bond and oath of office duly executed.

I was born in North Carolina and am appointed from that state I have never resided in Liberia. Awaiting   Awaiting your further instructions.

I have the honor to be Sir,
Your Obedient Servant,
Ezekiel E. Smith.
