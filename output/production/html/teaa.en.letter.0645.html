No 2     Gambia     1874

In the thirty seventh year of the reign of Her Majesty Queen Victoria

31st March 1874

An Ordinance to alter the Tonnage dues for the Port of Bathurst, and healthy certain exemptions from Port charges.

Whereas it is necessary to alter the Tonnage dues on vessels entering the River Gambia, and to exempt H. M. Transports and Mail Steamers from Port charges.

Be it therefore enacted by the Administrator and Legislative Council of the settlements on the River Gambia, as follows viz:

1st From and after the Promulgation of this Ordinance the duty of Two shillings per ton register measurement now payable once in the year on ships, Boats, or Vessels entering the River Gambia shall cease and   determine, and there shall be levied in lieu thereof on all such ships, Boats, or vessels, a duty of One shilling and sixpence per register measurement each time that any such Ships, Boat or vessel shall so enter the River Gambia

2nd It shall be lawful to admit H. M. Transports and Steam Vessels carrying Her Majesty's Mails under arrangement with the Postmaster General or with the Colonial Government of the settlement on the Gambia free of any or all Port charges.

C. H. Kortright
administrator

Passed in the legislative Council this Thirty first day of March, in the year of Our Lord One thousand, eight hundred and Seventy four.

Robert H. Lyrett
Acting Clerk of the Legislative Council
