Returns and accounts.

Synopsis:

The quarterly returns and accounts and semi annual return of passports issued, quarterly returns for the 4th quarter, 1879.

 

No. 21.

Consulate General of the U.S.,
Monrovia, La, June 30th, 1879

Sir:

I have the honor to respectfully transmit herewith my quarterly returns and account for the 4th quarter, 1879.

The same consit of the following inclosures: The digest of the Invoice Book; register of official dispatches and letters received at the legation and consulate general; register of official dispatches sent from legation and consulate general with amount of postage paid-named, and receipt Hon. F. W. Seward,
Ass't Sec'y of State,
Washington   receipt of Post Master; list of arrivals and departures of American vessels, return of deceased American citizens, and abstract of passports issued during the half year ending June 30, 1879; rent and miscellaneous expense account vouchers, a transcript of Invoice Book, an abstract of contingent expenses of legation; and inclose a receipt for £3-8-8. There should be an additional receipt for £2-3-10, making a total of £5-12-6; this was partially the result of a mistake in sending the Illustrated London News instead of the Daily Times- which I desired. You will observe the correction made, but an absence of receipt   receipt for £2-3-10, on account of my letter containing drafts in payment- to B. F. Stevens, London- not having reached England in time for an answer to be sent inclosing receipt for balance.

Salary account and accompanying certificate. In the account, I have returned the U.S. balance of $40.71 from account of Relief of Destitute American Seamen, according to my balance heretofore reported, and as appears from the adjustment of said account by Fst. Comptroller.

I am, Sir, With higest sentiments of esteem and consideration, Your obedient servant

Jno. H. Smyth,   Jno. H. Smyth,
U.S. Minister Resd't & Consul Gen'l

Note.–This return to be made eery six months to the Department of State. If the individual is a native citizen of the United States, the abstract should give his place of birth; if naturalized, the date of naturalization, the place where, the name of the court by which a certificate of naturalization was granted, should be stated.

When husband, wife, and minor children expect to travel together, a single passport for the whole will suffice. For any other person in the party a separate passport will be required.
