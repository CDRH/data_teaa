Department of State Received Sept 20 1870

Commercial Agency of the United States of America at Sierra Leone Aug 19th 1870

Sir

I have the honor to enclose a copy of a letter from His Excellency Sir Arthur Kennedy E.B. the Gover-in-chief, relative to a common[?] addressed to him by Mr Henry Rider previous to his departure for America

Mr Rider sailed on Sunday morning the 7th inst. for New York in a very feeble state of health.

I herewith transmit an Inventory of the Archives & furniture of the U.S. Commercial Agency at this Port.

I have the honor to be
Most respectfully
Your Obt. Servt.
JB Upton
U.S. Com Agent
Pro Tem

Honorable Hamilton Fish
Secretary of State

Colonial Office Sierra Leone
August 10th 1870

Sir

I have the honor to acknowledge receipt of your letter of the 6th inst. reporting the departure of Mr Rider for America, and stating that you have been authorized by him to act and perform the duties of Commercial Agent, for the United States of America

Having laid the same before His Excellency the Governor in Chief, I am directed to inform you that you will be acknowledged as Commercial Agent pending the approval H. M. Government

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your most Obdt. Servt.
(Sgd) J. J. Kendall
Colonial Secretary

J. B. Upton Esq.   Enclosure Commercial Agency
