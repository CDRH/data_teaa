Department of State Received Dec 15 1879

No 8. US Consulate Sierra Leone Nov. 12. 1879

Judson A. Lewis To Dept of State
Acknowledging receive of Circular No 16 regarding condition of Archives of Consulate &c.

 
No 8.

U.S. Consulate at Sierra Leone
Nov. 12th 1879.

Hon F. W. Seward
Ass't Secty of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have to acknowledge the receipt of Circular No 16 - Dept of State Washington dated August 27th 1879 - regarding the condition of the Consulate &c and in reply would say 1st The instructions and correspondence received are not bound and indexed for convenient reference.

2nd The outgoing dispatches and correspondence are fairly transcribed to date in their respective record books but not indexed.

3rd Press copies are not used for any part of the correspondence of this Consulate

4th There are no copies of translations   of any kind preserved in this Consulate.

5th There has been no detailed permanent record kept of the accounts of the Consulate

6th I have no suggestion to offer the Department concerning the care of the records and archives excepting that I believe they should be carefully preserved and kept in convenient form for reference.

Upon taking charge of this Consulate Sept. 16th 1879. I wrote the Dept under date of Sept 19th Dispatch No 2 - that I found the archives in a very bad and disorganized condition and stated there was no Case in which to keep the Record Books &c. and asked for instructions if I would be allowed anything by the Department for necessary furniture (cases &c) for the proper preservation of the Records &c. I am now at work as fast as I can find   time - separate from my merchantile duties in putting the condition of the Consular papers &c in proper shape

I am Sir
Your Obt Servt
Judson A. Lewis
U.S. Consul
