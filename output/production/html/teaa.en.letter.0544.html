No. 12

Consulate of the United States,
Loanda, February 26, 1890

Honorable
Assistant Secretary of State
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

In reply to the circular from the Department, dated November 25, I have the honor to report:

1st. There are no refrigerators in this Consular district.

2nd. Being unacquainted with the construction of them or their operation under different conditions, I cannot say if any peculiar feature would be requisite for this district, unless it might be that the machine ought to be an ice-making one and refrigerator combined.

3rd Answered in first paragraph.

4th " " " "

5th There are a few, not more than   three or four small French ice-machines in use here, and not one of them is capable of turning out more than may be, two or three kilos of ice per day. They are seldom put to such use however, the cooling of water being the usual content of their services.

I have no idea what the charge would be per 100 lb of ice if that quantity could be produced here for sale. I can however say that for about a quart of lemonade made of lime-juice, sugar and this machine cooled water a charge of thirty cents is made. Value of lime juice and sugar 5 cents.

A machine turning out 100 to 200 kilos of block-ice per day, (refrigerator combined) would meet the present wants of this city.

The principal hotel proprietor of the place. Mr F. Frugier, is anxious to purchase such a machine, but not at prompt payment, as his capital is very, limited   limited. He suggests payment of a certain proportion on delivery and rest by instalments.

I would suggest that catalogues with designs be forwarded to me, stating price, cost of working to produce 100 to 200 kilos of ice, and in fact such necessary particulars as will enable me to lay the matter clearly before people who might become purchasers.

There are no means here of preserving food and liquids and if the cost does not bar the way[?], the introduction of refrigerators would be a boon to many, as meat turns bad here in twenty four hours.

I am, Sir,
Your obedient servant
Ed Bannister.
Acting Vice Consul.
