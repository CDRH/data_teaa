Mr. President

As the first representatives of the Island of Madagascar who have visited this great Republic; we feel ourselves honored in this meeting at its capital with yourself its distinguished leader.

We have only been a very few days in this country, but we have already begun to be conscious of a warm desire on the part of the American people for our kingdom's welfare, and we hope that our visit will tend to deepen that kindly feeling in the hearts of all your citizens the foremost lovers of National independence.

Commerce between this your country and our own distant island has increased of late to such an extent as to have demanded the attention of both our Governments. This progress is in no small measure due to the efforts of your excellent representative there, Colonel Robinson, whom we have learned to respect as few other foreigners are respected. In the name of our Queen we desire to hear this testimony to this wisdom and ability.

Our business today as Ambassadors of Her Majesty the Queen of Madagascar is to present the credentials which we were hidden to lay before you. We have also been made the bearers by your Government Queen of a few articles of Malagasy workmanship which she desires you Â  you to accept, not as things of value in themselves but as tokens of friendly respect.

Lastly, Mr. President, we desire to thank you for a most hospitable welcome to America, and beg you now to consider us as being at the disposal of Your Government for the consideration of those affairs which are included in our commission.
