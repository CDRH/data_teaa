Department of State Received Mar 29 1881

No. 50. U.S. Consulate at Sierra Leone Feb 23rd

Judson A. Lewis - To Dept State

Regarding Official visit on board the German Corvette Victoria.

No. 50. U.S. Consulate at
Sierra Leone Feb. 23rd 1881

Hon John Hay
Ass't Secty State

Sir:

I have the honor to inform you that on Monday Feb. 21st the German Corvette "Victoria" Captain Victor Valois Commanding arrived in this Port. On the following day, I paid an official visit on board and was received with all the honors due my office.

The Victoria is on special detail and bound to Monrovia Liberia - for the purpose of conferring with the Liberian authorities regarding the loss of the German trading Steamer "Carlos" Oct. 25th 1880. and pillaging of the crew by the Natives at or near "Saspa Town" in Liberia.

You are no doubt already advised of this by the Minister Mr Smyth.

The Liberian Government claim that the Steamer had called or was about to call at a place not a Port of Entry for the purpose of shipping Kroomen (as laborers) contrary to the laws of the Republic.

I am informed the crew, while attempting to save their lives by escaping in boats were set upon and robbed of every thing even to their clothing, and left to roast in the sun, and to punish these people is the mission of the Victoria.

The Captain informs me that he goes with the kindliest feelings toward the Republic of Liberia
