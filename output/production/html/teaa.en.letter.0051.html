Rec'd March 19, '64.
No 3.

Commercial Agency of the U.S. of America
at St Paul De Loanda December 31st 1863

Sir

I have the Honor to enclose Custom House and Port Charges for the Port of St. Paul De Loanda.

Also Arrivals and Departures for the Quarter Ending December 31st 1863

Also Official Letters and Blanks Received

By this it will be perceived that the Box with Flags and Blanks, reached here on the 24th November about 9 months after it left Washington.

Mail Facilities between this Place and the U.S. are very slow and uncertain. By the English Mail it is seldom less than three; and often five months between the mailing of a letter in New York and its receipt here, in fact there is no regular mail, except as far as Fernando Po, where it lays until some English Cruiser chooses to bring it up

I received the box from St Helena via Cape of Good Hope, forwarded to me by Walter Graham Esqr.

The Draft and voucher for stationary I hope will prove satisfactory

I have also the Honor of enclosing a Tabular Statement of export Duties and Other Charges for this Port. Also Aggregate Amt of Fees

I have the Honor to state that Clearing a Vessel from this Port is a perplexing operation:

1st Notice of eight days must be given,

2d A Bond with two sureties (merchants) in the sum of Four Thousand Mil Reis Fracos $2.675.59 must be entered into as a Guarantee that the vessel will not Engage in the Slave Trade

 

This is well, when suspicion attaches to any vessel, but it is exacted indiscriminately previous to granting a Clearance, not that there is any fear that a well known vessel will engage in the Slave Trade, but that the merchants who sign the bond may realize the Fee, which is forty five pound or One hundred and twenty five Dollars for so doing;

The Practice is continued (I am told) in accordance with an inference drawn from a Law of 1836, there being no positive law to justify it;

The merchants established here sign the Bond for for each other, but an American Vessel arriving here for the first time, encounters much trouble and expense in consequence of said Bond.

She must also select a Consignee (if not previously Consigned) which obliges her to pay a further sum of One hundred Mil Ries a Mast[?] (Fracos) or $66,66 2/3.

I the case of the Whaling Schooner Glendower His Excellency Gov General (José Baptista Andrade) over-ruled the Custom House authorities in regard to both Bond and Consignee, thus saving her a useless expense of $338.00. She Came here for supplies only;

I have not succeeded in obtaining a copy of Duties on Imports for this province; the present Duties are based upon the Tariff Law of 1842, modified for Colonial Purposes, but I have the assurance that on the 1st of February next a New Tariff will be published. Based on the Tariff on 1863, with Colonial Modifications, which will I understand increase the Duties on both Imports and exports, I will forward it as soon as possible.

At Benguela, Mesamides and Loanda, Duties must be paid; All other Trading Station are free   I feel it to be my duty to mention the fact, that the Iron Lighters here belonging to the Government are nearly valueless, being rusted through in many Places, cargo that would sink (Coal for instance) cannot be transported in them safety; in fact they are not well adapted to this navigation, either in model or material, Lighters for this harbor should be of wood forty five to fifty feet long, and Coppered, the model such as are used at Havre de Grace M.d. very strong, Cheap, light draft of water, Burthensome, easily managed, and will sail well, when sail is put upon them; I have no doubt, such Lighters could be sent here all ports previously fitted, and put together here, for one thousand Dollars each. I am informed that freight alone on one of those Lighters now here amounted to twelve hundred Dolls and their term of service has been very short.

For two years last past, trade with the interior has much been obstructed, in consequence of A War between the Portuguese and the King (Jaga) of Cassange, one of the most powerful Potentates of the Interior of Africa, and whos dominions border upon the Portuguese possessions. On the 29th of August last, a treaty of Peace was entered into between the belligerents, the Jaga of Cassange agreeing to Pay the whole expense of the war. On the 26th of September the Embassador of the Jaga was received at the Palace of the Government General, when the Treaty was ratified; in consequence it is expected that trade with the Interior will revive and Importations increase

Our own unhappy civil war has a very depressing effect upon American Trade here, in consequence of the high price of Cotton, heretofore   the most profitable article of American manufacture imported into this Country, also an important auxiliary to all other article of sale or barter, under present circumstances the English (who have only one house here) sell Cotton goods for at least 30 per cent less than the same article can be bought in the U. States

With despatch No 5 I had the Honor to mention the fact of the loss of my Commission and Passport, I have not been able to recover them, and am under the necessity of renewing the request that others be sent to me, nor have I received the necessary Exequetur requested in the house of the same despatch.

I also had the honor to mention the fact, that the Salary of Agent at this station is inadequate to its propper support, one must live as do Representatives of other Countries, or lower his own Country in the estimation of the authorities, which I am not willing to do, luxury is not requisite, but respectability is.

I am aware that Congress must concur in whatever is done in the premises, but a recommendation from Your Honor to the propper authorities, together with the concurrence of the Representative from my Congressional District (the Hon E. H. Webster) I feel Confident would procure an increase of salary; in default, I would Respectfully solicit the appointment of Naval Store Keeper here, or the appointment to some situation where the salary is adequate to support the station without engaging in Commercial pursuits

I have the Honor to be
Your Most Obedient Servant
John T Bradberry Commercial Agent.

W. H. Seward
Sec of State
