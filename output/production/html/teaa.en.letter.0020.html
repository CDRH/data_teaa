Recd July 5
No 14.

United States Commercial Agency
Loanda April 29 1861

Sir,

Under date of April 5th I had the honor of addressing you under care of the US Minister at Lisbon.

I now enclose Returns from this Commercial Agency to the Quarter ending March 31, '61. The several accounts & certificates I have directed to the Treasury Department, & have drawn on the Honorable Secretary of the Treasury for $223.39¢ being balance of my Salary for that Quarter.

In my Letter of April 5th I informed you of the particulars respecting Mr E A Birnie, US Naval Shop Keeper, & have now to add that he still remains in Prison, without as yet, any evident signs of a quick despatch of his case by the Judicial author- ities To The
Honorable
Secretary of State, USA   ities of this place.

I having received some time since, Leave of Absence from the State Department, it is my intention to take passage for Salem Mass. in a few days, & have placed the Business relating to the Commercial Agency in charge of Mr. Robert Cunningham who has been recognised as Acting US Coml Agent by the Governor General of this Province.

On my arrival at Salem I will inform the Department, & if required will visit Washington in order to give further particulars respecting Slave Trade. Imprisonment of Mr Birnie & Removal of the US Depot for the African Squadron.

Very Respectfully
Your Obt Servt
John G. Willis
US Coml Agent
