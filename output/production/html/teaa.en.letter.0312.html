Consulate of the United States of America

Freetown, Sierra Leone
August 31st, 1866.

Sir,

I had the honor of addressing the Honorable the Secretary of State for Foreign affairs on the 26th ultimo and have since received your dispatch N: 1, dated the 24th of May 1866, bearing the appointment as Commercial Agent of the United States at this place of Mr. Henry Rider, as also a form of bond and oath of Office and allegiance to be executed and Honorable
F. W. Seward
Assistant Secretary
Washington D.C.   and forwarded by that gentleman to your Department.

The Consul's Manual, together with a list of the Diplomatic and Consular Offices of the United States, and such circular issued by the Department as are now in force, have not been received.

Mr. Henry Rider, as I had the honor of informing you, having left for England I shall deliver to him the abovementioned documents on his return.

In case a Bond should be required from me I am prepared to furnish sureties in the United States to the   the amount specified in the form of Bond, but not having the honor of being a citizen of the United States, I am unable to take the oath of allegiance, but I am prepared to swear faithfully to discharge the duties of this Office.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant
Rosenbush.
Actg: Comm: Agent
United States
