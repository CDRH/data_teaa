Subject.

Change of quarters.

 

No. 3 Consular Series,
Consulate General,
Monrovia, Liberia; Sept. 16, 1898.

Hon. John B. Moore,
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington.

Sir:

I have the honor to transmit to you the intelligence that on the 6th of Sept., 1898, I vacated the quarters rented by my predecessor, Hon. W. H. Heard, for the use of the Legation; and moved back to the building, formerly, rented of Mr. Sharp, and used as the Legation, prior to the administration of the Hon. Mr. Heard, on the 7th instant. The present quarters, not having been rented to any specially selected   class of persons, since its vacation by the Hon. Mr. Heard, was found to have been very badly used; the repairs of which are now going on, at the expense of the premises; while the Legation pays for cleaning building and premises.

The terms of the contract have not yet been reduced to writing, but do soon as possible, will furnish the Department with a copy.

I have the honor to be
your obedient servant,
Owen L. W. Smith.
