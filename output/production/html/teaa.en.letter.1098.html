Department of State Received Apr 16 1889
No. 18 Consulate General, U. S. A.
Monrovia, Liberia, March 11 1889 Mr. Ezekiel E. Smith
To the Department of State. SUBJECT:

Acknowledging receipt of despatch No 4

ABSTRACT OF CONTENTS.

Acknowledgment of the receipt of despatch No 4 of January 10th, 1889. Explanation of despatch No 1 of November 6th 1888.

  No. 18 Consulate General, U. S. A.
Monrovia, Liberia, March 11 1889 Honorable G. L. Rives
Assistant Secretary of State,
Washington, D. C. Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to acknowledge the receipt of your No 4 despatch under date of January 10th 89.

In explanation of my (incorrectly numbered 1) despatch of November 6th, transmitting a reply to the Department circular of September 20th last, concerning the rates of duty levied on American goods; I wish to state that, prior to the reception of the circular above mentioned, I had received from the Department no document or communication over the signature of Hon. A. A. Adee; and as the despatches from me hitherto, had been addressed only to the Secretary and Assistant Secretary, I was not quite sure that a reply to   to a document, from Hon. A. A. Adee, should be numbered among those addressed to the Assistant Secretary.

I am, sir,
Your obedient servant
E. E. Smith,
U.S. Consul Gen'l.

To Hon. G. L. Rives,
Assistant Secretary,
Washington, D.C.
