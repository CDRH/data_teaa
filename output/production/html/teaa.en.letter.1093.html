Washington, D. C. Sir:

I have the honor respectfully to transmit herewith, the returns and accounts of this office for the quarter ended Dec. 31st 1888, as follows: Digest of invoice book 1; arrivals and departures of American vessels 2; account for salary 3; account for destitute seamen, Albert Garbon and Robt Kuhn, who came here on the Bark Liberia of N.Y. Oct. 20, 4; contingent account with vouchers 5; official letters sent 6; official letters received 7; Statement of contingent expenses 8.

I hope the same may be found correct. I am, sir,
Your obedient serv't.
Ezek. E. Smith
U.S. Minister & C. G.
