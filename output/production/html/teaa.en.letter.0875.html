Department of State Received Mar 29 1881

No. 48. U.S. Consulate at Sierra Leone Feb 21st 1881.

Judson A. Lewis - To Dept State

General Observations regarding Commercial Relations.

 

No. 48. U.S. Consulate at
Sierra Leone Feb. 21st 1881.

Hon John Hay
Asst Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to address you at this time regarding certain matters bearing more or less upon trade in this Colony and its dependencies.

I have received at this Consulate numbers 1. and 2. October and November. "Commercial Relations". and am glad to know that arrangements have been made by the State Department with Consular Officers for more frequent Reports and provission by Congress for the publishing and distribution of the Same - beleiving that the result will have a tendency to greatly extend the the commerce of the United States. I shall endeavor to keep the Department advised from time to time regarding all matters of interest at this place.

We are now in the midst of the dry or busy season, but the universal testimony of Merchants and Traders in Sierra Leone and all the surrounding Rivers is that trade is very dull, unusually[?] so for this season of the year - trade does not "come down" from interiorward. The roads are blocked up and one tribe or clan are not permitted to pass through the domains of any other. Now this never ceasing cry of war (I think usually bloodless) is a great damage to trade and a source of annoyance to all those who have produce to bring down.

 

There are no manufactures of any importance in Sierra Leone - and Agricultural industry is sadly neglected - given entirely to trade - so when the untutored native from the interior is prevented from coming to town, trade to a great extent ceases and the town proper is as dull as a New England village on Sunday - straightway our occupation is gone. The only remedy for this will be to go to work and cultivate the land.

The Colonial Government of Sierra Leone has ceeded a large tract of land (2000 acres) to Hon William Grant (native) who is now making extensive experiments [?] - in growing Sugar Cane, Cocoa, Cola Nuts Ginger &c - the sugar cane grows well and the juice the nature of contents, or the number the packages and have said numbers appear on the Invoice - for when a thousand different packages are sent from half as many merchants aboard a vessel for a foreign port, its no joke to be obliged to open all the boxes before you can find a certain article for which you may be in search, it would also greatly facilitate the passing of goods through the various Custom Houses abroad.

I remain Sir
Most Respectfully Your Obt Servt
Judson A. Lewis
U.S. Consul
