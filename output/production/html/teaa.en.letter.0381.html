No 7.
Received Department of State Aug 25 1869

Commercial Agency of the United States of America at Sierra Leone 26th July 1869.

Sir,

I have the honor to enclose a receipt of Captain John Havlin for two parcels shipped by me on board the American Brig "Ann Elizabeth" to the care of the Collector of Customs at Boston.

One contains Books etc: formerly belonging to a deceased seaman, Henry Clarke, of the American Barque "Gem" lost off Bulama; the other contains despatches No 3. and No 5. with sundry enclosures. H. B. Davis Esqre
Assistant Secretary
Washington, D.C.   enclosures.

I have adopted this mode of transmission for communications of minor importance in order to save heavy postage.

I have the honor to be,
Sir,
Your obedient Servant,
Rosenbush.
U.S. Vice Comml Agt.

 

Port of Freetown, Sierra Leone.

American Brig "Ann Elizabeth"
26th July 1869

I acknowledge the receipt of two parcels made up and sealed with the seal of the Commercial Agency at this port directed to the Department of State at Washington under the care of the Collector of Customs at Boston Mass: which I promise to deliver on the entry of the Brig "Ann Elizabeth" under my command at the said port of Boston.

John Havlin
Master.

U.S. Commercial Agency at Sierra Leone.
