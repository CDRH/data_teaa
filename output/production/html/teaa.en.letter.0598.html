Department of State Received Nov 30 1878

Sierra Leone
5th October 1878

Sir,

I have the honor to own receipt of Your Excellency's despatch of the     September informing me that the President of the Republic has been pleased to appoint Mr John A Parm MD to be United States Consul for Sierra Leone and its dependencies in lieu of W. H. Randall Esqre and enclosing a printed circular addressed to John Broadhurst Esq:, who has recently been in charge of the Consulate, containing the same information.

The latter has been handed to Mr Broadhurst, who immediately delivered over to Dr Parm the Archives and other appurtenances of the Consulate, and also apprised His Excellency the Governor in Chief of the contents of Your Excellency's communication. His Excellency was The Hon F. W. Seward
Assistant Secretary of State   was pleased to reply that having received no official intimation of Dr Parm's appointment, he must refuse to acknowledge him in the capacity of United States Consul.

Your Excellency is pleased to explain that Dr Parm's appointment has been made on the strong recommendations, no doubt bona-fide ones, of numerous and influential citizens of the United States. But Your Excellency must allow me to state that Dr Parm is a person whom I should deem it derogatory to my own dignity to work with or under, however strongly, and by however numerous and influential citizens recommended.

I therefore, however reluctantly, hereby resign my appointment as Vice Consul for the United States of America for Sierra Leone from the 1st inst, being the date of my receipt of Your Excellency's despatch informing me of Dr. Parm's appointment.

I have the honor to be
Sir
Your Excellency's obedt servant
James Frame
late U.S. Consul
