Commercial Agency of the United States
at Gaboon West Africa Oct 1864

Hon William H. Seward
Secretary of State Washington D.C.

Sir

In my communication of the 7th of June I requested their permission might be granted me to visit America for the purpose of recruiting my health. The state of my health is such as to render a change of climate necessary. I shall therefore take passage upon the American Barque Greyhound, which leaves this port on the inst bound direct for New York; leaving the affairs of this Commercial Agency in charge of Rev. J M Preston, requesting you will confirm his appointment as Vice Commercial Agent at this place. Mr. Preston is a citizen of the United States, was born in Massachusetts, and has been for the past sixteen years in connection with the American Board C.F.M. at this place being well known here on in America as a man of unblemished character. Enclosed will be found his oath of allegiance. Submitting the above for your consideration. I have the honor to be your obedient servant Henry May
U.S. Commercial Agent.
