Department of State Received Aug 1 1887

No. 3.

U.S. Consulate
Monrovia June 21st, 1887

Chas. H. J. Taylor to Hon Jas. D. Porter

Subject:

S. S. Seviers Contingent account, and fees rec'd official.

 

No 3. Consular Series
Monrovia June 21st, 1887

Hon Jas. D. Porter
Ass't Secretary of State
Washington D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor to inform you, that it will be necessary for the Government of the United States to retain of S. S. Seviers salary, at least $200.00 to make up for the "deficit" in his contingent and fee account, as consular officer for the first two months of this the second quarter. I have no hesitancy in informing you that he did a great many unauthorized and unwarranted "things" which will be reported at the end of this   quarter. Such as paying $20.00 for wine on acc't of some parade on the part of urchins in the Republic which account has been taken out of the contingent allowance for this quarter. Also $50.00 for which there is no voucher present etc.

Desiring to serve the Department in an upright manner, I have the honor to be, sir,
Your most obedient servant,
Chas H. J. Taylor

P.S. He is now on his way to America I presume he will call at the State Department. I told him to do so.
